Lost: Ashes of Vengeance

World Map

The World

Humanity occupies only a small fraction of the world.

Most civilization lies within the fertile western triangle of the Great Continent, where forests, rivers, mountains, and lakes have allowed kingdoms, cities, and villages to flourish for centuries. Beyond this settled frontier stretches an immense wilderness known simply as the Unknown—a realm of unexplored lands inhabited by powerful Wildborn and the remnants of forgotten civilizations.

Across the Sea of Glass to the northwest lies the Sea of Glass Archipelago, home to the island Kingdom of Faylora, while far to the north stretches the Endless White, an immeasurable frozen wilderness where the Kingdom of Norvalen endures amid eternal snow and ice.

Separating civilization from the Unknown is the Verdant Ring—a continuous chain of fortified cities, military outposts, and defensive walls stretching from the Frozen Sea in the north to the southern deserts. For centuries, it has served as humanity's final frontier, protecting the known world from the countless dangers beyond.

Everything east of the Verdant Ring, along with most of the Endless White, remains unexplored. Expeditions rarely return, and those who do speak of colossal Wildborn, forgotten kingdoms, ancient ruins, and landscapes unlike anything found within civilized lands.

"To most people, the world ends at the Verdant Ring. Beyond it lies only the Unknown."

Major Regions

1. The Great Continent

The Great Continent is the largest landmass known to humanity and home to the majority of civilization. Its fertile western triangle is blessed with rivers, forests, mountain ranges, and rich plains that have allowed kingdoms, cities, and villages to flourish for centuries.

Beyond these settled lands, however, the continent transforms into an immense and untamed wilderness. Vast forests give way to scorching deserts, towering mountain ranges, endless marshes, and forgotten ruins left by civilizations long lost to time. Despite countless expeditions, much of the continent has never been explored, leaving the greater eastern reaches known only as the Unknown.

2. The Unknown

The Unknown encompasses nearly all lands beyond humanity's frontier, stretching across the eastern reaches of the Great Continent into territories that have never been fully mapped. Ancient forests, colossal mountain ranges, barren deserts, winding canyons, and forgotten ruins dominate the landscape, where nature has reclaimed everything left behind.

Powerful Wildborn roam freely without the presence of civilization, many growing to sizes and strengths unseen within the settled kingdoms. Few expeditions have ventured deep into the Unknown, and even fewer have returned. Those who do often bring conflicting maps and unbelievable tales, leaving its true size, geography, and history shrouded in mystery.

3. The Endless White

The Endless White is a seemingly boundless northern wilderness locked beneath perpetual snow and ice. Along its southern edge lies the Kingdom of Norvalen, where scattered settlements have adapted to survive one of the harshest environments in the known world.

Beyond these settlements stretches an endless realm of frozen seas, towering glaciers, jagged mountain ranges, and blinding snowfields untouched by civilization. Violent blizzards, shifting ice, and powerful Arctic Wildborn make exploration exceptionally dangerous. Few have traveled far into the Endless White, and none have ever discovered where its frozen horizon truly ends.

4. The Sea of Glass Archipelago

The Sea of Glass Archipelago is a chain of tropical islands scattered across the northwestern seas, separated from the Great Continent by the calm waters of the Sea of Glass. Blessed with fertile soil, abundant fisheries, and natural harbors, the archipelago has flourished through maritime trade, fishing, and seafaring.

The largest island is home to the Kingdom of Faylora, while countless smaller islands support independent villages, fishing settlements, lighthouses, and bustling trading ports. Together, they form one of the known world's most important maritime regions.

Despite its beauty, the surrounding seas are far from peaceful. Powerful marine Wildborn roam the deep waters, while predators lurk among reefs, cliffs, and isolated islands. Merchant vessels and fishing fleets rely on armed escorts and skilled navigators to survive the voyage, making life upon the Sea of Glass as dangerous as it is prosperous.

The Known Kingdoms

1. Norvalen Kingdom

Name Meaning: "North Vale"

Description: Norvalen is the northernmost kingdom of humanity, carved from the frozen southern reaches of the Endless White. Blanketed by perpetual snow and ice, it is a land where survival is earned through resilience rather than comfort. Fierce blizzards, frozen seas, and towering glaciers define the landscape, while powerful frost-dwelling Wildborn roam beyond its settlements.
The people of Norvalen have adapted to this unforgiving environment by constructing homes from ice, stone, and reinforced timber. Fishing beneath frozen lakes and hunting Wildborn provide their primary source of food, while thick furs and preserved supplies sustain them through the endless winters. Though civilization reaches only the kingdom's southern frontier, countless explorers have attempted to venture farther north into the Endless White. Few have ever returned.

Cities

  • Nivara City — Capital City
    Derived from the Latin word nix, meaning "snow."
  • Draemreach City

2. Faylora Kingdom

Name Meaning:Inspired by fey (mystical beauty) and flora (lush life).

Description: Faylora is a prosperous island kingdom renowned for its freedom, maritime trade, and cultural diversity. Located within the Sea of Glass Archipelago, it thrives upon fertile tropical islands surrounded by crystal-blue waters rich with marine life—and dangerous marine Wildborn.
Its bustling ports welcome merchants, explorers, artisans, and travelers from every kingdom, making Faylora the commercial heart of the known world. Colorful markets, grand harbors, and vibrant festivals reflect a society that values opportunity, innovation, and open exchange above rigid tradition.

Cities

  • Freymar City — Capital City
    From "freedom" + mar ("sea"). A thriving port city celebrated for its open trade, tolerance, and independence.

3. Aethelgard Kingdom

Name Meaning: "Noble Protection" (Old English), symbolizing strength, duty, and guardianship.

Description: Aethelgard is the strongest military power among the known kingdoms, founded upon discipline, unity, and unwavering order. A single royal house governs the realm, while trusted members of the noble family oversee its major cities. Loyalty, honor, and service are considered the pillars upon which the kingdom stands.
Positioned along much of the Verdant Ring, Aethelgard bears the greatest responsibility for defending humanity against the Unknown. Its armies are among the largest and best trained in the world, and generations of soldiers have dedicated their lives to holding the frontier.

Cities

  • Aethermarch City — Capital City
    "Marching beneath the heavens." A commanding city of towering fortresses, royal banners, and disciplined armies.
  • Halvard City — City of the First Wall
    A heavily fortified border city whose massive defenses form one of humanity's first bulwarks against the Unknown.
  • Ravenscar City — City of the Second Wall
    Built upon rugged cliffs, Ravenscar is renowned for its watchtowers, veteran guardians, and commanding views over the frontier.
  • Mistwood City — City of the Deep Forest
    Hidden beneath ancient forests and perpetual mist, Mistwood is famous for its abundance of Wildborn and Rootseal traditions. It is home to many renowned beast tamers, trackers, and Echo of Tame users.
  • Jubila City — City of Joy
    Known for its lively festivals, music, fine wines, and celebrations, Jubila provides a welcome contrast to the kingdom's otherwise disciplined nature.

4. Lanthea Kingdom

Name Meaning: Derived from soft lyrical tones, symbolizing peace and harmony with nature.

Description: Lanthea is a peaceful agricultural kingdom renowned for its fertile farmlands, tranquil lakes, and abundant coastlines. Life follows the rhythm of the seasons, and its people have built a culture centered upon farming, fishing, craftsmanship, and close ties to the land.
Though its military is modest compared to Aethelgard, Lanthea prospers through self-sufficiency, stable communities, and plentiful harvests that help feed neighboring kingdoms. Much of its eastern frontier borders the Verdant Ring, where fertile lands gradually give way to the wilderness beyond.

Cities

  • Liora City — Capital City
    A serene lakeside city known for its crystal-clear waters, blooming gardens, and elegant architecture. It serves as the cultural heart of the kingdom.
  • Tillara City — Border City
    Named after the act of cultivation, Tillara stands beside the Verdant Ring as both an agricultural center and an important frontier city.
    Ruling House: House Rosendale (Current Highlord: Richard Rosendale)
  • Salrima City — Salt City
    A thriving coastal port famous for its salt fields, fisheries, and maritime trade. Sea breezes carry the scent of salt and spices through streets lined with white-stone buildings adorned with wind chimes and drying herbs.

Landscapes of the Realm

Rockveil Cave

A vast mineral-rich cavern nestled between Halvard and Aethermarch in the Aethelgard Kingdom. Its glittering stone walls conceal abundant veins of iron, Q-metal, and other valuable ores, making it one of the kingdom's most important mining sites.

Unlike many caves beyond the Verdant Ring, Rockveil has been fortified, patrolled, and carefully managed for generations, allowing miners to safely harvest its rich resources.

Susurro Forest

A sprawling ancient woodland within the Aethelgard Kingdom, perpetually draped in mist and shadow. Hidden deep beneath its towering canopy lies Mistwood City, where sunlight rarely reaches the forest floor.

The forest teems with Wildborn, and travelers often claim the wind carries faint whispers through the trees, giving the forest its haunting reputation.

Name inspired by the Spanish and Italian word susurro, meaning "whisper."

Mount Tharros

The tallest mountain in the known world, rising over the rugged lands surrounding Ravenscarin the Aethelgard Kingdom. Its jagged peaks disappear into the clouds, while its steep cliffs and frozen ridges make climbing nearly impossible.

Legends tell of ancient ruins, rare Primeflora, and countless powerful Wildborn hidden among its heights.

Derived from the Greek word thárros, meaning "courage."

Luna Lake

A vast crystal-clear lake just beyond Liora, the capital of Lanthea. Though peaceful in appearance, its true depth has never been measured.

Local legends speak of mysterious aquatic Wildborn and rare Primeflora dwelling beneath its still waters. Under the moonlight, the lake reflects the night sky like polished silver, giving rise to its name.

Mount Korun

A massive four-peaked mountain range forming the natural border between the Kingdom of Lanthea and the Gobe Desert. Though shorter than Mount Tharros, its immense breadth stretches across the horizon like a colossal stone fortress, guarding the fertile lands beyond.

Korun is inspired by the Turkish word korun, meaning "to protect."

Gobe Desert

A vast sea of golden dunes stretching south of the Kingdom of Lanthea and marking one of humanity's southern frontiers.

Blistering days, freezing nights, and scarce water make survival difficult, while countless desert Wildborn roam its shifting sands. Beyond its far reaches lies territory swallowed by the Unknown.

Inspired by the Mongolian and Chinese word Gobi, meaning "waterless place."

Naga River

A great winding river that forms much of the natural boundary between the kingdoms of Aethelgard and Lanthea.

Its twisting course nourishes fertile farmlands, supports river trade, and serves as one of the most important waterways in the western continent.

Inspired by the Sanskrit word Nāga.

Seas of the Realm

Sea of Glass

A vast sea northwest of the Great Continent, separating it from the Sea of Glass Archipelago and the Kingdom of Faylora.

Named for its remarkably calm waters that often resemble polished glass, it remains one of humanity's most important trade routes despite the dangerous marine Wildborn lurking beneath its surface.

Frozen Sea

An icy northern sea separating the Great Continent from the southern reaches of the Endless White and the Kingdom of Norvalen.

Much of its surface remains frozen throughout the year, broken only by drifting ice and narrow shipping lanes navigated by experienced sailors. Beneath its frozen waters dwell some of the most formidable marine Wildborn known to humanity.
