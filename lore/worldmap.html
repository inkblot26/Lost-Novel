World Map

The World

Humanity occupies only a small fraction of the world.

Most civilization lies within the fertile western triangle of the Great Continent, where forests, rivers, mountains, and lakes have allowed kingdoms, cities, and villages to flourish for centuries. Beyond this settled frontier stretches an immense wilderness known simply as the Unknown—a realm of unexplored lands inhabited by powerful Wildborn and the remnants of forgotten civilizations.

Across the Sea of Glass to the northwest lies the Sea of Glass Archipelago, home to the island Kingdom of Faylora, while far to the north stretches the Endless White, an immeasurable frozen wilderness where the Kingdom of Norvalen endures amid eternal snow and ice.

Separating civilization from the Unknown is the Verdant Ring—a continuous chain of fortified cities, military outposts, and defensive walls stretching from the Frozen Sea in the north to the southern deserts. For centuries, it has served as humanity's final frontier, protecting the known world from the countless dangers beyond.

Everything east of the Verdant Ring, along with most of the Endless White, remains unexplored. Expeditions rarely return, and those who do speak of colossal Wildborn, forgotten kingdoms, ancient ruins, and landscapes unlike anything found within civilized lands.

"To most people, the world ends at the Verdant Ring. Beyond it lies only the Unknown."

Major Regions

1. The Great Continent

The Great Continent is the largest landmass known to humanity and home to the majority of civilization. Its fertile western triangle is blessed with rivers, forests, mountain ranges, and rich plains that have allowed kingdoms, cities, and villages to flourish for centuries.

Beyond these settled lands, however, the continent transforms into an immense and untamed wilderness. Vast forests give way to scorching deserts, towering mountain ranges, endless marshes, and forgotten ruins left by civilizations long lost to time. Despite countless expeditions, much of the continent has never been explored, leaving the greater eastern reaches known only as the Unknown.

2. The Unknown

The Unknown encompasses nearly all lands beyond humanity's frontier, stretching across the eastern reaches of the Great Continent into territories that have never been fully mapped. Ancient forests, colossal mountain ranges, barren deserts, winding canyons, and forgotten ruins dominate the landscape, where nature has reclaimed everything left behind.

Powerful Wildborn roam freely without the presence of civilization, many growing to sizes and strengths unseen within the settled kingdoms. Few expeditions have ventured deep into the Unknown, and even fewer have returned. Those who do often bring conflicting maps and unbelievable tales, leaving its true size, geography, and history shrouded in mystery.

3. The Endless White

The Endless White is a seemingly boundless northern wilderness locked beneath perpetual snow and ice. Along its southern edge lies the Kingdom of Norvalen, where scattered settlements have adapted to survive one of the harshest environments in the known world.

Beyond these settlements stretches an endless realm of frozen seas, towering glaciers, jagged mountain ranges, and blinding snowfields untouched by civilization. Violent blizzards, shifting ice, and powerful Arctic Wildborn make exploration exceptionally dangerous. Few have traveled far into the Endless White, and none have ever discovered where its frozen horizon truly ends.

4. The Sea of Glass Archipelago

The Sea of Glass Archipelago is a chain of tropical islands scattered across the northwestern seas, separated from the Great Continent by the calm waters of the Sea of Glass. Blessed with fertile soil, abundant fisheries, and natural harbors, the archipelago has flourished through maritime trade, fishing, and seafaring.

The largest island is home to the Kingdom of Faylora, while countless smaller islands support independent villages, fishing settlements, lighthouses, and bustling trading ports. Together, they form one of the known world's most important maritime regions.

Despite its beauty, the surrounding seas are far from peaceful. Powerful marine Wildborn roam the deep waters, while predators lurk among reefs, cliffs, and isolated islands. Merchant vessels and fishing fleets rely on armed escorts and skilled navigators to survive the voyage, making life upon the Sea of Glass as dangerous as it is prosperous.
