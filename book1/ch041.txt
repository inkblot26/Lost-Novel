Chapter 41: The Voice of Authority
“Look what you’ve done, dear sister,” Richard sneered, conjuring another small flaming bird and hurling it at Rose. Her barrier, now thin and cracking, barely held the flames at bay.
“Look at heroic Henry now—bleeding out because of your foolishness.” He laughed, bitter and wild. “And all those poor souls you dragged into this with you. When the soldiers arrive, I’ll make sure each one of you suffers.”
Rose held firm, teeth clenched as she kept her barrier alive, eyes flicking toward Henry, who lay wounded a short distance away. Blood pooled beneath him.
I need to do something. Fast. Sir Henry needs help, she thought, desperately searching for an opening.
Then the barrier shattered with a sharp crack.
Rose ducked as another flaming bird flew past her head, scorching the stone wall and leaving a blackened mark near one of the tall windows. 
“I'm going to enjoy killing you... slowly,” Richard hissed, grabbing his longsword from the floor.

Out in the narrow hallway, lit by evenly spaced wall torches, Ezra spotted Leon slumped against the wall near a closed door, struggling to push himself back to his feet. 
“Damn it,” Leon muttered, pushing himself up, only to be struck in the legs by a white bolt of lightning from Ezra’s fingertips. He collapsed with a grunt.
“Don’t move,” Ezra commanded coldly, stepping forward with six fighters behind her. “Secure him,” she ordered, and one of them moved to bind Leon as she pushed open the door ahead.
Inside the chamber, overturned chairs and scorch marks scattered across the polished floor showed how much damage had already been done.
Lady Rose stood with blood trickling down her arms, a burn across one cheek and a small one near her left eye. Her rapier was gone. She struggled to defend herself, staggering under Richard’s relentless assault.
“No!” Ezra shouted, releasing a flurry of electric bolts that struck Richard in the side, just as he raised his blade.
Ezra’s Echo-forged sword appeared in her hand in a flash. She leapt forward and intercepted Richard’s swing, knocking it away from Rose.
“Ugh—!” Richard stumbled and dropped back, sliding across the floor.
“I’m sorry I’m late, my lady,” Ezra said, catching Rose and helping her stay upright. But her eyes never left Richard.
“I’m alright… help Sir Henry. Now,” Rose gasped.
Two fighters rushed to Henry's side, lifted him, and carried him toward Miss Mira for treatment.
“This... this isn't possible!” Richard growled as he limped backward. “How did all of you even get this far?”
“It’s over, brother,” Rose said, picking up the broken rapier. Her voice was cold but steady. “Hand over the Ring of Authority.”
Ezra and three other fighters flanked her, slowly closing in on Richard as he backed toward the arched window, his options vanishing by the second.
Suddenly, Richard pulled out a Whispering Scroll of Air and unrolled it.
A wide slicing blast of wind tore through the room.
Ezra reacted instantly, grabbing Rose and pulling her down, shielding her with her own body. At the same moment, Rose summoned a small barrier in front of them, just enough to take the edge off the blast.
Two nearby fighters weren’t as lucky, caught mid-motion and cleanly severed in half. A third managed to leap clear, but his lower left leg was sliced off. He collapsed to the floor, screaming in agony.
Richard didn't hesitate. With cold precision, he conjured multiple flaming birds and launched them at the wounded man, engulfing him in flames.
Then he turned, raising his longsword high, and brought it down toward Ezra. She barely managed to block the strike, sparks flying from the clash of steel and summoned energy.
“Brother!” Rose screamed.
With a final surge of strength, she slashed with her broken rapier, its jagged edge cutting through Richard’s right wrist and severing the hand that wore the Ring of Authority.
Richard howled in pain, staggering back. Blood poured from his wrist.
Desperate, he stabbed his weapon into the ground and pulled out another Whispering Scroll, this one of Light, and activated it.
A blinding flash exploded through the room.
By the time the light faded, he had crashed through the window.
Outside, a massive Willborn, a towering avian creature known as a Sunquill, swooped into view. Cloaked in vibrant yellow feathers streaked with gold and pale white, it caught Richard mid-fall and carried him into the sky, disappearing beyond the mansion walls.
“Are you hurt, my lady?” Ezra asked, helping Rose to her feet.
“I’m fine. Thank you,” Rose replied, her gaze drifting to the broken window. In the distance, she caught a glimpse of long golden tail feathers vanishing into the clouds.
“So that Willborn still lives,” she murmured, stepping forward and picking up Richard’s severed hand. With a firm breath, she pulled off the Ring of Authority.
“What was that creature?” Ezra asked, still watching the sky.
“A Wildborn my grandfather tamed in the Gobe Desert using a Rootseal. It was passed down to my father when he ruled. I hadn’t seen it since he died... I thought it had gained its freedom.”
Ezra frowned. “Then Richard must have found another Rootseal and forced it back into servitude. What do we do now, my lady? We can’t follow him.”
“We don't need to. He has no power anymore. No one will follow him.” Rose said, glancing at the three fallen fighters. Her voice softened. “Too many have already paid the price.”
She tightened her grip on the ring. “Now that it’s ours, we head to the rooftop. This bloodshed must end.”
“Yes, Highlady Rose,” Ezra said with a respectful bow.

On the flat stone rooftop of the mansion, Rose stepped toward the edge and raised her hand to the sky. From there, the courtyard and parts of the surrounding district were clearly visible. The Ring of Authority on her finger glowed, casting a soft green light that shimmered across the battlefield.
Her voice rang out, amplified by her Echo of Sound. It was gentle, but impossible to ignore. 
“Everyone, stop. In the name of the King—I, Rose Rosendale, your new Highlady—order all fighting to cease.”
All around the mansion, everyone—whether ally or enemy—froze.
Outside, guards and fighters alike dropped to their knees as the green light spread across the battlefield. It was a symbol none could ignore, the sacred power of authority granted by the king of Lanthea. 
“It’s the Ring of Authority,” one of the fighters whispered, awestruck. “Only someone of the Rosendale line can wield it…”

Inside, on the ground floor near the base of the main staircase, a kind-eyed young woman knelt and carefully dropped glistening nectar over a wounded warrior’s gash. Her Echo of Heal shimmered faintly as the wound began to close.
“That voice... it's Lady Rose,” she said softly.
“Miss Mira! She did it, she’s won!” a fighter called out, pointing toward the window where green light beamed from above. 
“I know. Now gather everyone who can still stand. We need to treat the injured fighters and guards,” Mira commanded.
“Even the guards, Miss Mira?” the fighter asked, hesitating.
“Yes. Now go!” she said firmly.
Clutching her hands, Mira murmured, You did it, my lady. Now you can rebuild this city into something better.

On the second floor, after receiving treatment from Miss Mira’s Echo of Heal, Torren regained enough stamina to fight. He headed up, intending to reinforce Lord Henry and Lady Rose—but instead found Lord Henry already being carried by two fighters, rushing him downstairs for urgent care.
Torren quickly stepped in to help, lifting Lord Henry and carrying him back to Miss Mira.
Just then, Lady Rose’s voice rang out in the distance, her amplified announcement echoing through the mansion. 
“We did it, Lord Henry. Just hang in there,” Torren said between breaths. “Soon, we’ll call Lady Clara and Young Ced to come back.”
Henry managed a faint smile. “That’s good…”

Back on the rooftop, Rose handed Ezra two sealed letters. “Send this to Sam’s location immediately, and deliver this to whoever is leading the soldiers there. The second letter must reach the Northern District’s military base camp—give it to Captain John. It will explain everything that happened tonight.”
She pressed the Ring of Authority onto both scrolls, sealing them with its emerald glow.
Ezra nodded, but Rose added, “Also, send a bird and any fast fighters you can spare to Oldman’s side. We need an update from them.” 
“Yes, Highlady Rose,” Ezra said, bowing before rushing down the stairs.
Rose lingered near the rooftop’s edge, the steady night wind moving through her hair and carrying the distant sounds of the city below.
I hope you all get what you’re fighting for, she thought, remembering the determined faces of Oldman, Gian, and Kel during their meeting.
She lifted her gaze to the sky.
Father… Mother… I will rebuild this city, just as you did. I will bring out the best in everyone, just like you taught me.
