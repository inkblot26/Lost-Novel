Chapter 61: Enemies in the Mist
Early the next morning, the group crept silently toward the bandit camp. Mist clung to the trees, softening their footsteps as they circled to the lone wooden house at the back.
Through a narrow window, Gian peeked inside. The remaining bandit sat slouched at a table, lazily shuffling cards and chuckling to himself. Outside, the leashed dog by the camp’s edge barely stirred, its back turned.
Kel smirked, gripping his Crimson Blood Axe. “Good thing he’s an idiot, huh—”
Thwip!
From the trees, an arrow of pure ice sliced through the air and buried itself in Kel’s shoulder. 
“Kel!” Gian grabbed him just as a second arrow, this one steel, slammed into the ground where Kel had been.
The sudden noise jolted the lone bandit, Maro. He rushed outside, only to see three unfamiliar figures crouched behind a broad Morphguard. Its shield was now fully expanded into kite mode, blocking a relentless hail of icy and steel arrows raining from the trees.
Maro froze.
Two women emerged from the trees. One, with short black hair with green highlights, calmly drew an ice-formed bow. The other, a striking blonde with long hair, moved with steady precision as she readied her traditional bow.
“Who… who are they?” Kel muttered, clutching his bleeding shoulder as the ice arrow melted into water.
Maro didn’t wait to find out. Panic flashing in his eyes, he bolted into the woods.
“He’s getting away!” Oldman barked.
“Go after him, Kel!” Gian ordered, eyes locked on the two approaching women. “Oldman and I will handle these two!”
“Got it!” Kel gritted his teeth and sprinted after the fleeing bandit.
The blonde young woman glanced toward Maro. “Should we pursue him?”
“Leave him. We’ll deal with him later,” the short-haired woman said calmly. She drew her icy bowstring again, a faint smile forming. “But this is interesting. That shield—it’s a Relic. I want it.”
Oldman didn’t give them time to act. He swung the Stickk, its length snapping outward like a whip toward the short-haired woman.
But with quick reflexes, she pressed a hand on her ally’s shoulder, pulling her down. Both ducked in perfect sync. The Relic’s extended strike sliced harmlessly through the air above them.
“Go, Kel!” Oldman shouted as he charged the two women. Gian retracted the Morphguard into its compact buckler form for mobility and dashed alongside him.
The black-haired woman narrowed her eyes. They have multiple Relics. This could be dangerous.
“Anni, be careful. They’re not your average ju,” she warned.
“Don’t treat me like a child, Auntie El,” Anni shot back, sharp but youthful. “And stop saying those weird old words you picked up on your boring book.”
“They’re ancient forgotten words, thank you very much,” El replied with a faint laugh. Her ice bow dissolved into liquid frost, reshaping into a long spear of pure ice. She thrust at Oldman.
He sidestepped, swinging the Stickk in a vertical arc. El met it with her ice spear in a ringing clash,  before they separated. They immediately collided again in a vicious flurry.
Meanwhile, Gian’s short sword thrust toward Anni, but she parried deftly with her saber. Frost shimmered in her left hand as tiny crystalline butterflies formed midair, darting toward him like silent predators.
Gian rolled aside, narrowly avoiding their first strike, only for them to curve unnaturally and chase him. Anni conjured two more icy butterflies, which hovered ominously as she advanced.
Gian swung his blade, shattering one, but frost clung to its edge. Anni was already there, her saber slashing diagonally for his chest. Gian barely raised his buckler in time. The impact rattled his arm, and two more butterflies curved around him and struck his back, chilling and biting through the thin gaps in his armor.
She’s strong... I may have to use it, Gian thought grimly, ducking another swing. He glanced sideways. Oldman was locked in a brutal exchange with El, slowly being pushed back. 
“Don’t look away from me!” Anni shouted, fury in her eyes. Frost flared around her as she summoned two more butterflies. “You’re all going to die here. You can’t save your ugly friend!”
Gian smirked despite the pressure. He parried her saber aside but didn’t counter. Instead, he jumped back just as the two butterflies lunged where he’d been.
“My friend doesn’t need saving. He’s too stubborn for that. And nice try baiting me, but it won’t work.”
Meanwhile, Oldman exhaled sharply. This isn’t working. I’m better in close quarters.
He shrank the Stickk Relic back to arm’s length, shifting his stance.
“Oh? You’re fighting my spear with a shorter weapon now?” El tilted her head with a smile. “Fine. Let’s see who’s better.”
The tip of her spear melted into frost and reformed into twin ice blades.
“Tsk. So you are an Echo Master. You can freely shape your ice,” Oldman muttered.
He shouted over his shoulder, “Gian! Finish that quickly and help me if you can! Don’t hold back just because she’s a woman!”
Hearing this, Gian frowned. "I just said you don’t need saving.”
“Oh my… asking for help already? How shameful,” El teased, covering a sly laugh with one hand.
“Every Echo Master is dangerous,” Oldman said flatly. “I might defeat you, but I don’t want to lose an arm doing it. So yeah, I’ll gladly take help.”
Tata, get away. I need to focus. He sent the thought silently.
The little driftmouse peeked from his pocket, squeaked softly, then hopped down and scurried to safety.
“A cute pet. How sweet,” El said mockingly. “But enough waiting. Let’s finish this!”
She darted forward, twin ice blades slashing in a rapid flurry. Oldman stood firm, deflecting each strike with precise swings of the Stickk Relic.
But she was relentless. A feint slipped past his guard, and the second blade slashed across his back. Pain flared, but Oldman gritted his teeth, spun, and drove a sharp kick into El’s face, forcing her back.
El wiped blood from her lip and smiled wider. “I think I owe you an apology. You are strong. You didn’t even flinch at a wound like that.”
“These are just shallow cuts. I’ve had worse,” Oldman replied coldly.

Elsewhere, Gian kept retreating under Anni’s relentless assault, deflecting her saber and shielding against the hovering butterflies circling like icy predators.
“Is what your ugly friend said true? You’re holding back? Don’t you dare look down on me!” Anni snarled. “I may be a woman, but I’ve trained my whole life!”
“That’s nonsense,” Gian replied calmly. “I believe in equality. Man, woman, young, old—it doesn’t matter. If you raise a weapon, be ready to face mine in return.”
Step by step, he lured her toward the cliff’s edge.
The rising sun crested behind him, flooding the ridge with blinding light. Anni winced, raising her hand to shield her eyes.
That split-second was all Gian needed.
He knocked her saber aside—it spun from her grip into the air—and followed with a horizontal slash toward her neck. But two ice butterflies darted in, intercepting the blade just in time. Frost exploded on impact, giving Anni the chance to leap back.
She landed lightly but was now unarmed.
From a distance, El shouted, “Anni!” Then she leapt back to Oldman’s side, hurling her two short ice swords at him before quickly manifesting an ice bow.
Oldman spun the Stickk Relic in a wide arc, deflecting the blades with practiced ease. “Your opponent is me,” he said, preparing to charge forward—when suddenly a crushing weight pinned him in place.
Oh no, she has another Echo… and a rare one.
El released an ice arrow aimed straight at Gian.
Gian raised his buckler just in time, blocking the arrow and giving Anni the chance to snatch up her saber.
“Don’t help me, Auntie! I’ll finish these bandits myself. I’ll make sure they all die!” Anni snarled, summoning three shimmering ice butterflies.
“Bandits?” Oldman frowned, confused. “Who the hell are bandits?” He glanced at El, who looked equally puzzled.
El lowered her ice bow, pausing the fight. “Wait… are you guys not bandits?”
“Do I look like a bandit? You're the bandits!” Oldman snapped back angrily.
“What? No, we’re not!” El said quickly, releasing the gravity Echo that had pinned Oldman. He straightened as the weight lifted.
“We’re after Diego, the bandit. We’re… uh—wait, what are we, Gian? Avengers? No… adventurers, I guess. Just with a different goal.” Oldman muttered.
Gian slowly lowered his shield and sword but stayed wary. “We’re not bandits. We came to kill Diego. Who exactly are you two?”
El stepped closer, her tone serious. “My niece and I live nearby. We heard what happened to Takamine Lesser Village, found this bandit base, and decided to hunt them down.”
“Where are the bandits? Don’t lie—that’s your base!” Anni accused fiercely.
“They left before sunrise. That’s why we were here, to ambush Diego when he returns,” Gian said firmly. 
“Then why are we fighting each other?” El tilted her head in confusion.
“The hell! You’re the ones who attacked us, and Kel’s injured because of it!” Oldman growled.
Raising her hands apologetically, El said, “Sorry about that. I hope the kid isn’t too angry.”
“Oldman, we have to find Kel,” Gian said urgently.
“On it. I sent Tata to follow him. Let’s move!” Oldman dashed toward the trees.
“Wait,” El called, stopping Gian briefly. “We really want to kill Diego too. Can we come with you?”
Anni frowned. “Auntie, they might be lying. How can we trust them?” She sheathed her saber, dissolving the ice butterflies.
Gian tightened his grip on his sword. “You can come, but if you betray us, I’ll kill you both,” he warned, then sprinted after Oldman.
El smiled faintly and looked back at Anni. “What a scary young man. Let’s go.”
“Why? We don’t need them. We can handle the bandits ourselves,” Anni muttered.
Exhaling, El replied, “We need them, Anni. I let you come because I believed you were strong enough to keep yourself safe. But clearly, that’s not the case here.”
“That was just a fluke. He used the sunlight, and I wasn’t ready. I can beat him,” Anni insisted.
El brushed a silver wing-shaped hairclip into Anni’s blonde hair and said gently, “I know you’re strong, and you have more potential than I did at your age. But this isn’t training. Real fights have many variables. That’s why I brought you along—to gain experience.” 
“We’re joining these guys. They probably know more about the bandits. Let’s move quickly or we’ll lose them. They’ll vanish like tiny forest payries out here,” El added.
“Fine, you and your weird ‘payries’ talk,” Anni muttered, rolling her eyes as she followed.
“Hey, ‘payries’ is a real word. Small, ugly forest creatures with wings. They even grant wishes or something… at least from what I’ve read from that book,” El laughed as they ran after Gian and Oldman.
