Chapter 38: The Ones Who Stayed Behind
In a narrow, dimly lit hallway, shadows pooled in the corners, absorbing the flickering light from torches set in iron sconces. Ezra and Jett wove through the space, their movements throwing fleeting reflections off the polished stone floor. Ezra continuously fired bolts of electricity from her fingers, but Jett slipped away every time, dancing just out of reach.
“I think this is far enough, Captain Ezra,” Jett said with a grin, deflecting the summoned sword in her left hand. He lunged suddenly, his blade piercing the gap in her scale armor at the shoulder. Blood began to seep through the metal as he quickly backed off, grinning wider.
“Nice cut, right?” he said, nodding toward her armor as crimson trickled down.
Ezra didn’t flinch. She calmly switched her sword to her right hand, eyes narrowed.
That reflex… that precision… He must have the Echo of Focus, she thought.
“No comment? Then I’ll keep attacking!” Jett laughed, rushing forward again.
Ezra slashed diagonally, but at the last possible moment, Jett ducked under the blow and thrust toward her leg. She shifted just in time, the blade scraping against her armor instead of piercing it. Jett grinned and retreated once more, light on his feet.
He's not unbeatable. That Echo of Focus takes a toll on the user. He can’t maintain it continuously, so he must be turning it on and off. That’s why he keeps backing away, Ezra realized.
Jett exhaled slowly, steadying his breath. His senses sharpened, and he felt everything—the distant clashes of battle, the dry scent of dust, and Ezra’s calm, steady breathing just ahead of him.
Then he moved.
He saw the arc of the electric bolt from Ezra’s unstable left hand, her aim thrown off by the wound in her shoulder, and easily dodged it, closing in and slashing his sword toward her neck.
Ezra tilted her head just in time. The blade sliced across her cheek instead of her throat as she stepped back to regain distance.
"So close," Jett said with a smirk.
"Yeah, your death," Ezra replied as she charged forward. But before reaching him, she suddenly hurled her summoned sword, then raised all ten fingers. In an instant, ten bolts of electricity burst out, each aimed at him.
Jett activated his Echo of Focus. He dodged the sword and evaded each bolt at the last possible moment, deflecting some with his blade. The final bolt nearly struck him, grazing past as he twisted his body just in time.
But then he saw it. The summoned sword he had dodged earlier had curved back midair, guided by Ezra’s thought. It pierced his face cleanly, ending him before he could react.
Walking toward Jett, Ezra pulled her summoned sword from the dead body.
After a moment, two fighters approached. One limped heavily, favoring his right leg. 
“Are you alright, Captain Ezra?” one asked.
“I think we should get you downstairs, Captain,” the other added. “Miss Mira is just at the bottom of the main hall, healing the others.”
He paused, noticing the blood soaking her shoulder.
“No need,” Ezra replied, grimacing. “I just need to stop the bleeding. Lady Rose needs me. You two should go and get yourselves treated.”
Ezra peeled off her scale armor, the metal clinking softly as she set it aside on the stone floor. She tore a strip of cloth and pressed it against her bleeding shoulder.
“That’s unacceptable, Captain,” said the limping fighter.
“You can’t stop me,” Ezra said, locking eyes with them. “I’ll follow Lady Rose no matter what.”
“We’re not stopping you,” the other fighter said gently, stepping forward to help her bind her shoulder. “We’re going with you, Captain.”
Ezra nodded. “Alright. Let’s go. Our lady needs us.”
And together, the trio moved out. 

At the stairway leading to the third floor, an intense battle was unfolding. Lady Rose had arrived to reinforce Lord Henry’s group against the enemy guards.
An arrow from Nora struck the arm of the guard dueling Sir Henry. Seizing the opening, he hurled a lance of molten rock, impaling the enemy against a stone pillar.
“Are you all right, Lord Henry?” Nora asked, hurrying to his side as he panted from overusing his Echo.
“I’m fine. Don’t worry about me, help the others,” Lord Henry replied, catching sight of Lady Rose raising a barrier to shield Torren from a rampaging shadowy bear.
Without hesitation, he summoned another lance and flung it at a guard whose back was turned, locked in combat with one of their allies. Then he sprinted toward Torren and Lady Rose, as chaos raged all around them.
Suddenly a sword slashed sideways toward him, missing only because he deflected it with the staff relic in his left hand. Before he could recover, the guard kicked him hard, sending him sprawling to the ground. The attacker advanced with his sword raised.
Still gripping the staff, Henry thrust it forward. In an instant, the tip shot out and snapped back, slamming into the guard’s ribs with a solid crack, catching him completely off guard.
Panting, Henry climbed to his feet and swung the relic once more. It extended and slammed into the guard’s face, dropping him. In the same breath, a short molten lance formed in Henry’s right hand. With a swift motion, he hurled it at the downed guard, ending the fight.

At the front, Torren swung his warhammer with full force toward Conrad, but the shadowy bear lunged in, its massive body absorbing the blow. At the same time, Conrad, standing beside the beast, slashed his greatsword upward. The attack was halted briefly by Lady Rose’s barrier before it shattered from the force. That brief pause gave Torren just enough time to step back and avoid the deadly swing.
Panting behind Torren, Lady Rose gripped her rapier tightly. She hesitated, unsure what to do, unable to get close to the duel between the two captains who have mastered their Echo of Strength. The shockwaves from each clash were powerful enough to shake the floor. 
“Torren,” Conrad called out with a mocking grin, resting his greatsword against his broad shoulder. “How long do you think that little maiden can keep assisting you?”
“He’s right, Lady Rose. I’ll give you a chance. Head upstairs,” Torren said, keeping his eyes locked on Conrad. Just then, Lord Henry and another fighter caught up to them. 
“No matter how many of you there are, I’ll kill you all!” Conrad roared, commanding the bear to charge as he rushed behind it.
“Now!” Torren shouted.
He swung his warhammer with all his might, striking the bear squarely. With a tremendous crash, the blow sent the beast flying straight into Conrad. Both of them were thrown to the side, crashing against the wall at a distance.
“Follow us as soon as you can,” Lord Henry ordered as he, Lady Rose, and the other fighter sprinted up the stairs.
Nearby, Nora loosed an arrow into the side of a guard locked in a deadlock with one of their allies. Seizing the opportunity, the ally beheaded the guard. Without wasting a second, Nora and the fighter rushed after Lady Rose’s group.
Seeing five of them manage to escape up the stairs, Conrad let out an enraged roar and charged straight at Torren.
“Let’s continue our duel,” Torren said with a smile.

Inside the main chamber on the third floor, the mansion’s head guard spoke cautiously. “My Highlord, you should leave. I sense five lifeforms approaching the stairs.”
“And where exactly would I go now, Dan, if they’re already here?” Highlord Richard snapped. “That fool Conrad... he let them slip through.”
I just need to hold on. It’s almost here, the Highlord thought, tightening his grip on his longsword as he stared out the arched window.
“Leon, when that door opens, ambush them. Dan, stay in front of me,” he ordered calmly.
“Yes, my Highlord,” both replied.
Leon activated his Echo of Claw and Echo of Agility, his eyes flicking from the door to the sharp claws extending from his fingers. He crouched, poised to strike.
The door swung open, and in a blur, Leon leapt forward. The person at the front of the group, just outside the hall, was slashed across the neck before they could even react.
As the fighter collapsed, his eyes met Lady Rose’s, no fear in them, only quiet gratitude for the hope she’d given him. Then the light left them.
Lady Rose immediately activated her barrier, its energy spreading around the group despite the toll it took on her. They could only watch as their ally collapsed before them.
“He’s fast. This is going to be difficult,” Lord Henry muttered, eyes fixed on Leon now standing menacingly in the hall.
Surveying the situation, Nora quickly spoke. “Lady Rose, Lord Henry, you two should go inside. We’ll hold the door.”
“Alright. Just stall him,” Lord Henry replied as he and Lady Rose dashed into the chamber.
“Hey, where are you going?” Leon shouted, but Nora unleashed a barrage of arrows at impossible speed, forcing him to stay back and cutting off his advance.
“We’ll hold him here, no matter what,” Nora said to the other fighter, who quickly closed the door behind them.
