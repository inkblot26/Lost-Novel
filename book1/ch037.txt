Chapter 37: Climb to the Top
Inside Highlord Richard’s opulent mansion, the walls shuddered from distant explosions, and the echo of clashing steel and frantic shouts reverberated up the polished marble staircases.
“Lord, we need to escape, just to be safe,” Dan, the captain of the mansion guard, urged, his voice tense. “Many of them are already on the second floor. It might only be a matter of time before they reach the third.” 
“I disagree, my lord. You should stay put,” said Leon, one of the personal guards, his hands already transformed into claws.
Suddenly, the door swung open and Conrad barged in, wearing only shorts and holding a massive greatsword.
“Lord Richard, are you alright?” he asked.
“Do I look alright, you dumb ape? Where were you? And why are you dressed like that? Don’t tell me you were having fun with one of the maids while we’re being attacked!” Richard shouted, furious.
“My lord,” Dan began, but Richard slapped him across the face with the back of his hand.
“Silence! Give me a minute to think,” Richard snapped. “My damn sister… I should have known she would do this.”
His voice seethed with rage. “We’re not going anywhere. I want to kill that woman myself. Conrad, take every guard on this floor and go down there. Dan and Leon, stay with me.”
He drew his longsword, the blade catching the warm glow of crystal chandeliers overhead, their light dancing across the high, ornate ceiling and casting flickering reflections on the polished wood floors. 
“Now go. Kill them all and bring me my sister.”
“Yes, my lord!” Conrad shouted as he rushed out, barking orders to the third-floor guards.
If it comes to the worst, I will just use that. You will never have my Ring of Authority, Richard thought, gripping the hilt of his sword tightly as he sat back in his ornate wooden chair.

An arrow came flying toward Lady Rose, but just before it struck, a barrier shimmered into existence, deflecting it. She had activated her Echo of Shell.
"My lady, you should stay back! It's getting dangerous!" Ezra shouted, releasing a charged bolt from her fingertip. The bolt shot through the air and struck the guard who had fired the arrow.
"Don’t worry. I can protect myself," Lady Rose said firmly. "We’re almost at the stairway to the third floor. Let’s go!"
Their group of five fought through the guards, when suddenly a horizontal slash came from one of the enemies, swift and precise. At the last second, Ezra’s summoned sword intercepted the blow with a sharp clang.
The attacker chuckled as he backed off. “I almost got your lady there. Didn’t expect someone would block that.”
“This one’s dangerous. I’ll handle him. The rest of you, take down the other guards and go with Lady Rose!” Ezra ordered as she charged forward.
She lunged at the grinning swordsman, but he dodged effortlessly, as if he knew exactly where her strike would land. Without stopping, she waved her hand and pointed two fingers. Two electric bolts shot forward, but he easily dodged them by sidestepping.
“I’m honored. You’re Captain Ezra, right?” he said, retreating slightly to widen the distance.
“And you’re Jett, the mad mercenary,” Ezra replied, immediately pursuing him.
“What do you say we dance a little, Captain?” Jett said with a grin.
“Fine. Let’s see how long you can keep up with me,” Ezra answered, tightening her grip on her summoned sword as she continued her assault.
Nearby, a shadowy snake formed by the Echo of Pack lunged and bit one of the fighters on the arm. Lady Rose drove her rapier into the creature, piercing its eyes and forcing it to release its grip. As it fell, the snake tried to strike at her but dissolved mid-jump when an arrow struck down the guard who had summoned it.
Looking around, Lady Rose saw reinforcements arriving, joining the clash between her group and the mansion guards. 
“My lady, are you alright?” asked Nora, one of her personal guards, holding a summoned bow as she ran to her side.
“Yes. Some of you stay here and assist Ezra. The rest, come with me!” Lady Rose shouted, dashing ahead.
“What about Ezra and the others?” Nora asked as they ran together.
“Fighting here is meaningless. Once we get to my brother and bring him down, all of this will stop,” Lady Rose replied, her eyes burning with resolve.
When they reached the stairway, they saw Torren clad in armor, locked in combat with a man wearing only shorts and wielding a massive greatsword. Each clash rang like a bell through the mansion’s high-ceilinged hall, steel striking steel with a thunderous impact, both men empowered by the Echo of Strength, making the floorboards tremble beneath them.
Torren was at a disadvantage beside his opponent stood a shadowy bear, summoned by the Echo of Pack, assisting by blocking his warhammer strikes with its hulking form.
“Let’s see which of us has truly mastered the Echo of Strength,” Conrad said, his voice low and brimming with confidence. He swung wide in a brutal arc, his blade howling through the air, aiming to cleave Torren in two.
Torren barely had time to react. He braced himself, gripping the haft of his warhammer with both hands. Steel met steel with a thunderous crack, the impact surging through his arms and hurling him backward several feet. He managed to plant his boots and steady himself just as a shadowy bear lunged forward. Its massive claws smashed into his armor, denting the steel and slamming him to the ground.
Torren groaned but rose, his iron shoulders trembling, jaw set. “You may carry strength,” he growled, “but my shoulders carry weight.”
To the side, seven guards and five allied fighters were engaged in a fierce swordfight. Amid the chaos, Lord Henry stood firm, using a dark staff to deflect incoming arrows. With a sharp motion, he channeled his Echo of Magma to conjure a glowing lance of molten rock, then hurled it at an enemy archer who was unleashing a relentless barrage using the Echo of Bow.
"Nora, assist Lord Henry and take down that bow user. The two of you, help the fighters!" Lady Rose ordered, directing Nora and the two fighters with them as she ran toward Torren to assist him.

After rushing into Ronald’s mansion, Gian spotted two guards stationed at the base of the staircase.
“You two handle them. I’ll take care of the fire!” he shouted, as the other two sprinted ahead.
Gian glanced at his heavy kite shield and thought of its earlier form. As if responding to his thoughts, the shield folded in on itself, reverting back into its compact, circular buckler form. Now lightweight, Gian dashed forward to join the fight. 
“Damn, they got inside!” Rex snarled from the interior balcony on the second floor as he ran toward the top of the stairs. Flames gathered in his hands, building toward a powerful strike as he prepared to intercept them. 
“Let’s go!” Kel said, engaging one of the guards. He brought his Crimson Blood Axe down in a powerful hack, while the guard raised his sword horizontally to block the attack.
Meanwhile, Oldman threw a dagger at the second guard. The blade was deflected with a swipe of the sword, but in that brief opening, Oldman closed in and landed a quick left-right jab to the guard’s face.
As the clash unfolded below, Gian raced up the stairs, determined to reach Rex before he could release the fire in his hands.
“I don't have enough time,” Rex thought, gritting his teeth as he slowly raised both burning hands toward Gian. The flames hadn’t fully charged, but he released them anyway, unleashing another wave of fire down the stairway.
Seeing Rex’s hands rise, Gian reacted quickly. His shield shifted back into its heavier kite form just in time. The blazing heat surged around him, searing the air, but he held his ground. Though he struggled under the shield’s weight, he stood steady and unyielding.
At the bottom of the stairs, Oldman struck the guard’s wrist, forcing him to drop his sword. He followed up with a hard punch to the gut, causing the man to double over in pain.
Nearby, Kel launched relentless strikes at the second guard, giving him no time to recover. The guard retreated, blocking frantically. Then came a slash to his shoulder. Pain shot through him. Another cut grazed his side. A third landed on his free hand. The attacks were getting faster. Stronger.
As he stared at the axe, panic crept into the guard’s eyes and fear began to take hold.
Then the axe struck the guard’s leg, nearly severing it. He collapsed to the ground with a scream.
Kel stepped forward and delivered the killing blow, cleaving the man through the chest. Breathing heavily, he paused for a moment before glancing around.
Oldman was grappling the second guard, strangling him with an armlock
On the staircase, Gian had managed to hold on until the flame attack stopped, and without hesitation, Kel rushed up the steps to join him.
Suddenly, the door to Ronald’s room burst open. A shadowy wolf—formed by Titus’s Echo of Pack—hurtled forward, claws scraping the polished banister, before colliding with Gian’s kite shield with a thunderous crash. The force sent him tumbling down the stairs.
Titus stepped out, longsword in hand, his eyes scanning the ground floor from the interior balcony. Oldman met his gaze with a hard stare, while Kel helped Gian back to his feet. 
“This is as far as you’ll get,” Titus said coldly, walking until he reached the top of the stairs. The shadow wolf growled beside him, its eyes locked on them.
Gian gritted his teeth, transforming his shield back into its compact buckler form. He looked up and declared, “You can’t stop us.”
“Hand over Ronald, and we’ll let you walk away,” Kel added, his voice firm.
Oldman remained silent, but the fury burning in his eyes spoke louder than words.
