Chapter 39: A clean shot
“How dare you insult Lord Ronald! Who are you, and why did you attack this mansion? Don’t you know Lord Ronald is the younger brother of the Highlord?!” Titus shouted.
“We’re precisely here because of that,” Kel said with a smirk.
“Let’s go! He’s just stalling, trying to buy time for that guard to hit us with fire!” Gian shouted, glancing at Rex before rushing toward Titus.
Damn, he noticed, Rex thought as he drew his machete.
“Attack!” Titus roared, summoning two shadowy wolves that materialized beside him.
“That’s three wolves. He’s strong, be ready!” Oldman warned, grabbing a sword from a fallen guard and following Gian up the steps.
Kel bit hard into his left finger, drawing blood. Droplets floated into his hand before he flung them toward the leading wolf. It won’t kill it, but it should do some damage, he thought, sprinting forward with his relic axe in his right hand.
The blood droplets struck the charging wolf, wounding its side just as it lunged at Gian. Gian suddenly dropped into a low crouch on the stairs, buckler raised and short sword angled forward.
The wolf’s claw slammed into the buckler, but the sword found its mark, piercing the creature’s shadowy form. Both tumbled to the ground in a crash.
Without hesitation, Oldman stepped in and brought his blade down hard, nearly severing the beast’s head in one powerful swing.
Kel reached just in time, intercepting one of the wolves and blocking its swipe with his Crimson Blood Axe. Meanwhile, the other wolf pounced on Gian, snapping its jaws as it tried to bite him. Gian struggled beneath it, using his buckler to keep its snarling face at bay—but the beast’s claws raked into his abdomen, pinning him down.
Oldman saw the danger. Without hesitation, he hurled his sword at the wolf. The blade struck true, embedding deep. He followed through with a powerful shoulder charge, slamming into the creature and knocking it off Gian, sending both of them tumbling to the side.
A few steps up the stairs, Kel was locked in combat with a snarling wolf, holding it at bay with his axe. Suddenly, Titus stormed in and drove a powerful kick into Kel’s side, knocking him off balance as he joined the fray.
Clutching his wound with his left hand, Gian looked up to see Titus charging down the steps, longsword raised high. Titus swung at him the moment he reached the landing. Gian dodged to the side, still holding his bleeding abdomen, his short sword pointed at his advancing foe.
“You’re all going to die here,” Titus growled, his eyes gleaming with a wild, murderous intensity.
Gian didn’t answer. The two began to circle each other, slow and focused, each one reading the other's stance and waiting for an opening.
Titus struck first as his longsword slashed downward, missing Gian by inches as he leaned back. Without pause, Titus spun into a reverse sweep. The blade slammed into Gian’s short sword, nearly knocking it from his grip.
Gian staggered but caught himself, feet digging into the floor. 
I need to finish this fast… and help the others. He’s got the reach. I have to get in close.
Gian’s eyes flicked to the side. Kel had just been bitten in the left arm by the wolf he was fighting, while Oldman was slowly getting to his feet, facing another wolf that was now limping.
Then, without warning, two arrows shattered through the window. One struck Titus in the arm. The other pierced the hind leg of the wolf pinning Kel.
Seizing the moment, Kel swung his axe and buried it deep into the wolf’s skull. With a grunt, he yanked it free and kicked the beast away.
At the same time, Gian rushed toward Titus but suddenly stopped when he spotted Rex upstairs with his right hand raised high. 
“Take cover!” Gian shouted to Kel. 
Rex lowered his arms, and a small burst of fire shot toward Kel. Kel dove to the side, narrowly avoiding a direct hit, though flames licked his left leg, leaving a burn.
Gritting his teeth, Kel rolled across the floor, extinguishing the flames clinging to him.
The brief pause cost Gian dearly. Titus's longsword slashed across Gian’s left arm, just outside the edge of his buckler. Knowing he couldn’t win in a contest of reach, Gian hurled his short sword at Titus. Titus deflected the blade but didn’t expect Gian to lunge immediately after. They collided and crashed to the ground, grappling for control.
Across the room, the limping wolf leapt at Oldman. He sidestepped swiftly, landing a sharp left jab to the wolf’s face as it passed. Spinning, he followed up with a sweeping kick to its right ribs, knocking the creature hard to the floor.
“Kel! Get up and deal with the fire Echo user. I’ll help Gian!” Oldman shouted, rushing toward the brawling pair.
Ignoring the searing pain in his leg and the wound on his arm, Kel rose to his feet. He thrust his hand toward Rex, and droplets of blood flew from his fingers, aimed at the distant enemy.
Rex ducked just in time, the blood projectiles whistling past overhead.

Upstairs in the room, Lord Ronald was panicking. His eyes darted around, unsure of what to do as he desperately searched for anything he could use as a weapon. 
“Lord, I’ll help them. Stay here,” Tavin said before dashing toward the door.
“Wait—don’t leave me!” Lord Ronald cried out in fear.
Tavin reached the hallway and, seeing the chaos below, leapt from the interior balcony. As he landed on the ground floor, dark tendrils from his Echo of Tentacle flared out, absorbing some of the impact.
Oldman paused before reaching Gian and Titus, who were locked in a brutal grapple on the ground, fists flying as they struggled to overpower each other. He glanced warily at Tavin, who ignored him and sprinted straight for the main door, clearly trying to escape.
Just as he stepped outside, an arrow struck him cleanly in the head. He collapsed instantly, lifeless before he hit the ground.
Oldman’s momentary pause gave the limping wolf just enough time to tackle Gian, knocking him away from Titus. Slowly, Titus got to his feet and retrieved his longsword. Oldman rushed to Gian’s side with eyes fixed on both Titus and the shadowy wolf.
“Look at you, panting after just fighting three summoned wolves,” Titus laughed. In front of him, a small shadowy circle formed on the ground. It expanded and took shape as another full-bodied shadow wolf emerged. 
“Don’t listen to him, Gian,” the old man said quietly. “Look closely. He only added one new summon, and the limping one hasn’t fully recovered.”
“That’s because he’s getting tired,” Gian muttered, eyeing the newly formed wolf.
“Exactly. There’s a limit to how many creatures can be summoned with the Echo of Pack,” Oldman explained. “It depends on the user’s stamina and the strength of the summoned beast.” 
“What are you two whispering about?” Titus grinned. “Regretting your choices already?”
“So we just need to keep attacking,” Gian said, clutching the wound in his abdomen.
“Right. Don’t let him recover his energy,” Oldman replied, eyes locked on Titus and the two shadow wolves. “Can you still fight?”
“Yes. Don’t worry about me,” Gian said, his eyes serious.
“I’ll take the man and the fresh wolf. You finish off the limping one, then back me up,” Oldman said, laying out the plan.
“What, you’re just going to stand there all day?” Titus mocked, then cast a quick glance upstairs, where Kel and Rex were still locked in battle, axe clashing with machete. 
“Now!” Oldman barked.
They rushed forward.
The uninjured wolf swiped upward with its right claw, but Oldman slipped to the side, countering with a left hook to its ribs. He followed up with a sharp knee to its face, then ducked beneath Titus’s downward sword swing with a quick sidestep.
Meanwhile, Gian summoned an arrow in front of him and snatched it mid-run. Using his buckler to block a desperate claw swipe, he drove the arrow into the limping wolf’s eye once, then again. Shadowy blood sprayed as the beast recoiled, but its paw slammed into Gian, knocking him back.
Gritting his teeth through the pain, Gian surged forward again.
Suddenly, two arrows streaked in from the broken window and slammed into the wolf that Oldman was fighting, striking almost the same spot on its back. 
“Damn that archer!” Titus snarled, falling back and stepping out of the window’s line of fire.
Oldman seized the opening, spinning and kicking the dying wolf with an arrow buried in its eye. Gian followed up, summoning a final arrow and plunging it deep into the wolf’s neck, finally bringing the beast down.
Titus dissolved the remaining wounded shadow wolf and bolted down one of the halls in the opposite direction.
Seeing this, Oldman grabbed a fallen sword from the floor and sprinted toward the staircase. “Stay here—I’ll help Kel!”
Gian turned toward the fleeing Titus. He summoned his black longbow and quickly loosed an arrow. It struck Titus in the right shoulder, but the man didn’t stop, vanishing around the corner.
Wincing from the pain in his abdomen, Gian retrieved his short sword and stored it in his Void Ring. He then picked up a fallen guard’s sword and, without hesitation, ran after Titus.

Upstairs on the landing, Kel swung his relic axe at Rex, forcing him back against the railing and preventing him from concentrating on the flames he tried to summon. 
This is bad, Rex thought in a panic.
Then he caught sight of Titus fleeing and something inside him broke. He dropped his machete and raised both hands in surrender.
“I surrender! Don’t kill me!” he shouted, just as Kel’s axe stopped inches from his neck.
“Don’t try anything,” Kel warned, panting heavily.
“No, of course not! Please—don’t kill me!” Rex pleaded, trembling.
At that moment, Oldman reached them.
“What are we going to do with him?” Kel asked, glancing at Oldman.
Without a word, Oldman stepped forward and thrust his sword into Rex’s chest, piercing his heart. Rex collapsed to the floor, lifeless.
Kel stared in shock and then looked at Oldman.
“That man abused a woman back in the city,” Oldman said coldly. “He deserved to die.”
He pulled the blade. “All of Ronald’s personal guards are the same, corrupt men who’ve done terrible things. That’s why they cling to him... for protection.”
Kel nodded silently, eyes dropping to the ground floor.
“…Hey, where’s Gian?”
