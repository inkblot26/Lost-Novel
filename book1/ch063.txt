Chapter 63: The Ambush Unleashed
The following day, as dusk settled over the forest, Gian crouched low with his black longbow, an arrow already nocked. Nearby, Oldman and Kel waited silently, Kel gripping his Crimson Blood Axe, Oldman listening for any sign of Tata’s return.
On the opposite side of the trail, Anni knelt with her bow alongside El, both hidden behind a massive rock.
“Auntie El, I think these guys are—” Anni began, but El cut her off.
“Not now. We’ll talk after this is over.”
Anni nodded just as Tata burst out into the open clearing between the two ambush points.
“That’s the signal,” El said, her voice low as she materialized an ice bow. “Diego and the others are approaching.”
Just as planned, they prepared to strike from both sides as the bandits passed through.
Moments later, a group of purple-robed figures appeared. At the front walked a man wearing a gold necklace and carrying a longsword. Behind him trailed a bulky figure wearing a bucket-shaped helmet with a narrow slit for his eyes. Beside them was a woman in a purple hat, and following at the rear was a plain-looking man whose wicked smile and unnaturally still eyes made him all the more unsettling.
The ambushers tensed and waited… until the bandits suddenly stopped.
“Did they spot us?” Kel whispered, lowering himself deeper into the brush.
“That shouldn’t be possible,” Oldman muttered. “We’re in a dense forest, and it’s almost dark. El and Anni are still hidden on the other side waiting for us.”
Laughter echoed through the quiet woods.
“Why don’t you come out already?” Diego called out. “No need for suspense. Let me see your faces.”
“He knows,” Kel hissed. “Now what?”
“Can you hit him from here, Gian?” Oldman asked sharply.
“Not with that bucket-headed guy in front of him,” Gian replied. “That’s got to be Kelly with the Echo of Earth. Maro said his Echo manipulates metal somehow. He'll probably stop my arrow before it even reaches Diego.” 
“Very well then!” Diego shouted. “We’ll come to you!”
In an instant, the four split apart, two charging toward Gian’s position and the other two toward El’s. 
“They need our help!” Kel growled, rising to his feet.
“Don’t—!” Oldman warned, but it was already too late.
One of the bandits, Belle, spotted him. She opened her mouth and began to sing. Her voice carried like a whisper directly into Kel’s ears. He screamed, clutching his head, dropping his Crimson Blood Axe as he collapsed to his knees.
“Gian! Kill that woman!” Oldman barked. “I’ll handle the big one.”
He stripped off his metal gauntlets and drew the Stickk relic. The armored figure barreled straight for him — Kelly, fully clad in metal armor shaped by his Echo.
I don’t know how he uses his Echo, Oldman thought, so I’ll need to remove all my metal weapons in close range.
With a flick, he threw his boot daggers to the side and sprinted to intercept.

On the far side, El stepped into the open and fired a volley of ice arrows at Diego and Gromo, who stood beside him wielding a longsword, but both men dodged effortlessly.
“Anni, stay behind me and provide support!” El shouted, transforming her bow into twin ice short-swords as Diego continued toward her. Gromo suddenly veered sideways, heading straight for Anni instead.
“Stay close!” El warned, blocking Diego’s advance. Anni loosed arrow after arrow at Gromo, each one expertly deflected by his blade.
El slashed with both her ice short swords, but Diego caught them, his hands clad in metal gauntlets tipped with sharp claws. With a sudden twist, he snapped both blades. El jumped back, but Diego didn't let up. He lunged forward, slashing at her with his clawed right hand.
Just in time, El conjured an ice dagger in her left hand, blocking the claws and pulling it away before Diego could grab it.
“Not bad, woman. You’ve got sharp reflexes,” Diego chuckled. “Most weapons would’ve snapped from the strength in these hands.”
He pressed the attack, clawing and swiping as El defended with twin ice daggers, her expression focused and calm.
Behind her, Anni dropped her bow and unsheathed her saber as Gromo reached her and brought his longsword down in a heavy arc. She deflected it to the ground with a clean parry, then countered with a slash to Gromo’s arm. He staggered from the blow. Anni spun swiftly and followed with a horizontal strike, cutting deep into the same arm.
Gromo screamed in pain as his right arm dropped to the ground, his longsword now clutched weakly in his left.
“You all deserve death for what you’ve done,” Anni said, eyes burning with determination.
Panicked and bleeding, Gromo turned and sprinted into the forest, leaving his severed arm behind. 
“Wait! Anni, don’t get too far from me!” El shouted, but Anni was already chasing after Gromo.
Diego lashed a whip from his side, catching El’s right hand and yanking her ice weapon from her grip. As he pulled her toward him and raised his claws for the kill, El formed a new ice blade in her left hand, extended it into a longsword, and slashed upward.
Diego halted his advance just in time, narrowly avoiding the strike.
“Fine. I’ll kill you first, then,” El said coldly.
The air around her shifted. A heavy pressure filled the clearing as her Echo of Gravity surged — roots, leaves, and dust pressed into the earth around her.
“You’re one dangerous woman,” Diego muttered, stepping back out of the zone of influence.
“And you’re one dangerous Wildborn,” El replied, transforming her blade into a sleek ice spear.
Diego smiled.

In the dense forest, Gian was about to move toward the bandit Belle when, without warning, Kel at his side grabbed his axe and turned to him with a hateful glare.
“I’m going to kill all of you bastards!” Kel roared as he swung his weapon at Gian.
Gian barely managed to raise the buckler strapped to his left arm, blocking the first strike.
“Kel, it’s me — Gian!” he shouted, stepping back as Kel continued to hack at him with furious strength. Each blow forced Gian to retreat, deflecting the wild attacks with increasing difficulty.
“Stop, Kel! Remember who you are!” Gian pleaded, but Kel didn’t listen. Instead, he pulled a short throwing axe from his back and pressed forward with renewed aggression.
Suddenly, the Crimson Blood Axe slashed Gian’s leg, leaving a shallow wound. Then came another, and another, each cut faster and deeper than the last.
This is bad, Gian realized. The more blood it draws, the stronger it gets. He remembered the night Kel had explained how his relic worked—a weapon that fed on blood, becoming deadlier with each drop it absorbed.
With no other choice, Gian unsheathed his short sword. The air between them rang with steel as he parried and blocked, fighting now not just to defend but to survive.

Just a short distance away, Oldman was locked in combat with Kelly. The bandit ignored the blows of Oldman’s Stickk relic, letting the strikes bounce harmlessly off his metal-plated armor as he pressed forward with heavy punches.
An uppercut whistled past Oldman’s chin as he sidestepped. He countered with a sharp bash of his arm-length Stickk to Kelly’s face, but the blow was blocked by a raised, armor-covered forearm.
When I go for his face, he always blocks... Oldman noted, pulling back and shifting into a more fluid fighting style. He struck low, high, and from unexpected angles, targeting the torso and occasionally feinting toward the helmet. Yet every time he aimed for the head, Kelly blocked it, while the body blows landed with little effect.
While analyzing his opponent’s pattern, Oldman made a misstep. The bandit caught him with a brutal kick to the side.
Oldman gritted his teeth, staggering but staying upright. I need to end this, he thought grimly, glancing sideways. Kel was fighting Gian, clearly under the influence of Belle’s Echo. 
“You’ve still got time to be distracted?” Kelly taunted. “You’re underestimating me.”
Before Oldman could react, Kelly’s metal gauntlet suddenly expanded — doubling in size mid-punch. The unexpected reach caught Oldman off guard, and the blow smashed into his face, knocking his hat to the ground and splitting his brow open.
“Nice scar you’ve got there. How about I give you another?” Kelly sneered.
With a deep, grinding sound, Kelly’s armor began to shift and grow. Plates thickened. His frame expanded. Within seconds, he towered over Oldman, who now stood chest-high to the fully transformed bandit. 
“Behold the full power of my Echo!” Kelly roared, arms spread as if to embrace the sky. “I call it Fullmetal K!” His laughter rang out, wild and fearless.
“More like Fullmetal Idiot to me,” Oldman chuckled, stepping forward, only to meet Kelly’s right hook. He ducked low just in time, narrowly avoiding the punch.
With a grunt, Oldman slammed his Stickk into the ground, then willed it to extend upward. The tip shot forward, striking Kelly’s bucket-shaped helmet right under the chin with brutal force. His head snapped back, body lifted clean off the ground before crashing down with a metallic thud.
“You just made yourself slower,” Oldman muttered, already sprinting toward Belle, ignoring Kelly as he groaned and struggled to rise.
But a shout stopped him.
“Oldman, dodge!” Gian yelled.
Oldman turned just in time to see an axe hurtling through the air. He swung his staff and deflected it with a sharp crack.
“Stand up, Big K, you stupid brute!” Belle shouted, her singing faltering for a second. Kel staggered, dazed, confusion clouding his eyes as the only echo master in the bandits resumed her song, her voice now a whisper of twisted thoughts worming their way into his mind.
Kel let out a scream and charged.
“I’ll kill you all! I’ll avenge my parents!” he roared, stabbing his left palm. Blood welled up—then floated, forming blood droplets that hovered like deadly daggers.
“Damn, this is getting out of hand,” Oldman muttered.
Kel swung his bloodied hand, launching the droplets at him. Oldman spun his Stickk in a tight circle, creating a barrier that deflected every drop.
Gian rushed in from the side, blade raised. He slashed at Kel’s back, but Kel spun and met the short sword with a Crimson Blood Axe. 
“I can’t think of anything else, Kel. I’ll just have to injure you enough to make you stop attacking us. Let’s see who’s stronger,” Gian said, eyes burning with determination.
The two clashed, weapons snapping and ringing as they blocked and parried. Every strike Gian made was aimed not to kill, but to cripple.
Gian’s gaze narrowed as Kel formed another weapon in his left hand—a vicious crescent blade of blood. 
“I’ll kill you slowly, bastard! I’ll tear you to pieces!” Kel snarled, eyes bloodshot with rage.
Kel jumped back and hurled the Blood Crescent. Gian reacted instantly, transforming his attached Morphguard into a kite shield form just in time. The blow slammed into it, forcing him backward, but he remained unharmed.
“Ahh!” Kel roared as he charged again, swinging his relic axe.
Gian braced himself. The kite shield was too heavy to move with, so he stayed rooted and raised his short sword, catching the incoming strike with a sharp clash of weapons.
Kelly caught up to Oldman within moments, and their brutal fight resumed. Oldman darted around with quick footwork, slipping past every punch, but his own strikes were useless. Each blow glanced off Kelly’s metal armor without leaving a scratch.
Belle sang softly, her Echo of Sound still holding Kel in sway. Nearby, something crept unnoticed through the tall grass. Then, without warning, it pounced—a flash of claws slit her throat.
The song ended abruptly. Belle stumbled, clutching her bleeding neck. As she dropped to her knees, her gaze landed on a small creature in the grass, dark-brown fur, no larger than a clenched fist. A driftmouse. It stared coldly into her eyes, then vanished into the undergrowth.
Kel staggered as though struck. A sharp pain split his skull as he fought to shake off the hallucinations clouding his mind. Slowly, his vision cleared. A few meters away stood Gian. Cut, bloodied, and smiling. 
“You’re a real pain, Kel. Stay here. I’ll come back for you,” Gian said, seizing the opening. He transformed the Morphguard into buckler form and summoned his black longbow. One arrow flew, striking Belle in the face. She collapsed for good.
He dismissed his longbow into the Void Ring and sprinted toward Oldman and Kelly, who were still locked in fierce combat in the distance.
Kel, finally released from the Echo’s control, swayed. Exhaustion and blood loss overwhelmed him, and he collapsed onto the ground.
Seeing Belle’s corpse, Kelly didn’t even flinch.
“Even if all of you come at me, you’ll never land a scratch on my Fullmetal K form,” he boasted.
Oldman stepped back as Gian moved up beside him, both facing the towering enemy encased in thick steel.
“I’ve got a plan. But you’ll have to use that,” Oldman murmured, leaning in to whisper in Gian’s ear. 
“Not a problem.” Gian unstrapped Morphguard and held it in his right hand, now in its small buckler form.
Kelly scoffed. “What are you two midgets gonna do? Stand there and stare? Use your blades or something!”
He strode forward. Gian immediately backed off, circling behind him, while Oldman spat on the ground and raised his middle finger at Kelly.
“Fine. I’ll crush you first. Even if that other runt summons his bow again, it won’t do a damn thing to me!” Kelly roared as he hammered punches at Oldman.
Oldman danced around them with ease, using nimble footwork to dodge and occasionally using his Stickk to redirect the blows while slowly retreating. 
“Come on! Fight me like a man!” Kelly bellowed. He brought his fist down in a brutal overhead smash. Oldman used the momentum to swing his Stickk hard at Kelly’s metal-plated knee, but it bounced off harmlessly.
Kelly laughed and swung another crushing punch at Oldman.
Oldman slipped beneath it, deliberately drawing Kelly's attention.
At that exact moment, Gian descended from above.
One clean swing.
The blade slipped through the narrow gap between Kelly's helmet and breastplate—the only place his armor couldn't protect.
For a heartbeat, nothing happened.
Then Kelly's head slid free from his shoulders and tumbled across the forest floor.
The massive suit of metal armor instantly dissolved, along with the bucket-shaped helmet, revealing the bearded man inside—lifeless.
Gian landed lightly and exhaled, standing tall with Morphguard in its Katana form.
