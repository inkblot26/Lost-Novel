Chapter 69: Shield of Unity
Early the next morning, a knock echoed through the room—soft at first, then louder and louder.
Half-awake, Kel groaned, “Okay, okay, I’m coming…” He stumbled to the door and pulled it open.
Outside stood El and Anni, already dressed and ready.
“What, you three just woke up?” Anni said, raising an eyebrow at Kel’s sleepy face before glancing inside the room. Gian and Oldman were still half-asleep, and Tata sat on the floor grooming itself, brushing its ear with one tiny foot.
Yawning, Oldman muttered, “What’s the rush?”
“First flap, first snap,” El said with a smirk, clearly amused by her own saying.
Kel frowned. “What?”
“You know—being early pays off.” El shrugged.
“Never mind her,” Anni interrupted, tugging El by the arm. “Just get ready. We’ll wait in the courtyard.”
A short while later, the group—with Tata trotting ahead—set out from Takamine Village. Their path led north, toward the climbable part of Fallen Cliff near the Aethelgard border.
Days passed as they journeyed across rugged trails and wild terrain. Along the way, they crossed paths with many wildborns—some they avoided, but most they fought, spurred on by Anni’s restless eagerness for battle.

After more than two weeks of travel, the group made camp under the shadow of trees. The fire crackled, sending sparks spiraling into the night sky, while the rich smell of roasted meat hung heavy in the cool evening air.
Kel chewed slowly, breaking the quiet.
“Hey… isn’t it dangerous, heading this far north? The last report said a horde of wildborn—led by Jorbugo—was battling near the outposts of Tillara City and Ravenscar City.” 
“Fine by me,” Anni said with a grin, pushing to her feet. “The more wildborn, the better.” She shot a glance at Gian. “Hey, you’ve rested long enough. Let’s spar.”
Gian, who had been sharpening his shortsword, stood without hesitation and stepped into an open space with her.
Oldman sighed, shaking his head. “First of all, it’s Jorgumo, not Jorbugo. Second, we don’t even know for sure she’s leading the horde—all we’ve got is Diego’s word that she was on the move. And third…” He gestured toward the darkened tree line surrounding them. “El and I believe the fighting is in Doom Forest, below Fallen Cliff. The hordes seldom attack this high up. And besides—Tata’s always on guard. If it senses a swarm nearby, we’ll change course immediately.”
“Alright, alright,” Kel muttered. “Just saying we should be careful. But really, how do those two have the energy to spar this late? I just want to sleep.” He yawned and slumped near the fire.
“You sleep first, kiddo,” El said, patting his shoulder. “You’ve earned it, cooking for us. I’ll take first watch.”
She stood, but froze when Oldman suddenly rose to his feet, face turning grim.
“What is it?” El asked sharply, signaling Anni and Gian to stop their sparring.
Oldman’s gaze dropped to Tata, the small driftmouse twitching its whiskers against the ground.
“Tata says footsteps. Someone’s coming this way.”
The group quickly armed themselves, tense and silent, waiting to see who—or what—would emerge from the darkness.
In a few moments, five soldiers in green military armor uniforms emerged from the dark, the crest of the Lanthea Kingdom gleaming faintly on their shoulders.
“Hey—it’s Sergeant Thompson and the others!” Kel said in relief, lowering his weapon as the squad approached.
“Didn’t expect to run into you all the way out here.” Thompson gave a tired but warm smile. He gestured to two new faces in his squad, who immediately moved to check the camp perimeter. “Glad to see you all alive.”
“How’ve you been holding up?” Private Jimmy asked, summoning two shadowy rabbits that bounded after the scouts to reinforce their patrol.
Private Jessica grinned as her gaze drifted toward El and Anni. “Well, well… Great Kel, you’ve picked up some new companions. And beautiful ones, too. I’m jealous.”
Kel’s ears turned red as he scratched his cheek, pretending not to hear her.
The two groups settled near the fire, swapping stories of their journeys since parting ways. After a while, Thompson exhaled heavily, shaking his head.
“Didn’t expect you to run into an Ascendant wildborn. Sorry you had to go through that.”
“Yeah… and you still didn’t find what you were after,” Private Jimmy added quietly.
“I really wanted to see that Private Joey,” Kel muttered. “Still his fault.”
“At least we met El and Anni,” Oldman said, nodding toward the two women. “We share the same goal.”
El, who had been quiet, finally spoke up. “Do you know who’s leading the wildborn attacks? And what’s the situation now?”
Thompson’s expression grew serious. “It’s true—an Ascendant named Jorgumo is leading the horde. Pathfinders from both the Lanthea and Aethelgard Kingdom confirmed it. But just eight days ago, the combined forces from the two neighboring outposts pushed them back. The horde was defeated—losses were heavy, but acceptable.”
Kel let out a long breath of relief. “Good. I was worried what might happen if those outposts fell.”
“There’s no need to worry,” Oldman said, folding his arms. “Do you know why humanity has survived this long?”
“Because of the Verdant Ring,” Kel answered quickly. “That chain of cities and outposts. It keeps the hordes out.” Gian and Anni nodded silently in agreement.
El chuckled, and Oldman raised a hand to hush her.
“That’s part of it, yes. The Verdant Ring is important—it’s our shield. But that alone isn’t why humanity thrives. The true reason is people. Strong humans. Echo wielders at the Weaver level. People like your idol Kel —the Pathfinder A26.”
Thompson leaned forward. “Tell me, what’s the basic requirement to be a captain in the military?”
“Having at least one Verdant Echo in Master level,” Anni replied proudly. “Like Auntie El.”
Jessica’s eyes widened. “Wait—you’re an Echo Master?” She looked at El with open admiration. El only smiled in response.
“Correct,” Oldman said with a nod. “Now—what about generals?”
Silence stretched for a moment. “They’re Echo Weavers,” Gian realized aloud.
“Exactly. Each city has at least one active general. And that doesn’t even include others in military who remain hidden or those who are retired. Then you’ve got other powerful organizations like the Falcon Order.”
Anni’s eyes lit with wonder. “So you’re saying… there are countless Echo Weavers out there?”
Oldman chuckled. “No, not countless. Only a few. But they’re humanity’s greatest strength. On the other side, in the Unknown, the wildborn have their own powerful Ascendants—monsters equal to Weavers.”
“But unlike us,” Thompson added, “they’re not united.”
“Exactly,” Oldman said. “They’re powerful, but selfish. Territorial. Unwilling to bow to anyone weaker—or even equal. Humans may fight among themselves, but when it matters, the higher ranks can and will join hands. That unity—that’s our greatest advantage.”
Kel, who had been quiet for some time, finally raised a hand. “So… you’re saying if someone strong enough appeared in the Unknown, the other wildborn would follow them?”
The question silenced the firelit circle. Even El’s smile faded. Oldman and Thompson exchanged a glance.
“Well…” Oldman admitted slowly, “theoretically, yes. By their nature, the wildborn follow strength.”
Thompson folded his arms and snorted. “But that’s impossible. Even if one did rise up, it would be injured after every fight, too weakened to challenge the next. No wildborn could ever rule them all.”
The fire crackled, and for a moment, no one spoke.
Breaking the silence, Thompson continued,
“Anyway, Jorgumo is fleeing deeper into the Unknown. From what I’ve gathered, some Pathfinders are already in pursuit, trying to finish it off. Just north of here—a few days’ travel—there’s a temporary military camp where the combined forces of both cities are stationed.”
“Yeah,” Private Jimmy added. “Multiple units have been dispatched to scout Doom Forest and Fallen Cliff and make sure Jorgumo doesn’t circle back.”
Oldman nodded thoughtfully. “Makes sense. From Fallen Cliff, you’ve got the height advantage. Perfect spot to establish a camp.”
“Tomorrow we’ll head back there, ending our shift.” Private Jessica said, before glancing at her sergeant. “Maybe they can come with us, sir?”
“Of course,” Thompson replied with a grin. “I was about to suggest the same thing. The more the merrier. Besides, with your strength, it’ll only benefit us.”
“Well, we can’t exactly say no to a sergeant,” Oldman said with a small smile. The rest of the group nodded in agreement.
“Settled then,” Thompson declared. “Tomorrow morning, Private Donny and four more from my unit will meet us, and we’ll all return together.”
With that, the two groups settled down for the night, taking turns on watch in pairs.

It took them four days to finally reach the temporary camp. From there, with the emerald token bestowed by Highlady Rose as a mark of recognition—and with a letter of endorsement from Sergeant Thompson, whose family held great prominence in Lanthea’s military—the group was granted permission to join a returning unit of soldiers from Ravenscar City. Together, they descended the steep paths of Fallen Cliff and marched alongside the column toward the Ravenscar outposts.
Nearly three more weeks passed before they arrived at Outpost Three of Ravenscar City. From a distance, its imposing strength was undeniable. Massive stone walls rose high, reinforced with iron braces—weathered by years of battle yet unbroken. Banners flew proudly above them, each bearing the sigil of a black iron castle set within a blazing orange triangular crest. Watchtowers crowned the battlements, torches flickering even in daylight, while heavy ballistae loomed at key points along the ramparts. Soldiers patrolled with rigid precision, their orange military armor gleaming in the sun like a living chain of steel. The steady rhythm of their boots echoed against the walls, a sound that carried the weight of discipline and power.
“At last, we’re back,” Kel grinned as they approached the gate. 
“I just want to sleep on a real bed,” Oldman yawned, exhausted from the long journey. On his shoulder, Tata squeaked in agreement.
Anni, though she said nothing, couldn’t hide the excitement in her eyes as for the first time she is stepping out of the Unknown. 
“It’s been so long since I returned. I wonder how much it has changed.” El said stretching her arms. 
“Well, I can’t tell you much since I’ve stayed in Tillara after retiring from my mercenary days.” Oldman said
Kel leaned closer to Oldman and whispered, “Is it true that women here are more beautiful and open than in our kingdom?”
El, with her keen ears, heard it and laughed. “From my previous experience Kel that is true but there is only one thing that matter here”
Kel looks at her curiously
“Strength” she added “Everyone respects power here. If you have that I assure you women will flock around you”
“Yeah that’s one thing that will never change this is the strongest kingdom after all” Oldman added
Gian stopped walking and glanced back toward the Unknown.
Over the past few weeks, he had experienced just how harsh it was out there—danger lurking at every step, never giving him a moment of peace. But it had changed him. It had made him stronger.
Now, as he turned forward once more, his resolve only deepened as he stepped toward what lay ahead with the same goal as he had step into the Unknown.
I’ll find you… no matter what.
