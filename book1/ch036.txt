Chapter 36: The Threefold Assault
At midnight, beneath a cold full moon, Sam and four others crept through dew-soaked bushes toward the enemy base camp, its low tents and watchfires flickering in the clearing ahead. Activating his Echo of Sound, he masked their presence while ten more allies waited in the distance, surrounding the camp and watching for his signal.
“Get down,” Sam whispered to Leo as a patrolling soldier passed their hiding spot. The moment the soldier turned away, Sam extended the reach of his Echo and drove a blade into the man’s back, dragging the body into the bushes.
“We need to get closer,” Leo murmured. “Near the entrance to their sleeping quarters. That way I can maximize the effect of my Echo.”
Moving in the shadows, they slipped past the guards, silently taking down a few more as they approached the barracks. Once in range of the wooden barracks clustered near the center of camp, they began their sweep.
They moved from room to room, keeping to the dark corners. In each room, Leo manifested red-patterned fungi that released spores. A faint yellow haze drifted over the sleeping soldiers, deepening their slumber until their breathing slowed to a steady rhythm. The process was slow and careful, one room at a time, leaving a trail of undisturbed silence behind them. 
“This is the last one,” Sam muttered, standing in the doorway of the final room. He glanced at Leo, who was panting from the strain of overusing his Echo.
“Let’s move,” Sam ordered. “We still need to deal with the patrols outside.”
Beyond the camp, Zoe and Drew, the two fighters assigned to seal the camp, waited silently on opposite flanks. The moment a torch signal flickered at the main entrance, they acted.
“Let’s go!” Zoe shouted, and she and Drew raised their hands simultaneously. Thick walls of mud surged upward with a grinding roar, tearing through grass and soil as they sealed the camp’s exits on both flanks.
Chaos erupted. The remaining patrols were swiftly cut down in the confusion, and within minutes, the camp was completely enclosed by earthen walls.
“Why don’t we just kill them all?” Zoe asked, slumping to the ground, drained from using her Echo.
“We can’t,” Sam replied, breathing heavily. “Our mission is to assassinate Highlord Richard, not massacre soldiers who are just following orders.”
“Besides,” Leo added, wiping sweat from his brow, “my Echo isn’t perfect. If we cause too much noise or injury, they’ll wake up.”
“And it’s just Richard now, Sir Sam,” Leo said with a small grin.
Sam laughed. “You're right. After tonight, Lady Rose will be the Highlord.”
Then he turned to one of the nearby fighters. “Send the signal-bird to the assault team. Let them know the camp is sealed. The rest of us stay here and keep watch, just in case.”

A short distance from the mansion, half-veiled by towering pines at the edge of the estate grounds, Ezra approached Lady Rose. Her scale armor caught the moonlight as she delivered the report.
“Lady Rose, we received the message. Sam and the others have succeeded,” Ezra said.
“Good,” Lady Rose replied, her hand resting firmly on the hilt of her rapier. “Relay the message to Sir Henry’s group on the other side.”
“Yes, my lady.” Ezra gave a quick nod and ran off into the shadows.
Lady Rose studied the Highlord’s mansion rising pale and imposing beyond the torchlit courtyard. Balconies and tall windows loomed over the grounds like watchful eyes.
Then she glanced at the fighters assembled behind her.
With the mercenaries we hired, we now number around forty. The guards inside are only thirty. We'll strike from the front and the rear, surrounding them in a pincer movement.
A few minutes later, Ezra returned. “The message has been delivered, my lady.”
“Excellent. There’s no need to stay silent any longer,” Lady Rose said. “Dan, the mansion’s head guard, will sense us approaching with his Echo of Perception as soon as we get close.”
She faced her fighters and raised her voice.
“This is it. Tonight, we take back this city. Our goal is the head of my dear brother, the man who has caused pain and suffering to all of you.”
She drew her rapier, her eyes burning with determination.
“Attack!” Lady Rose commanded.

Outside the city, in the western outskirts, a private stone mansion stood concealed within a dense stretch of forest, its darkened windows barely visible through the trees. Behind a tall tree near the edge of the clearing, Oldman, Gian, Kel, Michael, and three other fighters crouched in silence.
“Is this the place?” Gian asked, eyes scanning the structure.
“Yes. He’s already inside,” Oldman replied.
“Then why are we waiting?” Kel asked, tightening the grip on his weapon.
“His assistant and the girls he plans to deliver haven’t arrived yet. We need to rescue them first. Once the carriage shows up, we strike,” Oldman explained.
“I’ll stay out here with the girls once we rescue them,” Michael added. “The rest of you can push into the mansion.”
Everyone nodded in agreement.
Before long, the low rumble of wheels broke the silence. A black carriage appeared, rolling slowly toward the mansion.
“There it is,” Kel said.
“I’ll take the driver,” Michael whispered. “Gian, you hit the guard waiting by the entrance of the mansion.”
“Got it,” Gian replied, readying his longbow.
“Remember, our priority is to rescue the girls and capture Ronald alive,” Oldman reminded them. “Also, keep in mind that we’re not completely sure how accurate our intel is. The number of guards inside could be different.”
The carriage came to a stop.
“It’s here,” Kel muttered as the door began to open and a short man stepped out.
Two arrows flew through the air—one conjured by Michael's Echo, the other a fine black arrow from Gian's bow. Both found their mark. 
“Go!” Oldman shouted as the team rushed toward the carriage.
“Enemy attack!” Tavin yelled as he sprinted from the carriage toward the mansion. 
A brass alarm bell clanged violently from inside, and guards burst from the front door, steel flashing in the torchlight. From inside the carriage, terrified screams of young girls echoed into the night.
“You're not going anywhere,” Michael muttered, loosing another arrow alongside Gian’s.
But just before the arrows could strike, Tavin’s arm twisted grotesquely, transforming into a large, slippery tentacle that deflected the projectiles. Wincing in pain, he staggered through the mansion doors and shouted, “Kill them!” as the guards surged forward.
Five swordsmen and a bowman. Kel sized them up quickly as they charged in.
Kel, Oldman, and the three fighters clashed with the swordsmen, steel ringing through the trees. Meanwhile, Michael exchanged arrows with the enemy archer, giving Gian the chance to sprint toward the carriage.
Inside, six frightened girls huddled together, trembling and crying.
“It’s okay. We’re here to save you,” Gian said gently, offering his hand. “Come with me. We'll run to that big tree. Stay close, and don't get separated. Understand?”
From the corner of his eye, he saw the enemy bowman collapse, pinned to a pillar by one of Michael’s arrows. The fight still raged near the mansion, but the path to safety was momentarily clear.
The girls nodded nervously, clutching each other.
“Okay, let’s go!” Gian shouted.
With that, they dashed from the carriage. One girl clung to him, trembling. Another refused to let go of her friend’s hand, even as they ran, racing toward Michael and the shelter of the forest.

Inside the mansion…
“Tavin, what’s happening?!” Ronald shouted at his short assistant, who was clutching his bleeding hand as it returned to its normal shape.
“I-I don’t know, my lord,” Tavin stammered, wincing from the pain. “They came out of nowhere.”
“Lord Ronald, let me and Rex go outside and clean them up,” said Titus, Ronald’s head personal guard, gripping his longsword tightly.
“No!” Ronald snapped, panic in his voice. “You two have Echoes. Stay with me no matter what.”
“You two, get down there and guard the main door. If they force their way in, stop them. Whatever it takes,” he added to the other two guards.
“Yes, sir!” they replied, quickly running out the door.
“Why is this happening?” Ronald muttered, pacing in a panic.
“Sir,” Titus said firmly, “at least let Rex stand guard on the balcony hallway outside this room. From there, he can watch the main entrance below and strike if the enemy breaks in.”
Ronald hesitated, then finally nodded. “Fine. Just keep them away from me.”
“Go!” Titus ordered.
“Yes, sir,” Rex said, nodding. He exited the room, his Echo of Fire already sparking in his hands as he took position in the hallway.

Outside, Gian met up with Michael.
“I'll leave them to you,” Gian said as the black longbow in his hand vanished, and a short sword and a worn buckler materialized in front of him, summoned from his Void Ring.
Gian strapped the buckler to his left arm, glancing toward the ongoing fight. He saw Kel sever the hand of the last standing guard, while Oldman swiftly followed up with a finishing slash.
One dead, one injured, Gian thought grimly, noticing one of their fighters clutching his side, an arrow lodged deep in his ribs.
“I’m heading in,” Gian said, approaching Kel.
“Be careful,” Michael called after him.
“You can stay here,” Oldman told the wounded fighter gently. Then he turned to the small driftmouse on his shoulder. “Tata, you too. It’s dangerous inside.”
The driftmouse squeaked in protest but didn’t follow.
“Everyone, let’s move,” said the remaining fighter, leading the way while Gian, Kel, and Oldman followed close behind.
As they opened the main door and stepped inside the mansion, a sudden wave of fire roared down the corridor toward them, engulfing the fighter at the front. Heat slammed into their faces as flames devoured the narrow entrance hall.
Before it could reach Gian and Kel, who were just behind him, the plain circular buckler on Gian’s arm transformed—unfolding like metal petals into a broad, elongated kite shield. It flared out just in time, shielding them both as they stumbled back outside, the flames crashing against the now-expanded shield.
The fire continued to pour over them, but the kite shield held firm.
“What was that?!” Kel shouted from behind the shield.
“I don’t know!” Gian replied, eyes wide as he tried to process what just happened.
“What do you mean you don’t know?!” Kel asked, crouching behind Gian.
“I said I don’t know! I just instinctively raised the buckler. I thought we needed cover when I saw the flames coming at us!” Gian snapped as the flames finally died down.
“Talk later. Go, now!” Oldman shouted from the rear, taking advantage of the lull to push them forward.
They dashed through the doorway, heading into the mansion.
