Chapter 22: Before the dark
“Sir, Ryan and John still aren’t back. I think I should go look for them,” Edmund said. The two guards, originally assigned to the second wagon, were overdue from their patrol rotation.
Torren’s eyes narrowed. “Alright. Thomas, go with him, and report back immediately.” His tone carried the weight of command, but beneath it lingered unease. 
“What’s going on?” Kel asked, rubbing his eyes as he sat up from his afternoon nap.
“The patrol hasn’t returned,” Gian replied, already scanning the underbrush. His hand rested on the hilt of his weathered sword.
Kel’s voice dropped. “You don’t think…?”
“I hope not. But be ready,” Gian muttered, tightening his grip.
The minutes stretched thin with silence. The last of the daylight thinned between the trees, bleeding from gold to bruised violet. The forest grew quieter—not peaceful, but expectant.
Then Edmund and Thomas reappeared, their faces pale, steps hurried.
"Sir, this is bad," Thomas reported. "We found a trail of blood, but no sign of the men. Edmund tried listening for them with his Echo, but there’s nothing."
Torren’s gaze swept over the darkening treeline. “We need to move, Lord Henry.”
"It’s going to be dark soon. Is that a good idea?" Lord Henry asked cautiously.
"I believe it’s following us, sir. We should put some distance between us just to be safe," Torren replied.
Henry looked skyward, where dusk was already swallowing the light. “If it truly is stalking us, we must be prepared. But moving on in the dark may be just as dangerous.”
Torren considered, then gave a sharp nod. “You’re right. Thomas, tighten the camp’s formation. No scouting beyond sight. Three guards on rotation at all times, set in a triangle pattern. Everyone else, stay close to the fire.”
Just as the group began preparing, Edmund froze. His eyes widened as he pressed a hand firmly to the ground, listening.
“Sir, something’s coming! Fast!” he shouted.
Torren’s head snapped toward him. “Everyone, on guard!” He strode immediately to Lord Henry’s side, warhammer sliding into his grip.
The warning came a heartbeat too late. A blur of motion burst from the treeline—silent, swift, claws gleaming in the fading light. It shot straight for Lady Clara.
“Clara!” Lady Evangeline screamed.
Thomas reacted first, shoving Clara aside as his blade caught the blow, steel shrieking against claw. The creature rebounded, vanishing into the branches above.
The dim sunset filtered through the canopy, giving Gian a clear glimpse of it—an Ascendant wildborn, a Blinkclaw. Its hungry, glowing yellow eyes studied them with cruel amusement. Compact yet terrifying, it was built to kill—razor-sharp claws and blur-fast bursts of speed that could shred flesh or slip through weak armor with ease.
Lady Evangeline rushed to her daughter, clutching her tightly as Clara stumbled back to her feet.
Torren and Lord Henry exchanged a brief look—a wordless understanding forged from respect and long familiarity. Then Torren barked his orders: “Thomas, with me! Michael, cover us from range! The second one may be circling. Everyone else, stay sharp!” 
“Can you match its speed?” Torren asked, already moving. 
Thomas tightened his grip on his sword. “I can, sir, if I stay focused.”
“Good. Drive it toward me.”
With a roar, Torren swung his warhammer into the trunk where the beast perched, splintering bark. Michael’s arrow whistled past, forcing the Ascendant to leap again. Mid-flight, Thomas thrust his hand upward, a burst of wind erupting from his palm. The force caught the creature, hurling it down hard into the dirt.
“We can take it!” Kel shouted, eyes wide with excitement. 
“Only if it’s alone,” Baldwin replied, eyes sweeping the forest.
“Edmund, stay close to my family,” Lord Henry said, his tone heavier than usual. He gripped the dark staff in his hand. "If the second one appears, I’ll engage it with Gian, Kel, and Baldwin. Michael, shoot when you see a chance."
“Sir! The other one—it’s here!” Edmund shouted.
A blur streaked out of the treeline. Baldwin barely had time to raise his buckler before the impact rattled his arm. Gian and Kel swung in unison, but their blades cut nothing but air—the Blinkclaw had already sidestepped, landing as lightly as falling ash.
Without pause, it launched again. Baldwin tried to counter with a swing of his sword, but the creature was too fast. Its jaws clamped onto his arm, teeth sinking deep, nearly tearing it off. Blood sprayed across the clearing as Baldwin screamed in agony.
Lord Henry’s staff cut through the air. At first, the swing seemed off target, then the weapon snapped longer mid-motion, its carved length extending like living wood. The strike smashed into the Blinkclaw’s side, hurling it back and ending the frenzy.
“Baldwin! Are you alright?” Kel shouted, sprinting to him.
“I’m fine, just peachy,” Baldwin muttered through clenched teeth, clutching the torn, bleeding arm with a shaky grin. 
“Kel, eyes forward! Baldwin, fall back and bind that wound. Stop the bleeding!” Gian barked, never looking away from the predator as it rose again. His grip tightened. We can’t afford distractions now. I just hope Sir Torren and Thomas can handle the other one… 
The Blinkclaw’s head snapped toward the rear. Its glowing eyes fixed on Evangeline.
Lord Henry swung his staff downward, the weapon extending mid-swing toward the beast, but it slipped aside and darted low. Gian’s sword cut after it, steel hissing through the air, missing by a hair.
Then it feinted. With a sudden shift of speed, it veered toward the unconscious figure sprawled near Michael.
Damn—it was after David all along. Edmund’s chest tightened as his legs refused to move. If he rushed in, he’d leave Evangeline and the children unprotected… and he couldn’t take that risk.
Michael reacted first. His summoned shortbow flashed as he loosed two arrows in rapid succession. Both thudded uselessly into the dirt. The Blinkclaw was already there. One claw swept across David’s throat, swift and merciless.
David’s body convulsed once, then stilled.
Edmund flinched, his grip tightening around his weapon. “I’m sorry, David,” he whispered so softly it was barely a breath, yet he didn’t move. Not even an inch.
“Ah!” Cedric screamed, bolting forward in panic, only to be yanked back by his mother.
“Father, I can help!” Clara cried desperately, water gathering in her palms, only to collapse into mist before it took form. 
“No. Protect your brother. Stay by your mother,” Lord Henry commanded, voice iron. He stepped past Edmund, planting himself between his family and the monster.
“Sir, it’s too fast— I can’t hit it!” Michael shouted, falling back toward them.
 “Keep firing,” Lord Henry ordered coldly, his gaze lingering on David’s lifeless form. “Even if you miss, we can restrict its movement.”
Kel gritted his teeth beside Gian. “Damn it… how do we fight something that fast?”
Suddenly, the ground shuddered.
Lord Henry slammed his staff into the earth. Cracks splintered outward as molten light bled from beneath. In a flash, a lance of hardened magma erupted from the soil, stabbing toward the Blinkclaw. It twisted away just in time, retreating with a snarl.
So… Lord Henry bears the Rare Echo of Magma, Gian realized, his eyes locked on the man’s back.

The other battle had pushed deeper into the forest, where shadows thickened beneath the tangled canopy.
“Sir… are you sure it’s wise for us to be this far from camp?” Thomas panted, landing beside Torren during a brief lull. Torren’s skin shimmered with silver, his Echo hardening his body like living steel. Overhead, the Blinkclaw’s yellow eyes flashed once before it leapt into the branches and vanished. 
“I know what you mean,” Torren replied, gaze never leaving the treetops. “The other one already reached the camp. That’s why we have to keep them apart. Lord Henry and the others can manage. This one—we finish here.”
Without hesitation, he charged. Drawing on the Echo of Strength, Torren swung his warhammer into a tree’s base. The trunk splintered and toppled with a deafening crash, shaking loose birds and leaves.
The Blinkclaw launched off the falling timber, a streak of black and brown. Its claws raked across Torren’s chest with a metallic shriek, only to recoil as if striking steel.
With my Echo of Metal Skin, I can handle its claws. It only stings a little, Torren thought, steadying his stance. And with Thomas keeping up thanks to his Echo of Wind… we just need to kill this thing fast.

Back at the camp, the Blinkclaw’s shape became harder to track, its dark hide melting into the gathering night. Lord Henry unleashed lance after lance of hardened magma, each streak searing through the air with a hiss and crash, while Michael loosed a steady rain of arrows. Yet the Blinkclaw was relentless—slipping between each strike with taunting grace, its movements too sharp, too quick.
When it lunged toward Lord Henry, Kel stepped in, swinging his Crimson Blood Axe, while Gian flanked, his blade sweeping in with perfect timing. Steel found its mark, but the creature’s claws snapped shut around the edge of Gian’s sword with a ringing metallic shriek. In an instant, it pushed off, springing back to safety once more.
“Damn, we almost had it,” Kel growled, tightening his grip on his axe.
“It’s fast. Cautious,” Edmund muttered, his gaze locked on the beast as it prowled around them, silent and calculating, a predator measuring its kill. 
“My magma lance can’t keep up with its speed,” Lord Henry admitted, lowering his staff for a moment. “Can you hold it off for a minute?”
“We can manage it, sir,” Michael said, his summoned short bow shimmering as another arrow form  itself into place.
Lord Henry reached into his sling bag and withdrew a tightly rolled red scroll. The parchment seemed to thrum faintly in his hand, as though heat pulsed from within.
What’s that? Gian wondered, eyes narrowing, gaze locked on the scroll as the Blinkclaw’s yellow eyes glinted from the treeline.
