Chapter 65: Beware What Hides
El finally had a moment to breathe, the tension in her shoulders easing as the forest fell into a brief, uneasy silence. She drove her massive ice glaive into the ground and pressed a hand to her bleeding leg. Frost spread rapidly across the wound, sealing torn flesh in a thin crystalline layer and slowing the steady flow of blood.
“Okay… that’s better. Round two then.” Her gaze hardened as she yanked the glaive free, eyes fixed on the glowing, deer-like wildborn. “Don’t think I’ll let you off for barging into my fight.”
She sprinted forward. But halfway into the charge, the Glimstep vanished in a flash of light, blinking to her right and keeping distance. El immediately shifted course, refusing to let go. Again she closed the gap—boots tearing through grass—and again the creature blinked away in a flash of pale light, always just out of reach, calmly charging another blast between its antlers.
While El engaged it, Gian stood silent and vigilant, eyes scanning the trees for any sign of Diego.
That’s when he saw it — a blur like a whip flying at his face. He barely raised his buckler in time sending a jolt through his arm and hurling him flat onto his back.
“Gian!” El shouted, seeing him hit the ground.
Gian raised a hand. “I’m fine! Focus on that Ascendant!”
From the gloom, Diego stepped into sight — a long-limbed chameleon-like reptile, skin shifting through colors, a grin stretching over his toothy mouth as his whip-tongue dangled.
“I was planning to kill her first… but since another ascendant joined the party, I’ll let them have their duel,” Diego said in a deep, unsettling voice. “Now then, who are you? You don’t look military.”
Slowly, he crept forward, each step pressing deep, three-toed impressions into the damp grass under the weight of his heavy frame.
“Villagers from some lesser village we raided? Adventurers hoping to collect bandit bounties?” he guessed aloud.
El kept chasing the Glimstep but called over her shoulder, “Watch his grip. He can snap weapons clean in half! Just stall until I’m done here!”
Diego chuckled, watching her go. “She thinks she can kill an Ascendant that easily? As if I’d just stand here and wait for her.”
Gian pushed himself upright, stored the short sword in Void Ring, and shifted his buckler to his right hand.
Diego’s eyes narrowed. “A storage relic. Nice. So, boy — who are you really?”
Gian didn’t flinch. “Nobody. Just here to avenge everyone you slaughtered.”
The Morphguard, still in buckler mode, folded its metal plates inward like petals before reshaping into a sleek, 60-centimeter curved blade.
Katana mode.
Diego stopped, fascinated. “Now that’s a relic… I want it.”
“Then come and take it,” Gian growled, launching forward. He brought the katana up in a vicious diagonal slash. Diego raised his claws, only to feel the razor edge bite deep.
Blood spattered as the blade sliced clean through his defenses.
Diego leapt back, a wide grin splitting his face.
“Nice blade.”
Gian’s razor-edged katana clashed again and again against Diego’s monstrous claws, the wildborn snapping at him with savage swipes and occasional lashes of its whip-like tongue. Steel rang sharply with every clash, sparks bursting in brief flashes as blade met claw in the tight, close-quarters melee.

Not far away, the Glimstep finally finished charging its attack. It released another distorted white orb toward El.
She smirked and braced herself.
As the orb reached her, she swung her massive ice glaive with all her strength, striking it along the side. With a strained grunt, she forced the glaive against the orb’s momentum, redirecting it mid-flight, sending it hurtling back toward its caster.
The Glimstep blinked in surprise, vanishing in a flash of light just in time to evade the attack. But El had already anticipated the blink—her glaive was hurtling toward its reappearance point the instant the creature vanished.
“Got you,” she hissed as the glaive pierced into the creature’s flank.
The Ascendant growled in pain and fled, its wound spilling glowing dust that drifted in the air before settling along its path.
I’ll finish you later, El thought, turning toward Gian’s duel. She broke into a sprint, racing back to join the fight.
El formed an ice bow and loosed a volley of frozen arrows toward Diego. One struck his long left forelimb. With a hiss, he leapt backward, blending into the surroundings. But Gian spotted the ice arrow still sticking out of his camouflaged body, and with a sharp cry, he swung his katana toward that general direction. There was a pained grunt, followed by a spray of green blood. Diego ripped the arrow free with a hiss and melted back into the trees, his body blending seamlessly into bark and shadow until he vanished from sight.
El hurried to Gian’s side. He was bleeding from multiple cuts — a gash across his cheek, a deeper wound on his left arm, more on his knee and along his torso.
“Sit down. You’re hurt,” El said firmly, catching him under the shoulder.
“I’m fine. We need to finish him off,” Gian growled through the pain.
“We will, after we stop your bleeding first. Do you have anything in that storage relic?”
Gian nodded and summoned a Lileaf and a small first-aid kit from his Void Ring. He pressed the healing leaf to the gash along his arm while El wrapped his bleeding knee and torso in a bandage.
“I’ll go after Diego. He can’t have gotten far with that cut you gave him,” El said, rising to her feet.
Gian pushed himself up. “I’m coming too.”
She gave him a stern look, then nodded. “Fine. Stay close.”
“Where’s Anni?” he asked as they began moving.
“Somewhere nearby.” El pulled a small tear-shaped glass vial from her pocket, more than half-filled with dark-blue liquid. “As long as she doesn’t run into an Ascendant, she’ll manage.”
Noticing Gian’s curious look, she added, “This is an Echotech called a Vital Vial. It reflects a person’s condition in real time. She’s still safe, so don’t worry. I’ll explain how it works later if you’re interested. Now let’s move.”
Gian nodded grimly and followed.
Suddenly, something rustled in the grass ahead. A small shape burst out and landed on El’s shoulder. 
“Hey, little guy. Where’ve you been?” El asked, recognizing Tata.
The driftmouse squeaked rapidly, nodding its tiny head toward a direction in the trees.
“Got it. So that way.”
She and Gian moved forward again, Tata perched on her shoulder, twitching and glancing sharply left and right as they advanced.
After a while, El stopped.
“You two should leave me behind. You’ll move faster without me,” Gian admitted, realizing his wounds were still bad. His arm was only half-healed, every step slowing them down. 
He hated sitting still. Hated the weakness in his limbs, the warmth of blood slipping away. But more than anything, he hated slowing her down. 
“Don’t worry. I’ll kill him. Just stay here. I’ll come back for you,” El said firmly before sprinting into the darkness.

Minutes passed. Gian finally felt the bleeding in his left arm stop. He didn’t use his remaining two Lileafs since the rest of his wounds were manageable and not life-threatening.
Out of the corner of his eye, he spotted a silhouette approaching from the left. As it drew closer, he realized it was Anni, clutching her side with a leaf dressing wrapped around her torso. She staggered and collapsed against a tree when she saw Gian.
He stood, using his katana for support, and limped toward her.
Relief flickered across her face—too quickly, too perfectly. 
“Thank goodness you’re alive. Can you help me? Where are the others?” she asked, scanning the area anxiously.
“What happened to you?” Gian asked, not moving to assist.
“That bandit is dead. I beat him, but he wounded me badly during the fight. I only survived because I outsmarted him,” Anni said, trying to push herself upright. 
“How did you find me?” Gian pressed.
“I didn’t. I just got lucky and saw you while I was walking,” she answered with a bright smile.
Gian stepped closer, his grip tightening slightly on his katana. “I see… let me help you.” Without another word, he drove his katana forward in a sudden, decisive thrust. “Anni” caught the blade just before it reached her heart.
She smiled. “What gave it away?”
“I don’t know her well,” Gian said coldly, “but I’m sure she’s not weak enough to ask for help in that condition.” He leaned his weight into the sword, trying to force the blade through.
“Too bad,” the figure sighed, and the face shifted, transforming into Diego’s reptilian features. “I didn’t get the chance to study her more.”
Diego suddenly lashed out with a powerful kick to Gian’s chest, the force sending him skidding backward across the ground, but Gian held onto his katana as he hit the ground. Diego rose slowly, and little by little his reptilian body began to blend into the surroundings until he vanished entirely from sight.
Gian planted his katana in the dirt and used it to push himself upright, scanning the trees around him.
“I’m more injured than you, why don’t you show yourself?”
A voice drifted from the dense foliage.
“You’re not fooling me, Gian.”
Silence followed. Then, through the trees, Gian saw El sprinting toward him with Tata clinging to her shoulder.
“You alright? This little guy kept tugging me, I figured he wanted me to come back here.” El slowed when she saw Gian’s tense, alert posture.
Her eyes flickered to the ground, barely noticeable streaks of green blood on the grass. She leaned closer and whispered, “So he was here… I’ll draw him out. But my Echo will affect you too.”
Gian only nodded, determination etched across his face. Tata hopped down and disappeared into the grass, sensing the coming clash.
Suddenly, El activated her Echo of Gravity. The air seemed to collapse downward as the gravity field intensified, pressing heavily against everything within its range. Gian stabbed his katana into the earth, using it as a brace while the pressure slammed down on him. A moment later, something fell from a tree branch nearby with a heavy thud.
“Now, attack!” El shouted. She released the gravity field and conjured two ice swords in a flash, striking at Diego as he staggered into view. Though surprised, he still managed to raise his claws, meeting both icy blades even as his body flickered in and out of camouflage. 
“You’re not going anywhere,” Gian growled, following after El with a wide horizontal slash. Diego deflected El’s ice sword and batted it toward Gian’s incoming strike, halting his momentum, then snapped his whip-like tongue out, yanking the second ice blade clean from El’s hand.
Keep it, El thought, letting go. In the same motion she conjured a short ice lance and hurled it straight at Diego’s face. It missed, clipping only his right ear.
Diego roared with rage and swiped at El with his free claws but she mange to dodge the attack.
Gian seized the opening. He angled his katana low and swept it across Diego’s legs, slicing the right one clean off. The Mimivane crashed to the ground.
Gian stepped in to finish him with a reverse slash, but El yanked him backward and activated her Echo again, this time lessening gravity, before leaping away with him in tow. 
“Careful, Gian.”
He glanced down to see a thin, spiked tail embedded in the ground where he had stood moments ago, its tip still trembling from the missed strike. 
“So he was still hiding something… I didn’t even notice that tail coiled beneath him,” El muttered, eyeing the writhing wildborn now bleeding heavily and missing a leg.
Diego shrieked, “How dare you humans! You may kill me now, but your days are numbered! Soon the wildborn will rise. There are far more dangerous ones than me… and they’re already biding their time!!”
He cackled. “I heard Jorgumo is already on the move. You won’t escape her wrath.”
“I don’t know who or what that is… and I don’t care,” El answered coldly. A massive ice greatsword formed in her hands.
“Come then, human — come closer!” Diego screeched, struggling forward, coiled tail poised to strike.
“As you wish, wildborn,” El said, charging at him.
Diego lashed his tail in a deadly stab, but gravity suddenly spiked, warping his aim. The strike missed El’s heart, hitting nothing but air. She brought her greatsword down with crushing force, amplified by the pull of her Echo, cleaving straight through Diego from head to torso in a single, crushing strike, the ice blade burying into the earth beneath him.
The Mimivane twitched once, then fell still—its body split cleanly in two, frost spreading slowly across the severed halves.
