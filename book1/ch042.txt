Chapter 42: Guided by Purpose
Standing on the second floor, Oldman muttered, “Damn it... I told him to stay put.”
“You go after Ronald. I’ll head down and find Gian,” Kel said, already rushing down the stairs.
“Alright. Stay safe,” Oldman nodded, then turned toward the hallway.
With each step, his fists clenched tighter. His breathing grew heavier. The hatred in his eyes deepened.
Finally, he reached the closed wooden door at the end of the hallway.
He gripped the knob and pushed the door open. Inside the lavish bedroom, Ronald stood near the bed, trembling, holding a mop raised high like a weapon. 
“Who ordered you to do this?” Ronald stammered. “Whatever they paid you, I’ll double it! No, triple it! Just protect me. We can talk this out!”
Oldman said nothing.
He stepped inside. Slow. Silent.
“I’ll give you a mansion like this one!” Ronald said, dropping the mop as he backed away.
“Women? I’ve got connections—ten of them, all yours!”
Ronald’s voice cracked as his back hit the far wall beside a narrow window. 
“Young girls... I can get you as many as you want.” he added desperately.
Oldman’s fists were now bleeding from how tightly he clenched them. His jaw locked.
“What age do you like? Please... just let me live. I’ll give you anything!”
Ronald fell to his knees, sobbing.
Oldman had heard enough.
With a snarl, he punched Ronald square in the face, sending him crashing to the floor—barely conscious.
“Please... stop...” Ronald whimpered through blood and tears. “Anything... anything you want...”
Oldman grabbed him by the collar and dragged him close. Their eyes met.
His voice came low, shaking with fury.
“You should’ve killed yourself when you had the chance.”

Gian chased after Titus, catching a glimpse of him slipping out through a narrow backdoor that opened into the overgrown yard behind the mansion. He raised his bow and fired, but the arrow missed its mark.
By the time Gian reached the doorway and stepped into the yard, he fired another shot, but it veered off course and struck a tree at the edge of the forest. His aim was shaky, his side still bleeding, his body barely holding together.
Gritting his teeth, he pushed forward, refusing to slow down as he pursued him into the forest.
Suddenly, a translucent wolf lunged at him from a low branch of a tree just ahead on the forest path.
He dove to the side, hitting the ground hard, and fired a quick shot mid-fall. The arrow struck the creature in the stomach, slowing it down. Gian quickly stored his longbow and arrow into his Void Ring and scrambled to his feet.
The wounded wolf, still snarling with an arrow protruding from its gut, circled him warily.
Gian summoned his buckler and held it in his left hand, drawing the sword with his right, never taking his eyes off the creature.
It struck.
Gian deflected the sweeping claw with his buckler, then lunged forward and drove the sword into its chest. The wolf let out a fading growl before dissolving into nothing.
Panting, Gian turned back toward the direction Titus had gone and continued the pursuit.

At the front entrance of the mansion Kel arrived and rushed over to Michael, who stood watch beside the injured fighter and the frightened girls.
“Have you seen Gian?” Kel asked urgently.
“No,” Michael replied, keeping his bow ready. “I've been guarding the front and taking shots whenever I had a clear line. Why?”
“Gian’s gone. He probably went after that other guard. I need to back him up. That guy's strong,” Kel said, glancing back at the mansion with concern.
Suddenly, Tata squeaked from the ground, hopping frantically to get their attention.
Kel turned to the small driftmouse. “I think it's trying to tell us something.”
“You think?” Michael replied with dry sarcasm.
“It’s pointing that way. Something’s up,” Kel said, watching the rodent gesture.
Michael gave a quick nod. “Then go. I’ll lead the girls inside the mansion. Don’t worry, I’ll keep them safe.”
Kel nodded in return and looked at Tata. “Alright, you lead,” he said, then took off, following the darting creature with its dark brown fur.
Michael turned to the girls. “Alright, the mansion’s secure, but stay close to me, understand?”
Then to the injured fighter beside him, he added, “Once we get inside, guard the main door.”
The wounded man gave a silent nod, and they moved together.

As Gian moved deeper between the closely spaced trees, dry leaves and low branches brushing his armor, he froze at the sound of movement. Instinctively, he dropped low into the bushes and waited, watching. The leaves rustled, and something leapt toward him.
Tata landed on the leaf-covered ground in front of him, stared with a curious tilt of its head, then jumped onto his shoulder. It squeaked just as Kel arrived, panting from the run.
“Are you out of your mind?” Kel snapped. “Why’d you go off on your own?”
“I knew it was dangerous,” Gian admitted, clutching his abdomen with the arm strapped to his buckler. “But I couldn’t let him get away.”
Kel sighed, glancing at Gian’s injuries. “Alright, fine. But you’re supporting me from the side. I’ll take the lead.”
“You’re not much better off,” Gian shot back. “Your arm got bitten.”
“Then we fight side by side,” Kel said, relenting. He looked at Tata, now perched on Gian’s shoulder. “Can you still sense him? Is he close?”
Tata squeaked twice in reply.
“Alright, lead us,” Kel ordered.
With that, the driftmouse leapt from Gian’s shoulder and took off, darting through the underbrush. It didn’t rely on sight, but on its heightened hearing and sensitivity to vibrations in the ground, guiding them straight to their prey.

After a while, Tata suddenly stopped. Gian and Kel flanked the driftmouse, crouching low beside it.
“Is he near?” Kel whispered.
Tata didn’t squeak, but turned to Kel and nodded once.
“Can you see him?” Kel asked Gian, scanning the treeline.
“No...” Gian murmured, eyes sweeping over the softly swaying bushes and trees.
Tata stood alert, focused dead ahead.
“Guess he’s somewhere in front of us,” Kel muttered.
Then there was movement. Two shapes emerged from the dense bushes ahead, stepping into a small clearing between the trees.
“Let’s go!” Gian said, charging forward. He slashed his sword at one of the figures—it was Titus. Beside him, Kel swung his axe at the translucent wolf, but the beast dodged to the side.
Without pausing, Kel unleashed several droplets of blood, guiding them toward the wolf. The crimson shards struck, injuring it lightly. He turned to clash with Titus, but the man’s longsword met him mid-swing.
Tata darted away from the battle, keeping low and alert, waiting for the right moment.
The wolf lunged again. Kel narrowly dodged.
Titus raised his sword high and brought it down with force. Gian lifted his buckler above his head, blocking the strike, but the impact forced him to drop his sword. Using his right hand to brace the buckler, he withstood the blow.
A vicious kick followed as Titus struck Gian in the abdomen, worsening the wound and sending him rolling across the ground.
Before Titus could finish him off, Tata launched from the shadows, biting down on the back of Titus's ankle. Though its teeth weren’t long, it dug deep enough to make Titus stumble and curse, kicking the small creature away.
Gian crawled to the base of a thick tree trunk, leaned against the bark, and summoned his longbow and arrow in front of him.
Titus instinctively turned toward Tata, raising his sword to kill the small creature. Before he could strike, Kel intercepted with his axe. 
Gian shifted his aim and loosed an arrow, striking the wolf near Kel in the chest. It dissolved instantly into nothing.
Kel and Titus locked weapons in the center of the clearing, boots digging into the soft forest floor as they strained against each other. Kel, weakened by the wound on his arm, began to lose ground. Gritting his teeth, he suddenly tilted his axe, letting the longsword slide off and strike the dirt. He raised his hand to Titus’s face—blood droplets formed instantly and shot out.
Several struck Titus, piercing his cheek and one eye. Screaming, Titus dropped his longsword and clutched his face in agony.
Gian, now with a clear shot, drew another arrow and fired.
The arrow struck Titus in the neck.
He collapsed.
Kel staggered, panting, and gave Gian a thumbs-up with his right hand. “Nice shot.”
Gian let his longbow drop, head leaning back against the tree as he exhaled in ragged breaths.
Tata hopped onto Titus's body, puffed out its tiny chest, and squeaked triumphantly. 
Kel gave a tired smile. “Yeah, good job to you too.”
