Chapter 67: The Fall of Lipad  
Eleven years ago, atop the forested summit of Fallen Cliff stood several towering rock spires—jagged pillars so sheer that no ordinary person could hope to climb them. Upon one of these lofty heights rested the lesser village of the Lipad Clan, where its people lived peaceful, carefree lives far removed from the dangers of the world below.
The clan was completely isolated from the rest of the world, even from most Wildborn, save for a few avian ones. They lived mainly by farming, though from time to time a small party of hunters descended the impossible cliffs using the clan's secret ability. 
An eight-year-old Anni stood gazing at the sky, her blue eyes bright, when a group of hunters suddenly dropped from above, cutting through the air like falling feathers. Their beautiful white wings folded neatly against their backs before dissolving into shimmering motes of light that faded into nothing. They carried fresh Wildborn meat and jars of water from their journey below.
Among them was Daedalus, the clan's only Echo Shaper—a gifted young craftsman who had already forged multiple relics despite his age.
Smiling, he walked over to Anni. “Hey there, Anni. Where’s the Chief?”
“I don’t know,” she pouted. “Probably by the big white tree again, taking care of it instead of playing with me.”
Laughing, another man ruffled her blonde hair. “Well, that tree is the sacred heart of our clan, Anni. Your father has to take care of it. It’s his duty.” 
“Get off me, Uncle Rob! You stink,” she said, swatting his hand away.
Still chuckling, Rob and the other hunters headed toward the village center.
“Hey, Daedalus!” Anni called, marching after him. “You promised me a relic. Where is it?”
“Soon, Anni. Don’t be impatient,” he replied with a grin. “Once I finish my fourth relic, I promise it’ll be a big help to the village. And you’ll be the one to take care of it.” 
“You always say that. It’s been almost a year!” she huffed, crossing her arms.
“Careful, Daedalus,” Rob called over his shoulder with a laugh. “You don’t want our future Chief mad at you.”
After a short walk, they reached a tall, breathtaking tree—pure white from trunk to leaf, its surface glowing softly under the sunlight like polished ivory. This sacred tree stood at the heart of their village.
“Hey, everyone! Rob and the others are back!” a man shouted. Villagers emerged from their homes, gathering around the hunters. Laughter and chatter filled the air as they exchanged tales of the outside world.

That night, inside their cozy home, Eduna noticed Anni picking at her food with a grumpy expression.
“What’s the matter, Anni?” she asked.
“Nothing,” Anni mumbled, though she couldn’t ignore the way her mother’s gentle, caring gaze lingered on her, quietly reading her mood.
“It’s just… Father’s always taking care of that stupid tree or helping other people. He’s not even home yet,” Anni added, her gaze drifting toward the window where the silent night reflected back at her.
“Child, your father is the Chief of this village,” Eduna said softly. “He carries the responsibility of tending to that sacred tree and caring for our people. He manages everything. Someday, you’ll understand, when you become Chief yourself.” 
“What’s so special about that tree, anyway?” Anni pouted, shoving the last bite into her mouth.
Eduna reached over, gently stroking her daughter’s long blonde hair. “Long ago, your ancestor managed to climb this high cliff on a bet with his friends. After years of trying, he finally reached the top and found a strange tree. It’s an unknown Primeflora—something unseen even in the outside world. We simply call it the Sacred White Tree. When he looked closer, he saw it bore strange fruits. He ate one… and discovered a tome hidden within it.” 
“The Echo of Wings,” Anni said knowingly.
“Yes. A rare ability never seen before,” Eduna nodded. “Recognizing how special and unique this Primeflora was, your ancestor decided to protect it. Over time, he built a clan here, far from the outside world.”
She let her hand fall back to her lap before continuing. “Of course, the clan could never remain completely isolated. From time to time, a few chosen men were granted the Echo of Wings and became hunters. They’d travel beyond the cliffs to gather food and materials, meeting new people, sometimes even falling in love. Those who did would share the clan’s secret and bring their partners here to live.” 
“Just like you, Mother,” Anni said.
Eduna smiled warmly. “Yes, just like me. Your father was a young hunter when he met me in the outside world. We fell in love, and when I agreed to marry him, he brought me here and shared the clan’s secret.”
“What’s the outside world like? Don’t you want to go back?” Anni asked curiously.
“No, dear. My world is here, with you and your father,” Eduna replied, brushing a strand of hair from Anni’s face. “The outside world is… different. Dangerous. Full of Wildborn and bad people. Compared to that, here, everyone is happy and kind.”
There was a knock at the door.
Anni jumped up and ran to open it, only to find Daedalus standing there with his usual bright smile.
“What’s with the long face? Expecting someone else?” he teased, leaning in to greet Eduna.
“You should be happy, Anni. I finally finished the relic I promised you,” Daedalus said with a grin. 
“You did?” Anni’s eyes lit up, her excitement barely contained.
“Come in, Daedalus. Have you eaten yet?” Eduna asked, rising from her seat to prepare a plate.
“Now that you mention it… I haven’t,” Daedalus admitted, stepping inside as Anni eagerly grabbed his hand and began tugging.
“Give it to me! Come on, give it!” she demanded.
Laughing, Daedalus reached into his satchel and pulled out a small, ordinary-looking transparent jug. “Here—this is Spring Glass.”
Anni held it up, noticing a delicate wing symbol etched into the base. “That’s it?” she asked skeptically.
“This,” Daedalus said with a proud laugh, “is no ordinary jug, Anni. It slowly refills itself with clean, drinkable water over time.”
“Wow…” Anni whispered, staring at it in awe. Even Eduna’s eyes widened.
“That’s very convenient, Daedalus,” Eduna said warmly.
“Well, what can I say? I’m a genius,” he chuckled, then pulled out a wing-shaped hair clip. “Lady Eduna, here’s your storage relic. I’m returning it now that our outside hunt is over.” 
“Thank you, Daedalus. Actually, there’s no need to return it. You made it, after all,” Eduna replied with a smile. 
“True, but Wingclip was my gift to you when you married Chief Agnor. I should apologize, though. We keep borrowing it every time we go down the cliffs,” Daedalus said with a laugh. 
“Why not just make another Wingclip?” Anni asked casually, tilting the Spring Glass to pour water onto the ground.
“Hey! You’re wasting it!” Daedalus exclaimed, stopping her hand. “And for your information, Anni, it’s not easy to make another Wingclip. Besides, I’m a man. Why would I make another storage relic in the form of a hair clip?”
Suddenly, a deafening blast tore through the air, rattling the village to its core.
“What was that?!” Anni asked, eyes wide.
Eduna immediately pulled her close.
“Stay here. I’ll check outside,” Daedalus said, already rushing to the door. 
“Wait—!” Eduna tried to stop Anni, but the girl slipped free, curiosity pushing her to follow.
“Anni! Get back here!” Eduna shouted, chasing after her daughter.
What they saw outside made them freeze.
The Sacred White Tree blazed at its crown, its pure-white leaves curling into black smoke. High above, a colossal Wildborn hovered over the village, feathers like razor-edged steel streaked with silver and storm-gray. One by one, masked men in violet robes leapt from its back, landing among the startled villagers.
The fight exploded instantly—steel clashing, voices shouting, and Echo abilities crackling through the air.
Daedalus grabbed Anni and yanked her inside, slamming the door. Panic flickered openly in his eyes, barely restrained beneath his forced composure. “S-stay here. I’ll go… help them,” he stammered, trying to sound steady though his hands shook.
Eduna caught his shoulder. “Don’t even think about it, Daedalus. You’re not a fighter.”
“But I’m a hunter—it’s my duty!” he protested.
“You’re with the hunters because you’re an Echo Shaper, not a warrior,” she said firmly.
A thunderous blast shook the house, dust raining from the ceiling. Outside, the screams grew louder.
“I have the Echo of Wings. I have to do something,” Daedalus said, but his gaze shifted to Anni, who stood confused and afraid. 
“There is something only you can do,” Eduna said, locking eyes with him.
After a tense pause, he nodded.
Eduna crouched and took Anni’s shoulders in her hands. “Anni… you need to be brave. When this is over, we’ll come for you. I promise.”
“What are you saying, Mom?”
“Listen—your father and the hunters will fight them off, but I want you safe. Go with Daedalus. He’ll fly you away.”
“I’m not leaving you!” Anni cried.
A sudden gust like a blade ripped through the wall, slicing the dining table clean in two.
“Go! Now!” Eduna shoved Anni toward Daedalus.
“I’ll come back for you,” Daedalus said, gripping Anni’s wrist. “I’ll take her to my hideout.”
Be strong, my little wing… you must survive, no matter what, Eduna thought as she watched her daughter being carried away.
Amid the chaos, Daedalus summoned his white wings and launched into the sky, Anni pounding her small fists against his back, her cries breaking into desperate sobs.
“Stop! Take me back!” she sobbed.
“I’m sorry, Anni… I’m weak. This is all I can do,” Daedalus said, his own tears falling. Hearing the pain in his voice, Anni stopped struggling and clung to him silently.
They landed at a small cave hidden in the cliffside, its entrance disguised with a rock-patterned cloth.
“Stay here. Don’t make a sound. I’ll go back for your mother. Be brave.” He scanned the shelves inside, then took down a white tome marked with a wing symbol, its glow pulsing like a heartbeat.
“Take this. If I don’t return by tomorrow noon, open it. It will grant you the Echo of Wings.”
“What do you mean? You have to come back with Mom!”
“I will. But if I’m hurt and can’t… this is your last resort. Promise me.”
“…I promise,” Anni whispered. “Just save her.”
He smiled faintly, stepped to the entrance, and flew back toward the burning village.

Time passed painfully slowly, each second stretching into an eternity. Anni waited, heart pounding, hoping Daedalus would return with her mother or her father to say it was over. But reality was cruel. Fear gnawed at her until she could no longer bear it.
She opened the tome. The power to fly bloomed in her mind, but it brought no comfort. She sat there crying, lost in time.
Then—a sound. The cloth entrance shifted aside.
Anni’s eyes widened.
“Aunt Elanor?”
It was her father’s younger sister, who had left the village years ago to travel the outside world, defying the rule that only men could leave the village. Anni remembered her as the funny, adventurous aunt who snuck her sweets.
“Anni? Is that you?” Elanor asked.
Anni ran into her arms, sobbing.
“Why are you crying? What happened?”
“Men in violet robes attacked the village! You have to save Mom and Dad!”
“What?!” Elanor’s expression hardened. “Stay here—I’ll go check—”
“No! Please don’t leave me again!”
Elanor hesitated, then softened. “Alright. Stay close to me. I promise I’ll protect you.”
El carried Anni back up the cliff, her Echo of Gravity letting her walk as if the sheer rock face were level ground. At the village entrance, they froze. Daedalus lay sprawled in a pool of blood, his body pierced by arrows. 
Anni’s sobs broke the silence, while El’s hands shimmered with frost as she summoned twin ice blades, eyes sharp and searching. Together they walked through the ruined village, stepping past the bodies of fallen villagers scattered across the broken ground. Around them, homes lay in ruins, some still smoldering beneath drifting ash. 
When they turned a corner, the sight struck like a blade to the heart. Agnor, Chief of the Lipad Clan, slumped against the ruined wall of their home, his body unmoving. His own white longsword was buried deep in his chest. Beside him, Eduna’s lifeless body leaned against his, her hand still tightly clasped in his, as if refusing to let go even in death. 
“Mom! Dad!” Anni cried, rushing toward them.
She knelt beside them, pressing her forehead against their joined hands.
“I’m sorry, Mom… Dad. I promise… I’ll become strong. The strongest Lipad. I’ll avenge you all,” she whispered.
El’s gaze shifted to the village center. Where the Sacred White Tree once stood in radiant splendor, only a charred, lifeless husk remained, its former radiance completely extinguished. Its once-radiant leaves were gone, and the white trunk had become nothing more than a charred monument to a fallen clan.
