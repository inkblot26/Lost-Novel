Chapter 40: Three results, One resolve
Inside the third-floor chamber of Richard’s mansion, Lady Rose stood with her rapier drawn beside Lord Henry, who gripped a dark wooden staff. The polished floor reflected the flickering light from scattered torches along the walls. A few steps ahead, Dan positioned himself protectively in front of Highlord Richard, an axe formed from his Echo held ready. Behind him, Richard sat in his chair, a longsword resting loosely at his side. 
“Well, if it isn’t my charming, timid little sister. Who would’ve thought?” Highlord Richard said as he slowly rose to his feet.
“You’ve fooled everyone while quietly plotting to rule this city. What a brilliant actress you are,” he added with a twisted smile, though his voice dripped with venom rather than amusement. 
“I never wanted to rule,” Lady Rose replied, locking eyes with him. “But you left me no choice, brother.”
“And what do we have here?” Richard turned his gaze to Lord Henry. “Is this how you betray me, Lord Henry? How much did she offer you? She has no wealth—you're richer than she is.”
He smirked. “Unless… ah yes, unless she sold herself to you. Perhaps you’ve already replaced your wife.”
“Don’t you dare insult my love for my wife!” Henry roared, stepping forward.
With that, he charged, and the battle finally began.
Henry swung his staff, extending it toward Richard, but Dan intercepted mid-air with his summoned axe. Lady Rose moved lightly on the polished floor, her rapier slipping past her brother’s defense and piercing Richard’s left shoulder, leaving faint scratches on the floor from her quick steps.
Grunting in pain, Richard brought his longsword down in a furious arc. It struck only a hastily formed barrier in front of Rose’s face.
“Argh!” Richard cried out, releasing his grip on the sword to clutch the embedded rapier with his right hand. A dark shimmer pulsed around his palm as he activated his rare Echo of Rot. Corruption spread through the blade, decaying it instantly. Rose yanked her weapon back, but it had already rusted through, with only half remaining and the broken tip clattering to the floor.
“I’ll kill you!” Richard snarled, blood dripping from his shoulder. He activated his Echo of Fire and summoned a small, bird-like flame, then hurled it at Rose.
She dove to the side just in time, the fire narrowly missing her.

Henry shortened his staff just in time to deflect the axe hurled by Dan, but the weapon boomeranged back into Dan’s right hand, guided by his control over the summoned Echo.
Dan struck again, swinging the axe with force. Henry blocked it with his staff, but a second axe suddenly materialized in Dan’s left hand. The unexpected strike slammed into Henry’s side, forcing him to stagger back, clutching the deep wound.
“You look tired,” Dan sneered. “Must’ve been exhausting just getting here.”
He casually tossed and caught one axe after another, manipulating them with fluid precision through his Echo control. Henry kept dodging, retreating step by step until he reached the corner of the room. In one quick motion, he yanked down a bookshelf, sending it crashing to the floor just as one of the axes hit and became buried beneath the scattered books.
Seizing the opening, Henry conjured a short lance of molten rock and hurled it at Dan just as Dan flung another axe in return. The two projectiles collided mid-air in a burst of heat and sparks.
In the same breath, Henry spun and swung his staff, extending it with force. The blow struck Dan square in the face. Without wasting the opportunity, Henry whipped the staff back around, slamming it into Dan’s head as he collapsed to the ground.
Struggling to stay upright, Henry used his staff for support and began summoning a long molten lance. But before it could fully materialize, an axe came spinning through the air and slashed into his leg.
He cried out in pain and collapsed onto the fallen bookshelf.
“I’ll kill you,” Dan growled, blood dripping down his face as he crawled, trying to stand.
Gritting his teeth, Henry fought through the pain. He completed the molten lance and, with a final effort, hurled it toward Dan, impaling him to the floor. Then Henry collapsed.

At the stairway, Ezra arrived to find Conrad standing alone, blocking the path. The other guards lay dead, fallen in the fierce battle. Four allied fighters rested nearby, bandages on arms and legs, scanning the hallway despite their injuries.
“What’s going on here?” Ezra asked one of them, her eyes scanning the cracked floor, evidence of the brutal clash between Conrad and Torren.
“Captain Ezra! You’re safe!” one fighter called out with relief.
“Yeah. Where’s Lady Rose?” Ezra asked quickly.
“Lady Rose and Lord Henry went upstairs with a few of our people,” another fighter replied, his head wrapped in a blood-soaked bandage. “We’ve already taken down all the guards here, but we can’t help Sir Torren or head up.”
Ezra assessed the scene. Can’t blame them. One wrong move could throw off the rhythm of that fight. It could help Torren… or get him killed.
Torren stood at the center of the chaos, panting, his metal skin bloodied and covered in deep cuts. Across from him, Conrad was bruised and battered but still standing, with his shadowy bear barely clinging to form at his side.
“Looks like your bear’s at its limit,” Torren said between breaths.
“I acknowledge your strength, Captain Torren,” Conrad replied. “The rumors were true. It’s a pity you chose the wrong side. Any minute now, the soldiers will arrive.”
“I wouldn’t bet on that if I were you,” Torren said with a smirk.
Conrad’s expression tightened. Something’s wrong... It’s been too long. The reinforcements should’ve arrived by now.
“Let’s end this!” Conrad roared as he and Torren charged again, their weapons colliding with a thunderous clang. Sparks flew with every impact of greatsword and warhammer, while the shadowy bear waited at the side, ready to strike.
I can win this, Torren thought. But I need to let Ezra and the others go now.
Then came a pause—a brief gap in the exchange. Both men stepped back. That was when the shadowy bear lunged toward Torren.
This is it.
Torren slammed his warhammer into the floor. The weakened structure gave way beneath them, unable to withstand the accumulated damage. The ground cracked, then collapsed entirely, and all three—Conrad, Torren, and the shadowy bear—plummeted to the floor below in a storm of dust and debris.
A short distance away, Ezra watched in shock as part of the upper floor crumbled. To her surprise, a narrow portion of the staircase still remained, barely hanging on but intact enough to climb.
Without hesitation, she shouted, “Let’s go!” and sprinted up the stairs.

Down below, after the floor gave way, Torren groaned while pushing a slab of debris off his back. Dust coated his skin and clothes, and bits of broken plaster and floorboards littered the area around him. Nearby, Conrad was also rising to his feet, bloodied and breathing heavily. The shadowy bear had vanished.
Both men were exhausted. Both were unarmed, their weapons lost somewhere beneath the rubble. And yet, their eyes still burned with determination.
Without a word, they charged at each other and began trading punches. Each blow, amplified by their Echo of Strength, was powerful enough to stagger or knock out an ordinary fighter, yet they endured, refusing to fall.
Conrad swung a heavy hook, but Torren caught it on his raised left arm. He immediately countered with a sharp jab to Conrad’s face, making him stumble. Without hesitation, Torren followed up with a brutal uppercut that struck Conrad’s jaw cleanly, lifting him off his feet.
Conrad crashed to the ground—unconscious.
Panting, Torren staggered, then dropped onto a slab of broken stone, finally allowing himself to breathe.

After a while, two allied fighters approached Torren.
“Captain Torren, are you alright?” one asked, while the other glanced up at the collapsed section of the floor above.
“I’ll manage,” Torren said, slowly rising to his feet, wincing. “What’s the situation down here?”
“We have the advantage, sir,” the first replied. “Most key areas are secure. There are still a few skirmishes in other sections, but we’re winning.”
“Miss Mira is tending to the wounded by the stairway to the second floor,” the second added. “She can treat your injuries there.”
“Good. Guide me to her. We still need to help the others,” Torren said, deactivating his Echo of Metal Skin as he began walking, unsteady but determined. 
“Sir… where’s your warhammer?” one of them asked.
“It’s somewhere under the rubble. But we don’t have time to look for it,” Torren replied with a faint smile. “Besides, in my condition, I doubt I could lift it.”
“C-Captain! Over here, it’s Conrad. He’s still breathing,” the other fighter called, kneeling beside the unconscious man. “He’s the head of Highlord Richard’s personal guard. Should I finish him off?”
Torren looked over, his eyes tired but steady. “No. Leave him. He’s no threat anymore.”
As they walked, the fighter hesitated. “Are you sure, sir? Should we really let him live?”
Torren was silent for a moment, then said, “He’s not entirely bad. I heard he accepted the position because it was a military order—probably at Richard’s request. And while he’s a womanizer, from what I’ve heard, he doesn’t force himself on anyone.”
He sighed. “He’s just a fool who follows orders too blindly. A true soldier… but that’s not always a good thing. If he’s guilty of other crimes, Lady Rose will judge him later. But for now, let him live.”
“…Understood, sir. This way,” the fighter said, supporting Torren as they continued toward the stairs.

In the hallway, behind a slightly ajar door, Leon crouched with an arrow lodged in his right leg. The narrow space forced him to press close to the wall, shadows stretching across the floor from the flickering torchlight.
“Damn that woman… how can she fire that many arrows so fast?” he muttered, gritting his teeth as he yanked the arrow out, gripping the wound to stifle the pain. “If I’d known she was Ezra’s lieutenant, I would’ve killed her the moment they opened the door.”
Outside, Nora knelt, panting from exhaustion. Her Echo of the Bow flickered weakly, on the verge of vanishing, but she still guarded the door to the room, refusing to let Leon through. Her ally lay dead nearby, his face slashed open by Leon’s claws during the earlier clash.
How many arrows does she have left? How fast can she still shoot? Leon thought, peeking through a crack in the door. Nora remained in position, bow raised, eyes sharp despite her fading strength.

She’s probably bluffing. That stance is just for show. But… can I risk it? He winced as he looked down at his injured leg. This is dragging out too long. If I hadn’t taken that hit earlier…
Nora’s breath came in shallow gasps. Her arms trembled, and her vision blurred. If he rushes me now, I won’t be able to hit him mid-charge. My only chance is to strike the moment he’s right in front of me…
Suddenly, she saw a blur.
Leon charged.
She didn’t release her arrow. Not yet. She waited.
As his claws closed in, she loosed the final shot.
The arrow flew true, but at the last second, Leon pushed his Echo of Agility beyond its limit and vanished to the side in a burst of speed. He slid past her, his claws raking across her chest as he passed.
I’m sorry… Lady Rose… Nora thought, collapsing to the floor.
Panting heavily, Leon stumbled to a stop, clutching his chest as he tried to catch his breath. Behind him, the lieutenant lay still.
“That was close,” he muttered. “I had to push my Echo to the edge, beyond what I’ve ever done before.” He leaned against the wall and slid down to the floor, sweat trailing down his brow.
