Chapter 48: The Calm That Prepares
The sun dipped low over the open stone courtyard of Lord Henry’s mansion, residence of Tillara City’s Right-hand ruler. Warm gold light spilled across the surrounding walls and practice grounds as two young men sat on the flagstones near the edge of the yard, drenched in sweat and breathing heavily after another round of training. 
“Hey, Gian… what do you think’s for dinner tonight?” Kel asked, wiping his face with a towel, his tone light despite the ache in his limbs.
Gian stood, stretching out sore shoulders. “Not sure,” he said simply. “Anyway, gear up. We’re on patrol tonight.” 
“Yeah, yeah. I know. We can’t be freeloaders forever,” Kel said with a laugh, tossing his towel over one shoulder.
From the shaded walkway bordering the courtyard, a man in a brown gambler’s hat approached, a driftmouse perched calmly on his shoulder, its tiny whiskers twitching. 
“Guys,” Oldman called, tossing a water jug toward Gian. “Highlady Rose is summoning us to her mansion.”
“Why?” Gian asked, catching the jug effortlessly.
Oldman’s tone shifted serious. “I think… it’s finally time.”
“They’ve found something about them?” Kel sat up straighter, the fatigue draining from his voice.
“I think so. Now hurry up.”
“It’s been almost seven months,” Gian said as he passed the jug to Kel. “Even if there’s no solid lead, I think it’s time we move.”
“Yeah. I’m getting bored just training and doing patrols,” Kel agreed.
“I’m with you,” Oldman said. “We’ve rested long enough. It’s time to start looking for them.”
Squeak, squeak. Tata chirped as if in agreement.
“I know, Tata. Let’s go,” Oldman murmured, and the three of them crossed the courtyard toward the arched corridor leading back inside the mansion.

In the main receiving room of Tillara’s ruling mansion, Highlady Rose sat in a simple yet elegant high-backed chair near the center of the room, her posture calm but commanding. Ezra stood silently behind her like a shadow.
Across from them, Gian, Kel, and Oldman sat on a couch. Tata perched attentively on Oldman’s shoulder, tail flicking.
“Good evening to you three,” Rose greeted, her tone soft yet firm. “I have news that might interest you.”
She nodded toward Ezra, who stepped forward.
“A horde of Wildborn attacked Graylatch Village, at the edge of the Unknown,” she reported.
“Graylatch?” Oldman interjected, brows furrowing. “Isn’t that the stone-walled settlement near the rocky cliff inside the forest?”
“Yes,” Ezra confirmed.
“You know it?” Kel asked, glancing at him.
“I’ve been to the edges of the Unknown, back when I was a mercenary,” Oldman said. “Like most, I never went farther than the explored borderlands. But I remember some of the strong villages nearby.”
Ezra continued, “Graylatch lies further west of Tillara City, beyond the direct jurisdiction of the Lanthea Kingdom.”
“So what happened there?” Gian asked.
Rose looked directly at him. “The village wasn’t completely wiped out. And even if it isn’t part of our territory, I can’t ignore those in need. I sent a squad of soldiers to deliver aid and assess the situation.”
Ezra picked up the thread. “A unit of twenty soldiers, led by Sergeant Brina, was enroute with food relief… but they were ambushed by men in violet robes.”
At that, Gian, Kel, and Oldman’s expressions hardened.
“How reliable is this report?” Oldman asked sharply.
Ezra’s face was grave. “One soldier survived. He faked his death during the attack and hid in the underbrush. It took him several days to make his way back toward the nearest outpost. He finally stumbled upon a patrol team near the Verdant Ring before collapsing. A letter arrived this noon reporting the incident. He’s now receiving treatment.” 
“Which outpost?” Oldman pressed.
“Outpost Two,” Ezra answered.
The trio exchanged glances—silent but unified.
“I think it’s worth checking out,” Gian said.
“Yeah. It’s the first solid lead we’ve had on those masked men,” Kel added.
“Then it’s settled,” Oldman said. “We’ll prepare tonight and head out in the morning.”
Squeak! Tata chirped in agreement.
But Rose raised a hand. “I know you're eager to go, but I suggest you delay your departure at least a day.”
The three looked puzzled.
“Tomorrow, a group of crafters will arrive,” she explained. “Tillara hasn’t had proper crafters since the previous ones fled during Richard’s rule. High taxes and instability drove them out.” 
“Lady Rose convinced some of them to return,” Ezra added. “They’ll be reestablishing their shops here.”
“I was wondering about that,” Kel said. “I heard all cities have echotech shops, but I never saw one here. That explains it.”
“What do you think, Gian?” Oldman asked.
“A one-day delay won’t change much,” Gian replied calmly.
“Yeah. It takes about twelve days to travel from here to Outpost Two anyway,” Oldman agreed.
“And we’ll definitely find some useful echotech among the crafters’ goods,” Kel added with a grin.
“Don’t worry,” Rose said warmly. “When they arrive, I’ll have them come here first so you three can prepare properly.”
“Thank you, Highlady Rose,” the trio said in unison as they stood and bowed.

The next day, Gian, Kel, and Oldman walked down the bustling street toward Lady Rose’s mansion, passing market stalls and stone townhouses before stopping at the wrought-iron gate guarded at the entrance.
Silently, a hand tapped Kel on the back, making him jump. 
“Damn it, would you stop that, Sam?” Kel snapped, spinning around. Sam stood there grinning, his Echo of Silence letting him sneak up undetected. 
“Told you he wouldn’t stop,” Oldman said with a chuckle.
“You’ve got Tata with you,” Kel grumbled, glaring at the tiny creature. “He can sense vibrations. Why didn’t he warn me?”
Squeak. Tata turned his back pointedly.
Oldman laughed. “Tata says it’s not his job to warn you about every little thing.”
Kel scowled.
“How is it, Sam?” Gian asked, ignoring Kel’s ongoing feud with Tata.
“You’re just in time,” Sam replied. “A married pair of crafters arrived about an hour ago. They’re waiting in one of the rooms. Come on, follow me.”
They followed him through the front courtyard and along a side corridor to one of the guest chambers temporarily assigned to the visiting crafters.
Inside, a couple sat at a low table, sipping tea and chatting with Lord Henry, who looked up as the door opened.
“Gian, Kel, Oldman—you’re finally here,” Henry greeted warmly, standing to welcome them.
“Good day, Lord Henry,” the trio replied in unison.
“I’d like you to meet Mr. Brent and Mrs. Mica Quen,” Henry said, gesturing to the friendly couple. “They’re crafters from Ravenscaar City in the Aethelgard Kingdom.”
“Good day to you, gentlemen,” Brent said with a warm smile. 
“We heard you’re preparing for a journey,” Mica added. She stood, opening a faded brown suitcase. Inside were dozens of echotech items, all in miniature form. “Is there anything specific you’re looking for?”
“Whoa, that’s neat,” Kel said, stepping closer to examine the suitcase.
“This is called the Laeden Case,” Mica explained. “It’s a storage relic. The items inside appear in miniature form, but once pulled out, they return to their original size.”
Gian and Oldman exchanged a glance and nodded.
“Let’s start with essentials before moving to weapons and armor,” Gian suggested.
“Then I believe this will be useful,” Brent said, pulling out a tiny, three-pointed stone. He tossed it onto the polished floor, but instead of falling flat, it hovered just above the surface, spinning slowly as it pointed in three fixed directions.
“This is the Direstone,” he explained. “It aligns itself to point east, north, and west—basic orientation in the wild.”
“Wow, that’s cool,” Kel said, watching the stone hum softly before it descended to the ground.
“That’s a standard item,” Oldman added. “I used to have one of those back in the day.”
“We’ll take one,” Gian said. “Do you have anything for healing?”
“Yes, of course,” Brent replied, producing a dried green leaf. “This echotech is called Lileaf—a medicinal leaf that can be patched onto wounds.”
“It gradually heals minor injuries by absorbing body heat and converting it into regenerative energy,” Mica added.
“Why do we need that? We have Oldman,” Kel said tilting his head.
“We can’t always rely on him,” Gian said. “We need backup just in case.”
“Besides,” Oldman added, crossing his arms, “are you dumb? Who’s going to heal me when I’m the one hurt?”
“Okay, okay, just saying,” Kel muttered.
“Maybe we’ll get twenty?” Gian suggested.
Henry and Brent both laughed. Even Oldman cracked a smile.
“I’m sorry, sir,” Brent said politely, “but this is rare and expensive. We can only offer four for now. More might arrive in a few days with other crafters.”
“Well… how much does one cost?” Kel asked.
“Twenty Fangnotes each,” Brent answered.
“Damn, that is expensive,” Kel muttered.
“I think two is enough,” Gian said. “We still need to buy other gear.”
“Make it four,” Henry said with a kind smile. “Lady Rose and I have agreed to cover the cost.”
“You don’t have to do that, Lord Henry,” Gian said quickly.
“He’s right. It’d feel wrong to accept,” Oldman added.
“How about this,” Henry said. “You three choose whatever you need first, and we’ll discuss the total cost afterward. We can split it if needed. I won’t take no for an answer. It was Lady Rose’s intention to help you.”
The three nodded.
“Hey, do you have Flicks?” Oldman asked Mica.
“Of course, sir. How many do you need?”
“Five should be enough. Just for emergencies.”
“What’s Flicks?” Kel asked, while Gian also looked curious.
“It’s a waterproof handheld root-branch from the Walking Rhiza tree,” Mica explained. “When struck against a surface, it produces a flamelike light at its tip.”
“That sounds useful. Can we try one?” Kel asked.
“It’s not single-use,” Oldman explained. “But the branch shortens a little each time you light it. Save it for later. You’ll see how it works in the field.”
“Good point,” Kel nodded.
“Do you have something comfortable for sleeping?” Oldman asked suddenly.
Gian and Kel turned to him.
“What? You’ve never been in the field for months at a time. Trust me, you’ll miss having a bed,” Oldman said. 
“You’re in luck,” Brent said with a grin. “Mica, show them.”
Mica pulled out a glass bottle filled with water. Inside floated a strange water lily. She stepped into the shaded corner of the room away from the tall window, took out the lily, shook it dry, and gently tossed it onto the floor. The plant unfurled rapidly, blooming into a cushioned bed the size of a man. Tata immediately climbed on top and spun in a circle, claiming it.
“This echotech is the Nightlily,” Brent explained. “A portable sleeper bed. It blooms open in darkness, creating a soft resting space. But when exposed to sunlight or heavy moisture, it folds shut.”
“We definitely need that,” Oldman said, impressed. Kel nodded in agreement.
The buying session lasted a few hours as the trio purchased echotech items, weapons, and armor—and had their existing gear enhanced through the Echo of Shape. At last, they were fully equipped and ready for the journey ahead.

Early the next morning, just before sunrise, three figures stood waiting before the heavy wooden doors of Tillara City’s east gate, the stone walls rising high on either side.
Gian wore newly crafted black leather armor—sleek, close-fitting, and reinforced. A black longbow rested across his back beside a quiver of matching arrows. His Morphguard in a plain buckler  mode strapped to his left arm balanced the short sword at his side, its edge honed by the Echo of Shape.
To Gian’s right stood Kel, clad in deep red leather armor matching the hue of his messy brown hair. His Crimson Blood Axe rested at his side, while two short throwing axes were strapped securely across his back.
On Gian’s left was Oldman, dressed in a white long-sleeved shirt beneath a brown vest, both subtly reinforced. Black gauntlets covered his hands, while hidden knives rested inside each boot. In one hand, he held the shortened form of the dark Stickk relic. As always, his favorite brown gambler’s hat shaded the old scar across his forehead. Tata perched comfortably on his shoulder, tail curling lazily.
With a low creak, the reinforced wooden gates slowly parted inward, revealing the dirt road stretching eastward beyond the city walls.
The trio stepped forward—beginning their journey to Outpost Two, where they hoped to uncover more about the masked men in violet robes.
