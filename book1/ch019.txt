Chapter 19: Blood Brothers
Should I summon my bow with the Void Ring and show Kel the relic? Gian wondered. Even if I used it, the dark might make aiming impossible. 
“What are you thinking?” Kel asked, noticing the serious look on Gian’s face.
“How to kill them…and survive this,” Gian replied. 
“Same. I know I bragged in front of Lady Clara, but honestly, I don’t know how we pull this off,” Kel said, brow furrowed. 
“What do we know about them?” Gian asked, trying to piece together a plan.
“Well, they can jump high and blend into the forest, especially at night like this. Wait…how are they even tracking us?” Kel asked, frowning. 
“They’ve got a strong sense of smell,” Gian replied.
“How do you know that?” Kel pressed.
“I learned it during military training,” Gian said. A memory flickered—Kaito, the smallest in their class, adjusting his glasses as he pestered the elder with endless questions about wildborn. 
“So hiding’s out,” Kel muttered. 
They both fell quiet, breath fogging faintly in the damp air, listening as branches cracked and leaves shifted.
“Hit and run,” Gian said at last, low. 
“What?”
“Attack fast, then run. Over and over.”
Kel nodded slowly. “Split up. We draw them apart, fight in smaller numbers, then fall back to this spot. Stay within sight when we can. If one of us is in trouble, the other can help.” 
“Alright,” Gian agreed. They held their breath, and then, in the trembling leaf-shadow ahead, movement flickered.
Seeing the incoming Bloomfangs, the two charged straight at them, only to veer apart at the last moment.
Kel glanced back. Five of the beasts had locked onto him.
“Of course… I always get the worst luck,” he growled. He caught Gian’s glance and shouted, “Run! Stick to the plan. I’ll handle this! Meet back here!” Then he sprinted harder, leading his pack away.
Gian nodded silently. A Bloomfang lunged at him, claws flashing. He parried, twisted aside, and broke into a dash in the opposite direction.
Two behind me… and one flanking, he thought grimly.
He stopped suddenly, sword raised. The first beast leapt and impaled itself on his blade. The impact knocked him flat, the carcass dragging him down. Before he could recover, a heavy paw slammed into his back, forcing the air from his lungs. He rolled just in time to see another Bloomfang circling, eyes gleaming, fangs dripping, while a third stalked in from the side.
No choice. Gian lunged toward the first creature, still writhing on his sword, trying to wrench the weapon free. But the second beast pounced, claws tearing at the ground. Gian rolled away, barely escaping as it crashed down where he’d stood. 
I need to create some distance first. Heart pounding, he bolted, boots slipping on moss-slick bark as he vaulted a fallen tree half-rotted and soft beneath his weight.
A blur of motion. Pain seared through his arm as a claw raked across his forearm. Blood spattered into the mud, but he didn’t stop. He pushed forward, every step carrying both pain and resolve.

Minutes passed as Gian ran, weaving through trees and brush, deliberately guiding the two chasing feral in a wide arc. He circled back toward the place where he’d left one impaled.
There it was, still alive, staggering weakly, his sword jutting from its flank.
The Bloomfang tried to raise its head, lips peeling back in a wet snarl. Gian didn’t give it the chance. He drove a knee into its skull with a crunch, forcing it back down. Gripping the hilt, he yanked the blade free—steel tearing through flesh with a sick, wet rip. The beast convulsed once, then slumped, twitching in the dirt.
Blade in hand, Gian broke into a sprint.
But he didn’t get far. The brief delay had cost him. One of the pursuing Bloomfangs lunged, claws tearing through his shirt and scraping against his upper arm. The blow was blunted by his chainmail, but pain still flared as the impact forced him into a stumble.
He spun, teeth gritted, just in time to see both beasts closing in, one circling, the other charging.
Then they leapt—together.
Gian dropped low. The two collided midair with a thud of flesh and snapping jaws. Seizing the moment, he surged up and swung, blade cutting deep. One head parted cleanly from its body in a spray of dark blood.
But the second was faster. Before Gian could recover, it crashed into his ribs like a boulder, hurling him into the mud. The breath tore from his lungs.
Coughing, he scrambled up, sword clutched tight, and bolted. 
When Gian reached the meeting point, he had no time to scan for Kel. The remaining Bloomfang was still on him, snapping and circling. His left forearm bled freely, his grip weakening with every swing. With a sudden jerk, the beast caught his blade in its claws and wrenched it away, hurling the sword into the mud.
It lunged, fangs flashing.
Across the clearing, Kel was still locked in combat with another creature. He caught sight of Gian’s peril from the corner of his eye, panic tightening his voice. “I can’t—dammit, I can’t reach him!”
At the last second, Gian dodged to the side. Out of nowhere, the air before him fractured  and a single-edged short sword shimmered into existence before him. His hand closed on it instinctively, and in one smooth motion he slashed upward. The Bloomfang’s head parted from its shoulders in a clean arc, body collapsing at his feet.
Kel froze, dumbstruck. His hesitation nearly cost him; his opponent’s claws ripped through the space he’d just been. He snapped back, teeth clenched, and countered with a vicious sweep of his axe. The impact cracked ribs and sent the beast sprawling several paces away. 
“That was too close,” he growled, advancing on it. He raised the axe high and brought it down, splitting through bone and ending its struggle for good.
Breathing hard, Gian retrieved his sword from the ground. As his fingers closed around the familiar hilt, the mysterious short blade dissolved into thin air, as if it had never existed.
When Gian looked up, Kel was already averting his gaze, hacking at the Bloomfang in front of him, doing his best to act as though nothing unusual had happened.

As Gian approached, Kel kept darting his eyes around, clearly uneasy.
“The stars sure look nice tonight, huh?” he blurted.
“We don’t have time. We need to catch up; they might be in danger. Where are the other Bloomfangs?” Gian asked, scanning the trees behind him. 
“I slowed a few down. They won’t catch up soon. Then I killed this one… and that one,” Kel said, pointing to the carcasses nearby.
Gian studied him. Blood ran from a cut across Kel’s brow, his sleeve was torn, and his left leg carried a fresh wound.
“You alright? Can you still run?” 
“No worries, just some minor scratches. Well… except this arm, but I’ll manage,” Kel replied with a forced grin.
“Alright. Let’s move. Follow me,” Gian turned and sprinted back toward their path.
"Um, so..." Kel said hesitantly as he tried to catch up.
"Not that it's my business, but… what was that?" he asked.
Silence stretched between them, broken only by their footsteps and the hiss of leaves underfoot. Finally, Gian sighed.
“I have a relic. Dimensional storage.”
Kel’s eyes widened. “Damn. That’s awesome. Super handy. Even I was fooled for a second.”
Gian glanced sideways. “What about your axe?”
Under the moonlight, he could see it gleam clean, no trace of blood despite the fresh kills and despite Kel’s own injuries. 
“Wh-what are you talking about?” Kel said, feigning nonchalance.
“You don’t have to tell me if you don’t want to,” Gian said quickly.
Feeling a twinge of guilt, Kel muttered, "…It’s a relic too. Crimson Blood Axe. It grows stronger the more blood it absorbs from enemies. It's been passed down in my family for generations."
“I see. Mine’s this ring.” Gian lifted his right hand, showing the dark band on his middle finger. “Void Ring. Stores and summons small objects instantly.”
Kel winced at the gesture. “Couldn’t you wear that on a different finger, man?”
“Anyway—keep up.”
“I am trying,” Kel muttered, stumbling but refusing to slow.
“No time. We have to protect them.” Gian tightened the cloth around his bleeding forearm and pushed harder into the forest’s shadows. “Just don’t fall behind.”
