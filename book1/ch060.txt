Chapter 60: Whispers from the Camp
The next day, after hunting a Crashstripe for food, the group climbed up the Fallen Cliff.
Sweat dripped down Kel’s forehead as he paused to catch his breath, trying to fix his brown hair while glancing down at the dizzying drop. “To think this is the only climbable part… no one in their right mind would want to live up here.”
Oldman, already resting on a flat rock, fanned himself with his hat. “Actually, there are four known climbable sections on this side of the Fallen Cliff. One is here, usually used by our kingdom for expeditions. Another’s further north—Aethelgard’s preferred route. Then there are two more, way down south near the Gobe Desert.”
“Why would they even send expeditions up here?” Gian asked as he steadied himself on the rough stone.
“Resources,” Oldman replied. “There are mines to be extracted, rare Wildborns to study or capture, and Primeflora with unique properties to harvest.”
Kel squinted at the dense forest stretching out beyond the cliff’s edge. “So… what now? Do we head straight into the forest, or should we check another lesser village first?”
Oldman shook his head. “We’ll stay put for now. I’ll send Tata to scout ahead.” He glanced at the small Driftmouse perched on his shoulder, who let out a soft squeak in response.
“Isn’t it dangerous to let Tata go off alone?” Gian asked, a hint of concern in his voice.
Oldman chuckled. “No need to worry. Tata and I have done this countless times. He’s small, quiet, and fast. It’s safer for him to scout alone. He can hide or escape easily if anything goes wrong.”
Gian nodded. “Alright. We wait, then.”
Oldman carefully lowered Tata onto his hand and spoke softly, “Be careful out there, buddy. Find their base… and see if there’s a safe route or any good hiding spots we can use.”
The little Driftmouse gave a determined squeak before darting off into the trees, vanishing into the undergrowth.
“Be safe,” Oldman muttered, watching as Tata disappeared into the forest.

Perched precariously atop the jagged cliff was a crude bandit camp. At its center stood an unlit bonfire, surrounded by a haphazard sprawl of makeshift tents and rough wooden huts. Behind them loomed a lone wooden house, slightly larger than the rest. Scattered everywhere were piles of loot—chests overflowing with stolen coins, sacks of grain, and weapons stripped from fallen victims. The air carried the faint smell of damp wood, blood, and unwashed bodies.
A short distance away, hidden among the dense branches of a tall tree, Tata crouched silently. His whiskers twitched as he listened intently, his sharp ears catching every word below.
Near the camp’s edge, a mangy dog barked furiously, straining against its leash. A weary-looking bandit stomped toward it, muttering curses, but before he could reach the animal, another voice barked from the bonfire.
“Would you shut that damn dog up already!” snapped a bandit with crooked gold teeth, glaring from where he sat with two others. The three of them were huddled close, their voices low and conspiratorial.
Gold Teeth leaned in. “As I was saying… we should get the hell out of here while we still can.”
The bandit beside him frowned. “Why? And where would we even go?”
“There are plenty of other groups Diego hasn’t touched yet. We could join them, disappear.” Gold Teeth jabbed a thumb over his shoulder toward the wooden house. “Look around, idiots. We’re down to eleven men after the mutiny. Ever since Diego’s so-called right hand turned on him, everything’s been falling apart.”
The other bandit nodded grimly. “You’re not wrong. Something’s… off with boss Diego now. He’s not the same. What’s the point of all this gold and territory if we’re bleeding out men every raid?”
“Don’t call him boss anymore,” Gold Teeth hissed. He lowered his voice even more. “I think… he’s planning to join Anino.”
The other bandit’s eyes widened. “What? He can’t! What’ll happen to us?”
“Shh, you idiot! Keep your damn voice down!” Gold Teeth shot him a sharp glare. “Think for a second. What does it take to get into Anino?”
The third bandit, who’d been silent till now, finally spoke up. “You either get recommended by a member… or you’re invited.”
“Exactly.” Gold Teeth tapped his gold tooth with a fingernail. “Diego doesn’t have the connections to be recommended, so he’s trying to make a name for himself—force them to notice him. Haven’t you two realized? Most of the ones dying in his ‘raids’ are us. The weak ones. The ones with no Echo.”
The second bandit swallowed hard. “…Out of the eleven left, only six of us are without Echo.”
“Right. He’s using these raids as an excuse to weed us out,” Gold Teeth said bitterly. “Anino only takes Echo users. He doesn’t need the rest of us once we’ve served our purpose.”
The panicked bandit glanced around nervously. “So what’s the plan? How do we get out of here before it’s our turn?”

Later that night, while the group sat quietly around their small campfire, Oldman suddenly smiled faintly.
Gian noticed. “What is it?”
“Tata’s back,” Oldman said, standing and turning toward the trees.
Kel immediately grabbed a cup of water and handed it over. Oldman crouched, offering it to the little Driftmouse as it darted into camp and climbed onto his shoulder.
“Welcome back, Tata,” Gian said with a small smile, setting aside the short sword he’d been sharpening.
After a brief moment of silent communication between Oldman and Tata, the older man finally nodded. “I see… so that’s how it is.”
Kel leaned forward eagerly. ‘What is it? Did Tata find them? How many are there?’ he fired in quick succession. 
“Alright, let’s sit. I’ll summarize everything,” Oldman said, letting Tata hop onto the Nightlily to curl up and rest.
They settled around the dim fire as Oldman began. “According to Tata, there were eleven members earlier today. But three of them tried to desert the group.”
“Why?” Gian interrupted, brow furrowed.
“Apparently, those three non-echo bandits felt Diego was deliberately getting his own men killed,” Oldman explained. “They believe he’s thinning out the weak so only the strong, and those with Echoes, remain. Supposedly, Diego’s aiming to join Anino.”
“Anino… what’s that?” Kel asked, puzzled.
“Anino is a network of Echo-wielding criminals,” Oldman said, his tone grim. “Murderers, thieves, smugglers and so on. The only way to join is to be recommended by a member or invited directly.”
Kel muttered, “Great. So he wants to impress them by killing his own men.”
“Whether it’s true or not doesn’t matter. What’s important is that right now there’s a chance. After all that’s happened, only five remain—four Echo users and Diego himself.”
Kel tilted his head. “Wait, I’m confused. You said three decided to leave?”
“Let me finish.” Oldman’s gaze sharpened. “They fled this afternoon, but were discovered. They managed to kill two non-echo bandits during their escape, but Diego and the rest caught them. The deserters were executed. Then Diego killed the last remaining non-echo bandit, saying there was no point keeping a weakling alive.”
Kel gave a short shrug. “Can’t exactly pity them. They were bandits anyway.”
“So five people are left,” Gian muttered quietly.
“Technically four,” Kel corrected, “since Diego’s not really a man.”
Gian turned back to Oldman. “Did Tata learn what Echoes the remaining bandits have? Or at least who they are?”
Oldman nodded. “Tata overheard two names. One is Kelly, who goes by ‘Big K.’ Judging by the nickname, he’s probably a large man. Tata couldn’t learn his Echo.” 
“Big K? What’s next, Little D with an Echo of Dancing?” Kel muttered, smirking.
Oldman continued, voice more serious. “The other is a woman named Belle, a former bandit leader. She’s the only woman left in the group.”
“And her Echo?” Gian asked.
“Tata said her Echo is Song. She creates illusions—confusing her targets, making them turn on each other. That’s how the fleeing deserters ended up killing one another.”
Kel exhaled slowly. “This is going to be… difficult.”
There was a brief silence before Kel finally spoke, glancing between Gian and Oldman.
“So… what do we do?”
“Nothing,” Oldman replied calmly.
“Not yet,” Gian added, his tone firm.
“Tomorrow, I’ll send Tata again,” Oldman continued. “There’s no need to rush. Right now, we’re still in the shadows. They have no idea we already know where their base is. We need to learn more—their names, their Echoes, and their plans.” 
“I agree,” Gian said. “Sooner or later, they’ll have to leave that camp. When they do, someone will stay behind to guard their loot. That’s when we strike.”
Kel nodded. “Sounds like a good plan.”
With that decided, the group settled in for the night. One of them stayed awake on rotation, keeping watch while the others rested under the faint glow of the Flicks.

Over the next few days, Tata continued scouting the bandits’ camp, returning only late at night to report. Thanks to Big K’s talkative nature, the group gradually learned the names of all the bandits and even uncovered the Echoes of some members.
Late on the fourth evening, Tata returned once again. Oldman gently patted the little driftmouse on the head, a rare smile forming on his face.
“This is good news,” he said.
Kel leaned forward eagerly. “What is it?”
“Tomorrow, early morning, Diego will leave the camp with three others—Kelly, Gromo and Belle,” Oldman replied. “They’re heading to another bandit base, apparently to scout and recruit more members.”
Kel frowned. “Recruit? How? Why would anyone join them after everything they’ve done?”
Oldman shrugged. “Who knows where his confidence comes from. Maybe it’s the amount of gold they’ve amassed.”
Kel tapped his chin thoughtfully. “Could it have something to do with Belle? She has the Echo of Song. She can create illusions, right?”
Oldman shook his head. “It’s not that simple, kid. Her illusions are temporary—they’re not true mind control. Anyone with a strong will can break free.”
“Maybe Diego plans to infiltrate the other bandit group, kill their leader, and take over,” Gian said suddenly. “Whatever the case, what matters now is that only one member will be left in the camp.”
“Exactly,” Oldman nodded. “His name is Maro. He has the Echo of Tail, and he’s Belle’s brother.”
Kel said, “Then we strike tomorrow, grab this monkey, and interrogate him.”
The others exchanged determined looks and nodded.
