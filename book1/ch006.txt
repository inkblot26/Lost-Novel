Chapter 6: Balance Broken
Shards of metal rained down like death itself, slicing through flesh and armor alike. Screams split the air as townspeople trampled one another in a frantic bid to escape, blood and dust mixing underfoot. Chaos reigned. No one knew where to run. They only knew the next heartbeat could be their last.
On the roof of a nearby house, a masked figure stood, armor pulsing like wet clay beneath dark violet robes. He raised a single hand, and the ground rumbled. A wall of dense, glistening mud surged upward, sealing off the road to the north gate like a living barricade. Beside him, another masked attacker loosed arrows from a blackened bow with eerie calm. Each shot was mercilessly precise. Each one claimed a life.
One arrow sped toward a woman shielding her child. A patrolman lunged forward, body shimmering with a silvery glow as he activated his Echo of Metal Skin. The arrow struck with a metallic clang and bounced harmlessly off. He grunted, bracing himself.
Gian barely had time to breathe before Elder Eldon yanked him into a narrow alley, voice sharp and urgent. 
“Don’t stop. We’ll circle around the wall—go!”
As they turned the corner, Gian’s peripheral vision caught motion behind them. The silver skin patrolman was still holding the line when the Mud Echo User appeared behind him. With a spear glowing in a radiant yellow halo, he drove it straight through the man’s back. The blade pierced cleanly through his heart. The silver shimmer flickered and died. The patrolman collapsed in silence.
At the center of the street, a handful of other patrolmen fought with desperate courage, even those without Echoes swinging swords with grim determination. But their bravery was no match for the masked attackers. One by one, they fell—felled by arrows, impaled by spikes of hardened mud erupting from the ground.
Gian moved quickly through the chaos, heart pounding, lungs tight. The air was a suffocating mix of ash and panic. Shouts. Footsteps. The metallic crash of overturned carts. He tried not to look at the bodies but his eyes betrayed him, catching every glimpse.
After several grueling minutes, they spotted movement up ahead.
Three patrolmen emerged from a shadowed side street, battered and barely standing. One clutched a deep wound at his side, blood soaking through his uniform. Another limped, skin blistered and raw. The third was missing a hand, blood seeped through a hasty bandage as he waved frantically with his remaining arm.
“Go back!” barked the one holding his side. His voice cracked with exhaustion. “Fires are already spreading! North and west sides are lost!”
Gian’s voice faltered. “Then… what do we do?”
Elder Eldon glanced toward the rising columns of smoke swallowing the night sky. He hesitated only a second before answering grimly.
“It’s too late to escape that way. We head to the center plaza. Move!”
They doubled back through the ruined streets, only to return to the same ground they’d fled earlier, now unrecognizable. Destruction had turned the area into a nightmare of collapsed homes and charred debris. Bodies lay strewn across the path. 
Gian stopped cold.
The woman he’d seen earlier—the one shielding her daughter—was still there. Both of them were now impaled on a jagged mud spike, frozen in that final, hopeless embrace.
The two masked attackers were gone, but the air still echoed with distant screams and the relentless crackle of flames.
More survivors stumbled into view as they moved toward the plaza. Children clung to their parents. The elderly leaned on bloodied neighbors. A few patrolmen herded the desperate crowd together, trying to guide them toward any semblance of safety.
But hope was fleeting.
A piercing screech split the sky.
The Skareth descended.
In an instant, a rain of razor-sharp feather blades fell from above, slicing through the crowd.
“DOWN!” Elder Eldon roared, summoning a thick veil of smoke with a snap of his cane. The haze swirled upward, momentarily masking their position. He grabbed Gian’s arm and dragged him into the ruins of a half-collapsed house.
Gian hit the floor hard, gasping. He turned toward Eldon—and froze.
The elder leaned against the wall, blood streaming from a jagged feather lodged deep in his side.
“Damn thing got me…” Eldon muttered through gritted teeth, forcing a strained grin. “You’ve got some luck, kid.”
Only one patrolman had managed to follow them inside, the one missing a hand. But even he was wounded again, his remaining arm slashed open by a blade. Blood pooled rapidly beneath him.
Gian knelt between them, breath uneven, his gaze flicking toward the shattered doorway. Outside lay a waking horror. Bodies lay sprawled across the street, feather blades pinning them like slaughtered animals. Some still moved, crawling, gasping, their broken cries muffled beneath the ominous silence.
And above it all, the Skareth hovered.
Its massive wings blotted out what little light remained. Then, just for a heartbeat, it turned its head and locked its golden eyes on Gian.
Cold dread coiled in his chest.
There was no rage in the creature’s gaze. No hatred. Just an empty, knowing presence, as if it could see something far beyond him. Then, with one mighty beat of its wings, it vanished into the sky like a ghost. 
“I… I think we should stay inside,” Gian whispered. His voice was thin, fragile. “The fires are still far enough. Maybe this place will hold.”
He scanned the room. The house was damaged but clung to a fragile balance. One strong gust or tremor could bring it all down.

At the center plaza, the battle reached a strange pause.
The remaining soldiers, patrolmen, and Echo-users had formed a defensive line. Flames crackled at the edges of the square, but the mayor, wielding the Echo of Ice, had managed to halt their advance. A shimmering wall of frost now hissed and steamed where fire once roared. Beside him stood the captain of the soldiers and what remained of the fighters—bloodied, wounded, but still standing. Their eyes burned with quiet defiance.
A masked swordsman in a dark violet robe stepped forward from across the square. He moved with deliberate calm, untouched by the chaos around him. His sheathed sword swayed loosely at his side. Behind him, three more figures in violet robes stood perfectly still, their presence oppressive but clearly beneath his.
Above them, a Skareth loomed silently, its unnatural shadow stretching across the plaza.
The captain raised his blade, shouting across the smoky air. “Who are you?! Why did you attack this town?!”
“Do you not fear retaliation from the Kingdom of Lanthea?” the mayor added coldly, eyes narrowed.
The masked swordsman stopped a few paces away. When he spoke, his voice was smooth, calm—almost gentle.
“Why would we fear that?”
He took another step forward, his gaze shifting not to the mayor, not to the captain, but to someone standing behind him. 
“You’re the ones making the mistake.” His eyes locked on Warren.
Warren froze, face tightening.
“You bring imbalance to this world,” the masked man said, “secretly using a power to create unimaginable wealth—an Echo that should not exist.”
The mayor flinched, just slightly, but enough to be noticed.
