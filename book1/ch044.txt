Chapter 44: Stand Until the End
Down below, Richard had gone mad. Adrenaline surged through him, making him forget the pain of his severed hand. Cackling with rage, he launched flaming birds one after another toward Gian.
Struggling to keep up, Gian deflected each blazing projectile with the buckler strapped to his left arm. The room was narrow, with shattered furniture and scorched wood underfoot, forcing him to weave carefully to avoid tripping. I need to create an opening… keep his attention away from Oldman.
He began backing into a nearby room, baiting Richard to follow. Mind clouded by fury, Richard took the bait and charged in, screaming curses, threats, and broken laughter. 
Meanwhile, Tata had reached Oldman, who was groaning as he struggled to push his leg off the massive stone spike that had impaled it. Blood pooled beneath him. Breathing heavily, Oldman summoned his Echo of Healing. Tiny yellow-green fireflies shimmered into existence, circling his leg before slowly stitching flesh and sinew back together.
Tata squeaked frantically, circling him.
Oldman gritted his teeth and tried to smile. “I’m not dying, Tata. Don’t panic. Go help Gian, I’ll manage. We’ve been through worse, haven’t we?”
Tata stared at him for a moment longer, then gave a determined squeak and darted toward the room where Gian and Richard were now fighting.
Inside the compact room, Gian struggled to block Richard’s relentless barrage. One of the flaming birds curved mid-air, slipping past his buckler, and seared his back. He staggered, thrown off balance as another struck him square in the chest, the heat scorching his skin. 
“This is the end!” Richard roared.
But before he could release another attack, a loud squeak rang out. Richard turned and saw Tata at the doorway, baring its small teeth, fearless.
“You dare challenge a noble Highlord?” Richard snarled. “You’re just a pathetic Wildborn!”
He raised his arm and fired another flaming bird—this one curving midair, homing in on Tata. The driftmouse darted to the side, trying to outrun it.
Seizing the distraction, Gian gritted his teeth through the pain and charged. He raised his sword and slashed, but Richard lifted his remaining hand and activated his Echo of Rot. The blade corroded instantly, crumbling to useless shards.
The flaming bird, now without Richard’s guidance, veered off and missed Tata entirely.
“You fool,” Richard hissed. “You think getting close gives you a chance? I wield the rare Echo of Rot! My touch destroys everything!”

He seized Gian's buckler, expecting it to rot apart beneath his fingers.
Nothing happened.
His eyes widened.
Is this… a relic? he thought, stunned.
Gian didn’t hesitate. He summoned a short sword and, with a powerful strike, severed Richard’s last remaining hand. Screaming, the Highlord stumbled back and fled the room.

On the second floor, Kel approached the struggling Sunquill. Something changed. The Rootseal on its neck began to wither and crack.
The Wildborn’s eyes cleared as it regained its freedom.
The Sunquill fixed its gaze on Kel. Despite its injured wings, it rose slowly, beating against the air before gliding through the shattered window and into the open sky. 
From below, Richard saw the wildborn leaving.
“No—come back! Come back here, you traitor! Help me!”
But Gian caught up behind him.
“You’ve gotten weak—that’s why it rejected your connection,” he said coldly. “That Wildborn never respected you. It never cared.”
Richard spun, eyes burning with fury. He opened his mouth, but Gian’s blade was faster, slicing through his neck before the words could form.
The Highlord’s head hit the ground a moment before his body crumpled.

Gian rushed to Oldman, who was lying beside the stone spike that still stood rigidly from the floor, Tata perched protectively on his chest.
“I’m good. Don’t worry,” Oldman said, his voice weak but steady. “Closed the wound already, but I’m spent. I need to rest. Go check on the others.”
Gian nodded, then turned and ran upstairs.
There, he found Michael slumped against the wall, his body covered in cuts and bruises but still breathing. A short distance away, Kel was leaning heavily against the opposite wall, his eyes closed, skin pale and slick with sweat.
“Are you alright?” Gian asked, alarmed by the blood loss evident in Kel’s face.
Kel struggled to remain conscious, answering with a faint voice, eyes still shut. “Yeah... just used too much blood. Head’s spinning. What’s the situation?”
“Richard’s dead. The Wildborn escaped. Vince... didn’t make it.” Gian clenched his jaw. “Hang in there. I’ll send another message for help.”
“The girls…” Kel muttered, his voice nearly lost to exhaustion.
“I’ll check on them. Just rest,” Gian assured, then ran to the room where they’d hidden.
He knocked gently. “It’s me. It’s safe now.”
After a few seconds, the door cracked open. The brave girl who had stood her ground earlier peeked out. Her eyes widened when she saw Gian—bloodied, burned, and bruised.
“Are you all okay in there?” he asked gently.
“Yes, sir. Is it... really over?” she asked, glancing past him toward the hallway.
“It is. But stay inside a little longer, just in case,” Gian said, subtly shifting his body to block her view of the aftermath.
She nodded, and he closed the door behind her.
Turning, Gian walked back into the ruined hallway, eyes scanning the wreckage. Then, through the shattered window, something caught his attention—two distant silhouettes, flying toward the mansion fast.
Tension surged in his chest.
Without hesitation, Gian dashed outside. He readied his longbow and arrow, buckler strapped to his left arm, short sword sheathed at his hip. Whatever was coming, he would be ready for it.

After a while, Gian saw them—two men riding translucent eagles formed from an Echo of Pack. The birds descended and landed in front of him. As the eagles touched the ground, they dissolved, and the two men approached.
“Gian, what’s the situation here?” Sam asked, scanning the area with sharp eyes.
“We succeeded in capturing Ronald. He’s tied up and locked in one of the rooms. But… some didn’t make it,” Gian said, voice low. “Only Kel, Michael, Oldman, and I are still alive.”
He glanced back toward the mansion, anxiety creeping into his voice. “The three of them need treatment, badly.”
“I see,” Sam replied. “Don’t worry. This is Sergeant Will from the camp near Richard’s mansion. He has both an Echo of Pack and Heal. Highlady Rose sent a message that they won and ordered us to check on you. Others will follow shortly—we just rushed ahead.”
The three of them quickly headed inside.
“How did things go on your end?” Gian asked as they moved.
“All went well,” Sam said. “Thanks to Leo’s Echo, we managed to trap Sergeant Will and most of the soldiers at the camp. That gave Highlady Rose a clear shot at the mansion. She won and got the Ring of Authority. But that bastard Richard managed to esc—”
He froze mid-sentence. His eyes locked onto a bloodied, severed head lying on the floor.
“Is that…?”
“Yeah,” Gian said grimly. “That’s Richard. He showed up after we captured the mansion. We managed to take him down.”
Sam stared for a moment, then let out a long breath. “Damn. I don’t know if you’re insanely lucky or just cursed, crossing paths with him like that.”
Meanwhile, Sergeant Will knelt beside Oldman, checking his pulse and vitals. “He’ll be fine,” the healer assessed. “Just needs rest.”
“Michael and Kel are upstairs,” Gian added quickly. “They need urgent help.”
Will nodded and rushed off without hesitation.
Sam watched Sergeant Will ascend the stairs quickly, disappearing behind the landing. He nodded toward the quiet, damaged hallway. “Well, your luck isn’t entirely bad. Having Sergeant Will nearby might’ve just saved their lives.”
Gian gave a tired nod. “Yeah… I guess so.”
