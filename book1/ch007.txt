Chapter 7: Just a second
The fires crept closer, licking the edges of the ruins with hungry tongues. The sky above glowed orange, thick with smoke. Elder Eldon pressed his hand to the bleeding wound at his side and turned to Gian.
“We can’t stay here. The Skareth’s gone for now. That’s our chance.”
Gian nodded hesitantly, then turned toward the patrolman who had followed them in, the one missing a hand. He crouched beside him, gently patting his shoulder. 
“Hey… can you stand? We need to get out of here.”
There was no answer. The man slumped lifelessly, his breathing long since gone.
“It’s no use,” Eldon said quietly from the doorway. “He’s gone.”
He stepped outside, boots crunching softly over broken glass and ash. His gaze swept the street—bodies everywhere. Friends. Neighbors. People he’d watched grow up. A weight settled on him heavier than any wound. He hadn’t been strong enough to protect them.
If only I were younger… not trapped in this old body, he thought, guilt gnawing at him. He knew he could have done more if he were just a few years younger.
They continued on in grim silence.
After some time, they came upon a small crowd gathered outside a large, half-intact house. Murmured voices drifted through the smoke—tired, cautious, desperate.
“Elder Eldon… you’re alive,” one man said, relief fragile in his voice, as they approached.
“If you’re badly injured, sir, there’s space inside. We’re treating what survivors we can, prioritizing those who need it most,” offered a woman in a patrol uniform, her arm tightly bound in a sling.
Eldon gave her a weary nod. Gian, meanwhile, peered into the house, then froze. 
“Mother?”
He rushed in.
Helen was kneeling beside a child, her hands glowing faintly with a soft, healing light. The light flickered softly as the last traces of the wound closed. Just as she was finishing, her eyes widened the moment she saw Gian.
“Gian!” she gasped. Her voice trembled as she rose and pulled him into a tight embrace. “You’re alive…”
Gian clung to her, a wave of relief crashing over him. “You’re safe… I thought I’d lost you.”
She cupped his face, brushing dirt from his cheek, and noticed the gash above his brow. “You’re hurt.”
“It’s nothing,” Gian said quickly. “I’m fine.”
Helen pressed her hand to the wound. Her healing Echo sealed it in moments.
“Where’s Gideon? Have you seen him?”
Gian shook his head.
“I was going to ask you. I haven’t seen him since… since it all started.”
Helen’s face darkened.
“I don’t know. I stayed here to help the wounded with the other healers. We haven’t left since the attack began.”
“Don’t worry, Mother. I’m sure he’s okay—and right now, he’s out there, fighting those intruders, protecting our town,” Gian said, full of confidence.
Helen just nodded, saying nothing. Fear for the safety of her eldest son weighed heavily on her.

"After a few minutes, a commotion stirred near the doorway. A breathless patrolman, wide-eyed, rushed toward Elder Eldon, who was still receiving treatment." 
“Elder Eldon, we’ve got a problem. One of the masked men in the violet robes is approaching. He’s… coming alone.”
A ripple of panic spread. Parents instinctively clutched their children; whispers died, replaced by fearful silence.
Elder Eldon, having only healed some of his wounds, straightened, wincing but steady. His voice rang out, clear and commanding.
“Everyone who can fight, form up with me.”
Then he turned to Gian, his tone hardening.
“Stay with your mother. Don’t come out. No matter what.”

Outside, twenty-two people had gathered. Only four patrolmen with Echoes remained. One had stone-hardened skin using the Echo of Stone. Another wielded the Echo of Lightning. The injured spearwoman, her arm in a sling, used the Echo of Ice. And a young man had the Echo of Focus. The rest were civilians and non-Echo patrolmen, clutching bows or swords.
Eldon stepped forward, scanning them. “Some of you don’t have Echoes. Pair up—sword bearers protect the archers. Archers, keep your distance and strike only with a clear shot. Echo Users, flank me. We stay coordinated. He may be one man, but he won’t be easy. Understood?”
“Yes, sir!” came the chorus, voices shaking but determined.
“We can do this!” one patrolman shouted, trying to rally morale.
“He’s alone,” someone muttered. “And we’ve got Elder Eldon.”
But then the air shifted.
The masked swordsman stepped into view, tall, silent, cloaked in dark violet. In one hand, his blade burned with roaring flames that painted dancing shadows across the scorched ground.
Elder Eldon raised his cane sword, smoke already curling from its tip. His wound still bled, but his grip did not falter.
“Positions!” he barked.
The fighters moved. The stone-skin muscular patrolman took point beside Eldon, arms like shields of granite. Behind them, the short patrolman readied his twin daggers, arcs of electricity humming faintly between his fingers. The spearwoman steadied herself despite her injury, taking the rear. Archers nocked their arrows. The young man with the Echo of Focus closed his eyes, breathing slowly—his pupils dilated, the world sharpening to crystal clarity.
The masked man struck first, lunging with an explosive burst of flame. His sword fell like a shooting star. Eldon pivoted, smoke billowing from his cane sword, forming a twisting veil. The fiery blade sliced through but met nothing. Eldon reappeared behind him, landing a shallow cut along the attacker’s side—barely a scratch.
The masked man spun, unleashing a blazing shockwave. The stone-skin threw himself in front, absorbing the brunt. Heat scorched his arms, but he held firm, slamming a stone fist toward the enemy’s chest.
The masked man slipped aside effortlessly.
Arrows flew from the flanks, but most were deflected with a casual swipe of the burning blade. The short patrolman darted in, blades crackling. He slashed diagonally, but his attack met the flaming sword.
Then, with his free hand, the masked man released another blast of fire. The patrolman barely dodged, flames grazing his shoulder. He stumbled back, burns smoking.
Seizing the moment, the young man with the Focus Echo closed in. In his heightened state, time slowed; every shift in the masked man’s weight, every flicker of motion, was clear. He sidestepped and flashed his sword toward the enemy’s neck.
But a sudden wall of ice erupted, blocking the strike.
“He has two Echoes—fire and ice! Be careful!” Eldon shouted.
“That was close,” the masked man said with a smirk beneath his jagged mask, his voice cold. “To think I'd be forced to use my ice. I guess I’ll have to finish you off first.”
This is bad, Elder Eldon thought, jumping back.
Suddenly, the masked man unleashed a massive burst of flame aimed directly at the young man.
The injured spear woman reacted instinctively, raising a wall of ice in front of herself, the young man, and the short patrolman. It blocked most of the blast, but the stone-skin patrolman was too far. The flames engulfed him. He screamed as fire consumed him, and before he could react, a flaming sword pierced his heart.
The lightning user, burned and panicked, dropped one dagger. His eyes darted toward the alleys.
“We’ll all die here… I need to—”
Ice darts struck him clean through the back and head. He collapsed mid-step.
At the same time, sharp ice darts fanned outward toward the archers and sword bearers. They barely had time to scream before they were struck—heads, chests, throats. They fell like ragdolls.
Elder Eldon gritted his teeth. In mere moments, half their fighters were gone. He turned to the spear woman and the young man.
"You have the Echo of Focus, right?" he asked quickly.
"Yes, but it only lasts for a short time," the young man answered, his voice tight.
"That's good enough. I’ll veil this area with smoke to obscure his vision," Eldon said, already spreading thick smoke from the tip of his cane sword.
"But how can we all attack if we can’t see him clearly?" the spear woman interjected.
"It’s the same either way," Eldon responded firmly. "Look, the others are dead, and the ones still alive can’t even land an arrow from a distance. Only the three of us can still fight. And I need you focused on defense."
He turned to the young man. "I’ll keep attacking him inside the smoke. I can see through it. You, use your heightened senses to strike when you see an opening. Time your attacks. Go for a killing blow if you can.”
Smoke swirled like a living thing, thick and impenetrable to most eyes. Elder Eldon moved through it like a phantom, his cane sword gliding in sharp, precise arcs. Every strike was meant to pressure, not kill. He wasn’t trying to win yet. He was stalling, baiting, reading.
The masked man countered with fiery sweeps, slicing through the haze, but Eldon vanished into the smoke again, reappearing at his flank with a shallow cut across his shoulder.
“Is hiding behind all this smoke the best you can do?” the masked man hissed, flames bursting from his free hand in a wide arc.
Eldon answered with a sudden blast of compressed smoke, absorbing the heat and slipping behind a crumbled pillar.
From the edge of the haze, the young man with the Echo of Focus steadied his breathing. His senses sharpened, and the world slowed. 
Then he saw it.
A misstep. The masked man, for just a second, overextended, leaving his right side exposed.
Now!
With a shout, the young man rushed forward, sword flashing in perfect rhythm with his heartbeat. Every motion calculated. Every muscle honed. He saw the masked man's weakness and moved in to exploit it.
But it was a trap.
The masked man twisted just as the blade neared, revealing a grin beneath the mask. His left hand formed a blade of frost and drove it straight into the young man’s stomach.
The light in the youth’s eyes flickered. His sword clattered to the ground.
"No!" Eldon roared, smoke lashing wildly as he surged forward, but it was too late. The young man fell to his knees, the fire dying from his eyes before he collapsed fully into the smoke-covered dirt.
The masked man looked toward Eldon, flames dancing at his side.
"That one had potential," he said coldly.
"But potential means nothing if you can't see the kill coming."
