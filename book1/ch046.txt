Chapter 46: Let the City Rest
Three nights after the assault, inside the main dining chamber of Highlady Rose’s modest mansion in the Northern District, Lord Henry, Torren, Michael, Gian, and Kel sat around a long wooden table. Oldman leaned quietly against the wall beside one of the tall windows overlooking the quiet street outside.
The door swung open as Highlady Rose entered, Ezra at her side. Behind them followed Sam and a broad-shouldered man named Dareth, his right sleeve pinned neatly at the shoulder where his arm had once been.
“Good evening, everyone,” Highlady Rose greeted as she approached the table.
“Good evening, Highlady Rose,” Lord Henry and the others responded in unison as they stood. 
“I want to express my deep gratitude to all of you for helping us reclaim this city,” Rose said, bowing deeply. “And please accept my apology for only meeting with you now.”
Rose gestured toward the chairs arranged along both sides of the table, and they all took their seats again. 
“It’s quite alright, Highlady Rose. We know you’ve been busy managing everything—meeting people, sending letters, calming the people of Tillara,” Lord Henry replied kindly. 
“You must be exhausted from dealing with so many people,” Torren added.
“Yes... It’s not something I’m used to,” Rose admitted with a small smile. “Tomorrow at noon, I’ll be traveling to the capital city, Liora, to report directly to the king. I’ve been summoned.” 
“I see,” Henry nodded. “Then the king will officially announce you as the new ruler to the other nobles.”
“Wait, if you're gone, Highlady Rose… who’ll be in charge here?” Kel asked, confused, as he popped a grape into his mouth.
“That brings me to my next point,” she said, turning to Lord Henry. “If you're willing, Sir Henry, I would like to appoint you as my Right Hand to help lead the city.” 
“Whoa, that’s the second-highest power in the city,” Kel said bluntly.
“It also comes with enormous responsibility,” Michael said with a casual shrug, reaching for an apple. Torren elbowed him sharply before he could take a bite. 
“I understand this is a lot to ask,” Rose continued, “especially after you’ve just helped me overthrow my brother. That’s why I’m offering it as a choice. You can take your time and decide.”
Henry thought of his late wife and smiled faintly. “I know it won’t be easy. But if Eva were here… she’d tell me to accept. She always believed that those in a position to help have the duty to do so.”
If only the world truly worked like that, Gian thought silently.
“Guess I’ll have even more errands to run now,” Michael muttered.
Henry stood and bowed. “I will gladly accept this responsibility, Highlady Rose.”
Rose smiled warmly. “Thank you, Sir Henry.”
She gestured toward the spread of food laid out across the center of the table—platters of roasted meat, bowls of fruit, fresh bread, and pitchers of water. “Please, everyone, let’s eat.” 
“Well, someone already started,” Oldman said as he took a seat.
Kel and Michael glanced at each other from opposite sides of the table, both already holding food they had quietly taken.

After a while, as the conversation softened and plates had shifted across the table, Kel continued eating roasted chicken when Lord Henry spoke up. 
“What are your plans now, Gian? Kel?”
Kel answered first between bites. “Well, I want to sleep, explore the city, and rest for a long time… but I don’t think that’s possible,” he said, glancing at Gian.
Henry looked at Gian thoughtfully. “I know there’s something you need to do. But I think you should take time to rest, to settle down for a while and enjoy what peace we have. You don’t have to leave the city right away, do you?” 
“No, Lord Henry,” Gian replied. “I’m thinking of staying here for at least four or five months.”
“What?!” Kel said, shocked, nearly dropping his food. “You told me you wanted to find a mission and leave as soon as possible.”
“That was before,” Gian said quietly, looking down at his hand. “I knew I wasn’t that strong when I left Tala, but I didn’t realize just how weak I really am. I need to train more, sharpen myself before my next journey.”
Oldman glanced at Gian, his expression unreadable.
“What do you mean weak? You’ve survived multiple battles already,” Torren pointed out.
“Yeah,” Michael added, dipping a slice of meat in a creamy sauce. “And you’re what, eighteen? Nineteen? I was still messing around at that age.” 
“I only survived because of the help of others,” Gian said seriously, his voice steady as memories of the past few days crossed his mind.
Rose leaned forward, smiling gently. “It’s true that strength matters, Gian. But never think that relying on others is weakness. Look at me.”
She gestured lightly around the room. “None of this would’ve been possible without you, and without the sacrifices of those who stood beside us.”
Gian nodded with respect. “Yes, Highlady Rose.”
“Are you and Kel still planning to go after those masked men?” Oldman asked Gian.
“Yes,” Gian replied firmly, and Kel nodded beside him. “We’ll keep searching for them.”
“I see…” Oldman said quietly. Then, after a moment, he added with a serious look, “Can I join you?”
Gian and Kel exchanged a glance.
“Yeah, why not?” Gian said, nodding. “We’re after the same goal.”
“And it’d be a huge help having someone like you with us,” Kel added with a grin. “A healer and scout that strong? No way we’d say no.”
"Oldman nodded and gave a smile. “Thanks.”
Rose, who had been listening quietly, finally spoke. “Does this have anything to do with what you were trying to learn from Ronald?”
Gian, Kel, and Oldman looked at each other, then nodded.
Kel leaned forward over the table, lowering his voice slightly as the candles flickered between them. He began to recount everything—the events at the Eclipse of Tala, the assault on Ronald’s mansion, and the mysterious group of masked men in dark violet robes.
“We think those masked men in violet robes are part of the Verdant Keepers,” Kel added.
“Their attack likely had something to do with what that assistant learned about them,” Oldman said.
“Probably an attempt to silence him… to stop him from digging any deeper,” Gian followed.
Rose exhaled, her eyes narrowing. “I see. So my foolish brother was merely caught up in something larger...”
She paused, then continued firmly, “But that doesn't erase the sins he committed against this city and its people. By law, his crimes are punishable by death.”
“Tomorrow morning, we’ll carry out the sentence,” she said. “But I ask one thing, Oldman—please, no more torture. Let the law deliver justice now.”
Oldman nodded slowly. “As you wish, Lady Rose. My hatred for him has settled. What I seek now is justice… from those masked men.” Fire burned in his gaze.
Rose gave a small smile. “Thank you.”
“Gian, Kel... please be very careful about this,” Lord Henry said, his expression serious and full of concern.
“Based on what you've told us, this isn’t an ordinary organization. I won’t stop you, but I urge you to be cautious. Treasure your lives. Know yourselves. Know your limits. Don’t act unless you're confident. Bid your time.” 
“Yes, Lord Henry,” both Gian and Kel replied, nodding gratefully.
“I’ll also try to gather information about those men,” Rose added.
“I don’t think that’s a good idea, Highlady Rose. It might be dangerous,” Gian said with concern.
“He’s right, Lady Rose. You don’t need to involve yourself in this,” Oldman agreed.
Rose gave a soft smile. “When I had nothing—when I was weak and just beginning my revolt with barely ten people—you stood by me, Oldman.”
Ezra chuckled. “We weren’t afraid back then. Why would we be afraid now?”
“Exactly,” Dareth added with a grin. “And besides, we’ve got soldiers under Highlady Rose’s command now. You think anyone would dare attack this city?”
Kel, leaning back in his chair, smirked. “Well… we did attack this city’s former Highlord.”
That drew a round of laughter around the table, the earlier tension easing as the sound filled the dining chamber. 
“Don’t worry,” Rose said warmly. “I’ll keep my efforts quiet. Only those of us in this room will know about it.”
“Thank you, Highlady Rose,” Oldman, Gian, and Kel said in unison, bowing their heads.
“I don’t have much to offer,” Lord Henry said, tossing his short staff to Gian and Kel. “But take this. It’s yours.”
Kel caught it, and both he and Gian looked down at the relic. It was dark in color, fully inscribed with unfamiliar symbols.
“We don't deserve this, Lord Henry,” Gian said, refusing politely, while Kel also shook his head adamantly.
“If you two don't deserve it, then no one does,” Henry laughed.
“You’ve helped me and my family more than once. That night, when Clara and Cedric escaped... they wouldn’t have made it without you. I wanted to give you this back then, but I still needed it for our fight with Richard,” he said softly. 
“Now that I know you're going to do something dangerous, let me at least do this.”
“Thank you, Lord Henry,” Gian and Kel said in unison.
“It’s settled then. You’re welcome to stay as long as you wish. There are several guest rooms prepared upstairs,” Highlady Rose said with a warm smile toward Gian, Kel, and Oldman. 
“Thank you for your offer, Highlady Rose, but there’s no need. We can stay at Lord Henry’s mansion,” Gian replied.
“I see. Then I’ll go now—I need to prepare some things before I leave tomorrow,” Rose said as she stood up.
The rest followed and also rose to their feet.
“Sir Henry, there’s no need to rush into governing the city. I have someone who can handle that for now,” she said warmly.
“You should rest for a while and spend time with your family. I heard they’re now traveling back to Tillara. Enjoy your time with them. We can talk about the city when I return.”
Then she turned to Oldman. “I’ll have someone gather information about those men and inform you three as soon as I get something. I’ll also assign some men to prepare what you’ll need for your journey—equipment, weapons, anything useful.”
“Thank you, Highlady Rose,” the trio said, bowing slightly.
Smiling, Rose glanced down at the two rings on her hand—one the Ring of Authority, the other her late mother’s ring catching the candlelight. 
“Thank you, everyone… for aiding me in freeing Tillara City.”
