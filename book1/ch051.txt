Chapter 51: A Taste of the Unknown 
After a few days of walking, they finally reached the edge of the Doom Forest. From the open fields, they could clearly see the treeline ahead—towering trees packed tightly together, their leaves a mix of deep red, muted yellow, and dark green. A thin layer of mist lingered beneath the canopy, softening the forest floor from view. 
“Well… even from here I can see why it’s called the Doom Forest. It’s kinda spooky just looking at it,” Kel muttered as the group paused on a patch of flattened grass near the treeline. 
“You’ll get used to it,” Thompson chuckled, though his laugh carried a faint edge.
Jessica, arms folded as she stared hard at the vast, shadowed forest ahead, added quietly, “Cherish this peaceful moment, Mr. Gentleman. Once we step in there, anything can happen.”
Oldman handed a water jug to Kel. “She’s right. In there, it’s all chance. You might be lucky and see nothing… or be surrounded the moment you enter.”
Before Kel could reply, Gian stiffened. “Hey… what’s that?” He stood abruptly, pointing toward four figures in the distance.
They weren’t wearing Tillara military green uniforms.
Thompson’s usual carefree demeanor vanished instantly. His gaze sharpened. “Probably mercenaries or adventurers. Donny, check on them.”
“Yes, sir.” Donny moved out with two non-Echo soldiers, weapons ready.
After several minutes, Donny and the two soldiers jogged back across the field and saluted. “They’re an adventurer team, sir. Originally ten members, but… four days ago, they were ambushed by an Ascendant Wildborn in the night. It killed some of them. They panicked, fled in the dark, and ran into more Wildborn. Most of the team didn’t make it.”
Oldman grimaced. “An Ascendant? That’s bad luck.”
“What kind?” Thompson asked sharply.
“A massive Duskfang, sir,” Donny explained. “It slipped silently into their camp, coiled around their leader, then released a poisonous fume that choked and blinded them. That’s when the panic started.”
Kel leaned closer to Oldman and whispered, “Uh… what does a Duskfang look like?”
Oldman’s voice was low. “Think of a giant cobra, its scaled body several meters long, with a hood marked by dark, swirling patterns. It’s fast… and very patient.”
Kel swallowed hard, glancing at Gian. “Yeah… Doom Forest really is a fitting name, huh?”
“Yeah, we always need to stay alert.” Gian replied.
“Don’t worry, we have Tata. It’s got a sharp sense for anything around us,” Oldman assured as the small driftmouse straightened on his shoulder, puffing its tiny chest proudly.
Just then, a translucent rabbit bounded toward them, carrying a piece of paper in its mouth.
“It really is kinda cute,” Kel commented as it hopped closer.
“Right?” Jessica chuckled softly.
Thompson crouched, taking the letter from the creature. As soon as he touched it, the rabbit dissolved. His eyes scanned the message, and his expression shifted immediately. 
“This is bad. We need to move. Now.”
Gian straightened. “What happened?”
“Jimmy reports that Baron spotted a small flock of Scorchbeaks heading this way with his echo,” Thompson said quickly. “If they catch us out in the open, it’ll get ugly fast. We need to reach the forest for cover before they see us.”
His voice sharp, he barked the order. “Move! Double time!”
The soldiers quickly gathered their gear and started running.
“Hey—what kind of Wildborn are those?” Kel asked between breaths as he tried to keep pace.
Jessica glanced at him briefly while jogging. “It’s a large, vulture-like Wildborn native to these parts.”
Oldman added, “They usually move in small flocks of at least four.”
Thompson spoke without slowing down, “They’re not too hard to kill individually, but they’re avian. The problem is hitting them before they dive-bomb you.”
Gian, still running, asked, “What about those adventurers back there?”
There was a brief silence before Oldman sighed. “Gian… you’re a good guy, but you can’t protect everyone. They’re adventurers. They knew the risks of entering the Unknown.”
Thompson’s tone was firm. “And I’m not sending Donny back out there. They’re still a ways off. Even if he reached them, how would he get back to us safely? No. We keep moving.”
Kel muttered, “Maybe we could just shout?”
Oldman shook his head. “Not at this distance. They wouldn’t hear.”
Gian hesitated for a moment, glancing back at the four men far behind, but then turned his eyes forward and kept running.
After several minutes of running, they reached the shade of the first line of trees, where Baron and Jimmy stood just inside the forest’s edge. 
“How’s the situation?” Thompson asked.
“Just in time, sir. The flock is almost here,” Baron replied, his irises faintly shimmering as he focused on movements only he could see through his Echo of Perception. 
“Alright, listen up,” Thompson said, turning to his unit. “Our mission is to escort these three halfway to Graylatch Village. Engaging Wildborns isn’t a priority—avoid combat if possible.”
“Yes, sir,” the soldiers answered in unison.
“He’s right,” Oldman added, glancing at Gian and Kel. “I know you two are eager to test your strength, but out here in the Unknown, the first rule is survival. Avoid unnecessary battles whenever you can.”
Both nodded silently.
Squeak.
Oldman patted Tata gently on his shoulder. “No need for you to scout, little one. Save your strength. We’ll need you when it’s just the three of us again.”

After hours of trekking beneath tightly interwoven branches, just before afternoon, the group paused in a small clearing where filtered sunlight reached the forest floor. That’s when Baron emerged from the trees, his breathing steady but his expression tense.
“Sir, we have a problem,” he reported to Sergeant Thompson. “A troop of Apelang is moving this way. We can’t avoid them. They're moving through the canopy on both sides. If we retreat, they'll catch us before we reach another clearing.”
Thompson immediately stood. “How many?”
“Not sure. Those nimble long-limbed, monkey-like Wildborn blend seamlessly into the forest with their mottled brown and green fur. It’s hard to get an exact count,” Baron replied. 
“Alright, everyone, prepare yourselves,” Thompson ordered sharply, signaling his soldiers to ready their weapons. Then he turned to Gian, Kel, and Oldman. “We’ll have to fight this one. No way around it.”
Oldman cracked a grin, pulling the Stikk relic out. “No problem. We knew we’d meet a Wildborn sooner or later.”
Gian’s sharp eyes caught movement high in the treetops above the clearing as dark shapes leapt from branch to branch between thick, twisting limbs. Without hesitation, he drew an arrow from his black longbow and loosed it. The shaft flew true, striking one Apelang mid-jump and sending it crashing to the forest floor. 
Almost instantly, more arrows followed. Baron’s summoned translucent bow and the real bows of two non-Echo soldiers released in perfect rhythm. Three more Apelang were struck, their shrill cries echoing through the trees.
The rest of the troop let out furious screeches, their glowing yellow eyes now fixated on Gian’s direction. The noise rose to a frenzy as they sped up, swinging closer with terrifying agility.
“Brace yourselves!” Thompson shouted. Around him, star-shaped blades of spinning water formed, orbiting his body like a deadly halo. In his hand, he summoned a long translucent spear with his second echo, its tip glinting coldly.
Oldman chuckled, rolling his shoulders. “Don’t get yourselves hurt too easily, boys. I’d rather not waste my Echo this early.” Nearby, Tata scurried into a bush for safety.
“As if!” Kel grinned, hefting his Crimson Blood Axe. “Let them come!”
Gian stayed silent, his focus unshakable. Arrow after arrow flew from his bow, each shot precise, wounding more of the Apelang before they could close the distance.
Then, one by one, the troop of screeching Apelang dropped from the surrounding branches into the clearing, closing the distance rapidly. The soldiers clashed with them in a frenzy of blades and Echoes.
Gian switched to his short sword and rushed the Apelang in front of him. Its whip-like tail lashed out, fast and vicious, but Gian blocked it with his Morphguard in buckler form. Seizing the moment, he raised his shortsword and sliced clean through one of its elongated arms. The creature shrieked in pain and stumbled back. 
“You’re not getting away,” Gian growled. He followed through with a horizontal slash aimed at the neck, beheading the creature before it could retreat.
He quickly turned to aid Kel, who was fending off two Apelang at once. Kel blocked the whipping tail of one with the shaft of his Crimson Blood Axe. Gian moved behind it and drove his blade through its back. In the same breath, Kel pulled a throwing axe from his back and hurled it at the second Apelang, burying the blade straight into its skull.
On the other side, Oldman sidestepped a claw swipe, closing the distance and smashing his gauntleted fist into the creature’s face. Before it could recover, he followed with a brutal right hook and a sharp kick to its torso. Then, with a flick of his wrist, the Stickk relic extended, and he brought it down like a hammer onto the Apelang’s head, knocking it unconscious. He retracted the relic and pivoted to face another attacker.
At the front, Sergeant Thompson cut down Apelang with precise strikes from his summoned spear, while guiding spinning star-shaped water blades that sliced the wildborn into pieces. Beside him, the short but fierce Donny fought with a short sword in one hand while exhaling bursts of fire from his mouth, engulfing any Apelang that dared to come near.
Suddenly, a massive branch hurtled through the air toward Gian and Kel. They dove to opposite sides, avoiding the impact. Looking deeper into the forest beyond the clearing, they spotted more Apelang positioned on thicker rear branches, hurling rocks and broken limbs toward the group below.
“Jones! Jessica! Take out those ranged pests!” Thompson barked.
“Yes, sir!” the two echoed. Jones charged ahead, his Echo of Beast cloaking his skin in a thick, fur-like armor for protection. Behind him, Jessica activated her Echo of Bloom and spread her hands as thorny roots sprouted across the forest floor, slowing the advancing Apelang on their flank.
“We need to help them!” Kel shouted.
“Go—I’ll cover you!” Gian replied, sheathing his short sword and quickly pulling his bow from his back.
“On it!” Kel yelled, sprinting to join Jones and Jessica.
“Oldman, cover me!” Gian called as he drew an arrow. He released it in one smooth motion, striking an Apelang through the head just before it could reach Kel.
Oldman moved closer, smashing another Apelang aside with a swing of the Stickk relic, keeping the pressure off Gian as he readied another shot.
More arrows whistled through the trees as Baron kept firing his translucent bow with deadly precision, each shot striking an Apelang in the eyes, mouth, or neck.
One of the Apelang roared, lifting a boulder nearly as large as itself before hurling it toward Jones and Jessica. Jones stepped forward, crossing his massive arms just in time.
“Ughhh!” The impact forced him back several steps, his boots digging furrows into the forest floor, yet he stayed standing.
Jessica seized the moment. Vines burst from the ground beneath the Apelang, thorn-covered roots snaking silently up its limbs. Before the creature realized it was trapped, the roots pierced through its torso and legs. It screamed, clawing desperately at the vines as blood trickled down its fur. Jessica clenched her fist, and the thorns twisted deeper, finally ending its life.
She shifted her focus, blooming another thorny root that snaked up and entangled the feet of an approaching Apelang. Meanwhile, Jones ignored the ache in his bruised arms and charged another, driving his fist into its chest with enough force to hurl it crashing into a tree.
Kel finally caught up, his Crimson Blood Axe gleaming in the dappled light. Just as another Apelang’s whip-like tail was about to strike Jessica, he swung cleanly, severing it midair.
Arrows rained again as Gian and Baron loosed volleys from behind, picking off the Apelang that lingered in the distance. With their coordinated attacks, the soldiers pushed through.

It took a few more minutes before the last of the shrieking Apelang collapsed among the roots and fallen leaves scattered across the clearing.
Kel yanked his thrown axe from a corpse and wiped the sweat from his brow. “Phew… thought they’d never stop coming.”
“Thanks for the backup,” Jessica said, sitting down on the ground, breathing heavily.
“No problem. The Great Kel is always here for you.” He grinned with exaggerated bravado.
“Damn monkeys,” Jones muttered, flexing his left wrist and rotating it to loosen the ache.
“Are you hurt?” Jessica asked, glancing at him.
“Nah, just a scratch. Let’s head back,” Jones replied, already turning to regroup with the others.
Thompson narrowed his gaze. “Baron, Jimmy, sweep the perimeter. I want eyes on everything.” 
“Yes, sir,” both scouts replied, quickly moving off to scan the forest.
Thompson turned to the short soldier beside him. “Report, Donny?”
Glancing around at the aftermath, Donny answered, “No fatalities, sir. Just a few scrapes and bruises. Jones and the others are already heading back.”
“Good. We’ll rest here for a bit until Baron and Jimmy return. Then we move,” Thompson said.
Oldman adjusted his gambler’s hat with a small sigh. “How are you holding up, Gian?” he asked as Tata finally crept out from hiding, sniffed one of the fallen creatures, squeaked in disgust, and leapt back onto Oldman’s shoulder. 
“I’m fine,” Gian replied. “These wildborn aren’t that strong… just overwhelming in numbers.”
“Yeah,” Oldman said, fanning himself with his hat. “Let’s just hope the rest we meet in here are feral types like these, not ascendants.”
Gian didn’t reply. He scanned the surrounding trees and undergrowth, now quiet except for the faint rustle of leaves overhead and the distant creak of branches shifting in the breeze.
