Chapter 8: What Cannot Be Held	
Smoke curled and roiled, dense and choking, blinding most—but within the storm, two figures moved with terrifying precision.
Elder Eldon struck first.
His cane sword danced, slicing through the air in wide arcs and tight jabs. Smoke trailed from each motion, guided with purpose, swirling around the masked swordsman’s limbs, clouding vision and choking space. Eldon’s blade flicked up, caught the edge of his opponent’s robe, then twisted, redirecting a counter that would’ve split another man in half.
The masked man snarled, his flaming sword slashing through the haze in furious sweeps. Every blow was brutal, direct, designed to overwhelm rather than finesse. Fire surged from the blade in bursts, sheets of flame that painted the smoke red and gold.
Eldon ducked under one of the sweeping flames, rolled to the side, and launched a counter from behind the cover of smoke. His blade grazed the masked man’s ribs, leaving only a shallow scratch. Ice exploded from the enemy's off-hand just in time, forming ice darts that shot backward like spikes. Eldon blocked one with the flat of his sword and winced as another sliced across his thigh.
Behind them, the patrolwoman squinted into the churning gray, her spear gripped tightly. She spotted a sudden flare—flames rushing toward Eldon.
“Ice wall!” she shouted, slamming her spear into the dirt.
A crystalline wall erupted in a flash. It caught the inferno mid-flight. The flames hissed, steamed, cracked, but the wall held.
Eldon seized the moment, pivoting and vanishing into the folds of smoke once more. From within came a dozen shallow strikes, probing, testing. The masked man blocked most, but his stance wavered.
“You fight like a shadow,” the masked man growled, backpedaling slightly. “But shadows burn.”
He lifted his sword overhead. Fire poured down in a sweeping arc. Smoke erupted in bursts as pockets of flame tore through the haze. For a moment, the battlefield blazed like a sunburst.
The patrolwoman gritted her teeth and thrust her spear forward again. Ice erupted in a wide curtain, shielding Eldon and pushing the masked man back.
Bleeding and gasping, Eldon charged through the steam and smoke, his sword flashing like lightning through stormclouds. Their blades collided. Sparks danced. Steel shrieked.
The masked man’s flaming weapon radiated searing heat, while Eldon’s smoke slithered around him—distracting, veiling, misleading. The two clashed like fire and ash.
This isn’t working… we can’t beat him.
The grim truth settled in Elder Eldon’s mind like a blade to the heart. He gritted his teeth as he parried another flaming slash, the force rattling through his arms.
“Fall back!” he shouted to the patrolwoman. “Warn everyone! Tell them to flee while they still can!”
She hesitated, eyes wide with fear and exhaustion, her breaths ragged from overusing her Echo. “But what about you?”
Another wave of fire surged. Eldon raised his sword just in time, but a tendril of flame scorched across his side. He stumbled, a burn smoldering beneath his robes. 
“Just go!” he roared, voice cracking from pain and rage. “I’ll hold him off!”
She stared at him for a heartbeat, torn, then nodded and backed away into the haze. Her figure vanished, swallowed by smoke and fire.
Eldon turned back to face the masked man, breath shallow, body battered, but his grip remained firm. 
“You’ll have to go through me.” he muttered, raising his sword once more.
The masked man chuckled, a low, distorted sound behind the jagged mask.
"Such a useless struggle," he sneered, eyes glinting with cruel amusement.
"But I’m enjoying this. The fear. The screams. Watching hope die in their eyes—one by one. I’ll massacre every last one of them... slowly."
His voice dripped with sadistic pleasure, not just a killer but a monster who thrived on the suffering he caused. Each swing of his flaming sword wasn't just an attack—it was a performance, and the battlefield was his stage.

Not far from the battle, inside the partially collapsed house, the townspeople huddled in tense silence, watching the smoke and flashes of fire through shattered beams, each one silently hoping the fighters would win and they survive.
Suddenly, the patrolwoman appeared, sprinting toward them through the haze.
“Run! Scatter in every direction!” she shouted, her voice cracking with urgency.
“What did she say?” an elderly woman asked, confused.
Then, as if jolted awake, an injured father grabbed his wife and child, dragging them into the street. That single act triggered a wave—panic rippled through the group. People began fleeing in all directions, hoping the killer would chase someone else instead of them.
“Gian, we have to go!” Helen said, pulling at her son’s arm, desperation in her eyes.
“We can’t just leave Elder Eldon behind! We should fight—stand with him!” Gian protested, resisting.
A sudden slap struck Gian.
“Don’t you understand?” Helen snapped. “He’s doing this for us—he’s giving us a chance. Now run!”
She dragged him toward the door. Outside, chaos reigned. Screams echoed. Fire bloomed in the smoke.
Suddenly—a shriek pierced the air.
People collapsed. One after another. Blood pooled beneath unmoving bodies.
A new silhouette emerged cloaked in dark violet.
The survivors froze.
Another masked figure.
Fear gripped everyone. No one understood how the people had died.
Then came the voice—cold, sharp, female.
“What are you doing, No. 7? We’re supposed to regroup at the plaza. There’s already been enough destruction.”
No. 7 stopped mid-swing. Eldon took the chance to retreat a few steps, chest heaving, sword trembling in his grip.
“Shut up, No. 8,” the man snapped. “I don’t take orders from you. My mission’s complete. Now I’m having fun with this old Echo Master.”
“Why was I partnered with this? This is why I hate brats,” No. 8 muttered with disdain.
One civilian shakily picked up a sword, readying himself against the new masked figure. A few more followed his lead.
“I won’t do that, darling. It serves you better to just run—maybe some of you will make it,” No. 8 laughed.
She raised her hand sharply toward another fleeing group of civilians. In an instant, more bodies dropped, and panic erupted again as people scattered in different directions.
What kind of Echo is this? Elder Eldon thought, his mind racing. We might not make it out of this alive.
Then, the masked woman flicked her hand toward the direction of Helen and Gian.
Helen saw it—a flick of the wrist—and instinctively threw herself in front of her son, shielding him with her body.
A sickening force struck them. Helen and those nearby dropped instantly.
“No!” Gian cried, catching her before she hit the ground. He cradled her, trembling, and turned her over—only to see a knife had pierced her chest, buried deep. Blood spread across her clothes.
“MOTHER!!” Gian screamed, rage and guilt ripping through him.
The masked woman approached at a slow, cruel pace.
“Well, what do we have here,” she said, voice laced with mockery. “A mother’s love… How sweet.”
She paused, eyes gleaming behind the mask. “But how stupid. If you want to protect someone… be strong enough to do it.”
She raised her hand again—this time at Gian.
“Gian!” Eldon shouted, surging forward but No. 7 blocked him, flaming sword lashing out.
“Where do you think you’re going?” No.7 sneered, laughing maniacally. “We’re not done!”
Their blades clashed again. But this time Eldon didn’t fight just to survive.
This time, his fury burned hotter than any flame.
